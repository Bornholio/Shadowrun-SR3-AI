## Nährboden Haus — Matrix Den
*Back alley off 3rd Street · converted loading dock bay · two alley exits*

Illegal high-speed Matrix den. Twelve floor stations (partitioned cubicles, jack cluster, personal locker). Four private back rooms — full walls, lockable, wired for decking, 3× floor rate. Cash or credstick, no SIN, no logging. Three independent fiber trunks (stolen corporate tap). Roof antenna array boosts commlinks 200m radius — she charges separately.

**Nährboden** — operator, took location's name or gave it hers. Human female, mid-30s. Gray utility pants, datajack left temple, short hair. Transactional, not cold. Notices everything. Stubby shotgun behind counter, never had to use it in front of anyone. `B4 Q4 S3 C4 I7 W5 | Init 11+1d6` · Computer 8, Electronics 7, Decking 6, Etiquette(Street) 5, Perception 7, Negotiation 5

**Splice** — floor associate, human male, early 20s. Handles logistics, defers to Nährboden. Moves carefully — has been hit before.

**GM hooks:**
- Antenna array passive log contains burst transmissions matching RAVN parameters (last 48hrs)
- Regular client missing four days — she's noticed, hasn't asked
- Back room: chip still in slot from a session three weeks ago that terminated abruptly. Client never returned.
- Singer reads the infrastructure in ninety seconds. Muscle memory precedes context.

## Uwajimaya
*Belltown adjacent · ground floor + basement cold storage*

Pan-Asian grocery, third-generation Moriguchi family ownership. Survived the Awakening, food riots, two acquisition attempts, the Night of Rage — too essential to too many communities to touch safely. Original neon signage, patched twice. SIN-optional checkout lanes in back. Deli counter runs to 0200. Small awakened produce corner carries Orbital Organic product. Troll woman at the cash register since 2041. Does not ask questions.

---

## Orbital Organic
*Supplier label — not a storefront · hydroponic collective*

Low-orbit agricultural platforms plus two Cascades ground stations. Vacuum-sealed, certified awakened-free growth medium — no astral contamination. That's the selling point. Available at Uwajimaya and four other Seattle independents. Green-and-silver packaging. Slightly expensive.

*The "orbital" branding is mostly honest. One of three platforms burned up in 2054 — ground station output quietly rebranded. Certification not pulled. GM only.*

**Hook:** Orbital cargo manifests pass through port authority. Singer could read them in her sleep.

---

## Red Lake Nation Foods
*Ojibwe cooperative · Red Lake Band, Minnesota · Treaty trade goods*

Wild rice, maple products, dried fish, preserved game. Treaty-certified — crosses NAN/UCAS borders with minimal inspection. Containers sealed at origin, waved through at the border. The cooperative's leadership is aware someone figured out the shipping window fifteen years ago and takes a cut. Funds the school in Redby.

At Uwajimaya: small dedicated shelf. Wild rice sells out. Maple syrup distinguished from the cut competitor product by the band seal pressed into the lid.

**Hook:** Crowbar picks up the package and something moves in him before he knows why. He's had this before. Doesn't remember where.

## Trinity — Nightclub
*Three floors, one address, one door staff*

Declare your floor at entrance. Stairs are watched.

- **Ground:** Industrial. Loud enough to think in — deals happen here.
- **Second:** Ambient/trance. Mages come here. Three shamans independently noted the astral texture as unusual — none told each other.
- **Third:** Members only. Criteria unknown.

License held by a shell with a Tir Tairngire forwarding address.

## Ohm Nightclub

Electricity as metaphor, load-bearing. Music as current, body as circuit. Crowd skews heavily augmented — chrome visible and celebrated. Light rigs designed to interact with optical cyberware. Unaugmented: disorienting. Augmented: no words for it. House decker runs the light system live from a booth above the floor. No weapons scan. No weapons scan policy.

## Italo Macinare
*"Italian Grind" · coffee bar until 2200, nightclub after*

Transition unanounced — espresso machine moves, lights drop, whoever's still there self-selected. Music: pre-Awakening Italian electronic, Moroder-adjacent, some actual vinyl. Forty people is a full house. New faces evaluated before welcomed.

**Enzo** — owner, human, late 60s, Seattle since 2031. Left Milan for reasons he describes differently every time. Speaks four languages. Meridian walks in — within twenty minutes someone's speaking French to her because they asked.

**Back table:** Same three people every Thursday. Nobody sits there other nights.

**Hook:** Enzo knows Pentarus's name. Hasn't said it to anyone in eight months.

## Cascade Fold

Sound design venue pretending to be a nightclub. Music composed live, generative — responds to crowd density and movement through a sensor floor. No two nights identical. No recordings by policy; owner enforces with a signal jammer worth more than the sound system. Crowd skews academic and awakened. Bar serves nothing carbonated — no explanation.

On three documented occasions the generative system produced sounds shamans described as *structurally similar to a summoning*. Owner's response: *"Good."*

**Hook:** When the floor hits a particular resonance, the building's Hearth Spirit moves to the center of the room and stays until the set ends. Only the awakened can see it. It looks pleased.

---

## Vetro Nero
*"Black Glass" · ground floor of a building that should have been condemned in 2041*

Small. Dark. Bar top is actual black glass — salvage, origin unknown, faintly warm regardless of ambient temperature. Music curated, not live. Deep cuts implying the selector was alive long enough to have been there.

**Sable** — bartender, elf, gender presentation variable night to night, has worked the bar since it opened and may have opened it. Doesn't confirm either.

Clientele arrives late, leaves later. Mix of metatypes. Regulars have the look of people who have done significant things and stopped talking about them.

**Hook:** Matchbooks on the bar have a commcode written inside the back cover. Changes monthly. Nobody has explained what it connects to.
