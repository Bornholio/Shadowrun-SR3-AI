# DOUBLE TAKE
*An adventure from Corporate Punishment (SR3 #7330)*

---

## INTRODUCTION

*Corporate Punishment* offers three different Shadowrun roleplaying adventures linked by a single theme: the corporations and what they are willing to do in the cause of one-upmanship. The stakes are high and the corps know that even one slip-up can damage their image and their relationships with the other movers and shakers. From petty theft to wetwork, the corps know the shadows are the place to get the professional talent they need to stay one step ahead of (or catch up to) the competition.

### General Rules

Use standard SR3 rules throughout unless otherwise noted. All cyberware is alphaware unless otherwise noted. NPC stats are intended for moderately experienced players and gamemasters but can be adjusted as needed.

### Non-Player Characters

Knowledge skills are listed to add depth to characters. Gamemasters can add more for a wider range of interests or to serve a specific purpose. Stats can be toned down or ramped up as needed.

## BACKGROUND STORY

*Double Take* places the shadowrunners as pawns in a strategic contest between two vastly powerful enemies. Both opponents are members of Tir Tairngire's ruling Council of Princes: the dragon Lofwyr and the elf Aithne Oakforest. The pieces used in this round are corporations — specifically Saeder-Krupp and Telestrian Industries.

Oakforest, through his agent Count Evan Parris (aka Blackwing), instructs a company woman at Telestrian Industries to hire a team of runners from outside the country. Oakforest has strong connections with the Telestrian family and owns a significant percentage of stock.

The runners are hired to penetrate a Universal Omnitech subsidiary facility in Portland. The site houses a research lab that recently made a significant breakthrough in gene-mapping — concerning the genes allegedly responsible for magical ability (the so-called "magus factor"). This breakthrough may be the first step toward claiming the ten million nuyen offered in Dunkelzahn's will for explaining the basis of magical ability. The runners are told to steal the project data and wreck the laboratory. The data will go to Telestrian Industries for its own genetic research programs. Information linking the runners to the raid will be leaked to the Tir media, setting them up to take the fall.

To make matters worse, the runners are to be framed as Saeder-Krupp operatives. They will become fugitives trapped in a foreign land with no easy escape route. Meanwhile, the Tir media will pillory Saeder-Krupp and its head, Prince Lofwyr, at a time when Lofwyr is already under pressure in the Council of Princes. Ideally, the scandal will force his hand in a way beneficial to Oakforest. If things go smoothly, Telestrian will obtain important data from a rival corp, while Oakforest's bitter rival takes the blame and suffers a barrage of criticism, punitive measures and political pressure.

The Telestrian company woman chosen to handle the job is Moire Ferguson, who years ago ran the shadows in Seattle under the name Banshee. Looking for a new life, she was drawn to the Tir by its lofty ideals and commitment to building an elven society. After several years in service to Telestrian and the Tir, she has become disillusioned. Instead of a promised land, she found a society wracked with the same prejudice, elitism and backstabbing she sought to escape.

Count Parris chose her for the operation because higher-ups have noticed her change in attitude. Fearing she may become a security risk, the count has decided to dispose of her rather than risk her going renegade. Having run black operations for the corp for years, Moire knows more than an untrustworthy employee should.

Unknown to the runners, Banshee is having her people create personnel files for the runners and inserting them into an appropriate Saeder-Krupp Matrix host. Conveniently, many of the items recently vanished from a Saeder-Krupp supply depot in Denver. Banshee is also inserting (and backdating) abbreviated files identifying the runners as "known Saeder-Krupp operatives" into the datastores of Tir law enforcement agencies.

## PORTLAND AT A GLANCE

Portland is a buffer zone between Tir Tairngire and the rest of the world. Most imports and exports to and from the Tir run through Portland, and Portland is the only Tir area where visitors and non-citizens are allowed to go. The city is surrounded by a massive ten-meter-high wall; movement to and from the rest of Tir Tairngire goes through heavily fortified checkpoints.

Portland is officially run by a military tribunal under a modified martial law. The local Peace Force is well-armed and armored, and will quickly clamp down on any violent situation. These officers have legal authority to stop and question anyone, as well as demand identification — failing to carry ID is a crime, especially for visitors. Luckily for the runners, the Peace Force is not a pervasive presence except downtown and in upper-class areas; they deliberately ignore several squatter neighborhoods.

Portland is seventy percent elven, and its society adheres to the same rigid caste structure as the rest of the country. Social rank determines access to privileges from housing and schooling to taxation and exemption from military service. Rank is determined at age eighteen through a battery of tests and political maneuvering. Most standard citizens are ranked as gentry (addressed as "Squire"). Further up the ladder are chivalry ("Sir" or "Demoiselle"), then nobles, counts and countesses, dukes and duchesses. At the top are the royalty, including the ruling princes who live just outside Portland in Royal Hill.

Though subtly masked, discrimination against non-elves is common. Visitors like the runners have no social rank and are treated accordingly. Sperethiel is the country's official language but is only common among upper castes.

Saeder-Krupp is specifically banned from Tir Tairngire — Lofwyr, who controls it, is a member of Tir Tairngire's ruling Council of Princes (a perceived "conflict of interest"). Aztechnology is also banned for its symbiotic relations with Aztlan.

### Getting Around Town

Portland's well-maintained road and highway network makes it easy to get around. Most people avoid the primary public transportation system — a shoddy subway with frequent delays and public hazards. At night, the subway turns into a free-for-all. A sizeable web of abandoned subway tunnels hosts a thriving underground culture and offers an excellent way to move around without being traced.

Popular shadow hangouts include: the Mill (a dank hole ideal for weapons deals), the Ivanhoe (a biker bar/motel that serves as Dog's main hangout), and the Edge (a club known for its lack of racial and class bias and its avant-garde underground musicians).

## SCENARIO 1: ALL THAT JAZZ

### What's Up, Chummer?

This scenario puts the runners in contact with a mysterious elven Johnson. It quickly becomes evident she is a pro, possibly even a former runner, though exactly who she is and who she works for remain a secret. Runners who dig into her background may find out she used to run the shadows under the name Banshee.

The runners respond to a cryptic invitation to come to Matchstick's, a classy jazz club with a reputation as a cool, understated hangout for low-key professional shadowrunners. At the club they meet their Johnson, who sketches out a job offer: travel to Portland in Tir Tairngire, hit Universal Omnitech's offices, steal research data about mapping human and metahuman genes to explain magical ability, and sabotage the research lab.

### Tell It to Them Straight

> It's Friday night and you're relaxing in your doss when a priority message comes over your telecom. It's a formal invitation, forwarded by your fixer, to come to Matchstick's tonight to discuss business. The closing line instructs you to "tell Carl that the password is swordfish." Sounds intriguing, so you decide to go see what this is all about.
>
> When you arrive at Matchstick's, you see a small but growing line of people waiting to get inside. Here and there in the line you spot a few chummers you've worked with in the past, or that you recognize from other runner haunts. When you reach the door, a troll bouncer and a snappy human in a zoot suit block your entrance. You tell them you're here to speak to Carl. The human nods and asks for the password. You give the one from the invitation, and he escorts you inside.
>
> The club is smoky and dim, and reminds you of a Prohibition-era speakeasy. Jazz piano tinkles in the background, accompanied by a haunting female voice. You barely have time to take it all in before your escort shows you to a private back room. As the door closes behind you, you spy an elven woman sitting in the corner of the room furthest from the door.
>
> "Thank you for coming," she says, her voice low but crisp. She motions toward the chairs and waits patiently for everyone to sit. She wears snug synthleather pants and matching boots with a sleeveless zippered vest, giving her a menacing street look.
>
> "You may call me Johnson, of course, and please don't waste our time by asking for background details beyond that. I have a lucrative bit of biz you might enjoy; it entails a trip into Tir Tairngire, which I can help to arrange. Your job is to hit a Universal Omnitech facility there and steal some data from their system as well as sabotage a laboratory. The pay is a total of 100,000 nuyen; you decide how to divide it among yourselves. I can give you up to a third up front. Before I go into more detail, I need your answer: yes or no?"

**When the runners agree to take the job, read the following aloud:**

> "Excellent. I can get you into Tir Tairngire with visas and the like, but you'll still need to be careful. Tir Customs can be touchy, so I advise against jokes about the purpose of your visit. You'll be flying into Portland under the guise of legitimate business, so unfortunately most of your gear will have to stay home. I can arrange what you'll need to get the job done: weapons, armor, tech and a cyberdeck.
>
> "You'll be stealing the project logs and related materials pertaining to the mapping of metahuman genes, specifically the data concerning the origins of magical ability. Universal Omnitech's system is difficult to crack, but you should find it relatively easy to access the datastore directly from the lab and download the files with what I give you.
>
> "The best way I know of to gain entrance to their offices is to pose as gardeners from the company they use. The gardeners show up every Saturday afternoon to take care of the gardens that encircle each floor. The floor you'll be pruning is the fifth. Hardly anyone will be there on a weekend, but you might encounter a few employees logging in some overtime. Things could get difficult if you do; I suggest caution. I can give you a map, gardening uniforms and gear, and other essential equipment. They'll be delivered at your hotel rooms once you get to Portland. The run absolutely has to go off next Saturday morning. Once it's over, meet me in Pioneer Courthouse Square in the middle of downtown Portland. I can give you the rest of your pay then."

### Hooks

Play up the mystery and excitement inherent in the shadows. Matchstick's is a jazz club for members only, recalling the style and atmosphere of the 1920s and 30s. Membership is given to noteworthy individuals or to those sponsored by a current member.

The Johnson, Banshee, is a study in contrasts. She seems comfortable in her street-tough look, yet carries herself like someone more at home with the finer things in life. Though company loyalty compels her to do her job efficiently, she has become increasingly reluctant to play shadowrunner employer.

If the idea of going to Tir Tairngire seems to scare the runners off, play up Banshee's calm and confidence that the run is likely to go off without a hitch.

### Behind the Scenes

Carl Steward, the manager of Matchstick's, is an old friend of Banshee's from her shadowrunning days. He's the human in the neo-zoot suit at the door. Matchstick's does not screen its members for weapons. After Steward leaves the group in the back room, he will go to his office and plug into the club's security system through a hidden port under his desk.

Should any magically active player characters wish to astrally scan Banshee, she notices but will not stop them. As a Grade 3 initiate using masking (p. 76, MitS), she is confident of concealing her magical ability and feelings about the run.

**Assensing Banshee Table**

| Successes | Result |
|---|---|
| 0 | Aura? What aura? |
| 1–2 | She's Awakened and obviously an initiate. Her masking is tough to pierce. She's also feeling apprehensive about something. |
| 3–4 | She's been through several ordeals in becoming an initiate. She's definitely feeling uneasy about the run, but the reason isn't clear. |
| 5+ | She's a hermetic mage with a Magic Rating of 9. You feel a strong sense of guilt from her, and it seems like it's connected to the run. You also pick up some slight feelings of … self-loathing? |

Assensing characters will likely notice the room is astrally protected by a Force 5 ward.

The runners will find negotiating for more money difficult but not impossible. Make a Negotiation (6) Test (p. 93, SR3), applying a +2 modifier because Banshee is reluctant to give them more. If successful, she raises payment by 5,000 nuyen per success, up to a maximum of 110,000 nuyen. If the runners accept, Banshee gives them the 30,000 nuyen advance.

Banshee will ask the decker what model cyberdeck they use and which programs they favor. Let the player characters choose what gear they want within reason (nothing higher than Rating 6 or Availability 8; nothing that takes more than a week to obtain). Before she leaves, Banshee gives the runners a Matrix address and asks them to submit passport-quality photos and identifying information so she can arrange for visas.

## SCENARIO 2: STRANGERS BEARING GIFTS

### What's Up, Chummer?

The characters begin their journey to Portland by going through the arduous customs process on both ends of an uneventful flight. With a few possible exceptions, their entrance goes fairly smoothly. The runners then wait for Banshee's people to deliver their promised goods — and the wait gets long. The delivery men arrive early Friday night with the team's gear and offer their services as local "independent operatives."

### Tell It to Them Straight

*(Airport customs — read aloud)*

> Airports are always boiling over with cranky people hurrying to get somewhere other than where they're at, and now you're one of them. You work your way through the noisy crowds to the Cinanestial ticket counter, where a male elf in a dark green, silver and white uniform awaits. "I need your passport, a copy of your medical records including up-to-date vaccinations, your Visitor's Authorization Visa and your round-trip ticket to and from Tir Tairngire, please."
>
> "You will be asked to present these documents once more, and also to submit to a blood test by the Customs and Immigration officers, when you arrive. Should you fail to comply, you will be escorted out of the country. Any cyberimplants, with the exception of limbs needed for mobility or other life-sustaining devices, will be deactivated. Illegal reactivation carries a heavy penalty and also warrants immediate deportation."
**Translated** “Co cirolle, verespitosa se [documents] od verespitosa samriel ti’morel co faskriethsa ti gorofaskit. Pechet liayait, ozidanit con goro pechet. [Cyberimplants]-sa, pechet limbs co morel od teheron, telegasta carronasto-ya. Nagee veresp ti carronasto-ya perestat samriel saraégh od ozidan con goro pechet.”

*(Arriving in Portland)*

> The flight from Seattle to Morningstar Field in Portland is short and bumpy. As you wait your turn in line to see the Customs and Immigration officers, you notice quite a few deadly-looking elves with equally deadly-looking weapons close at hand. Finally standing in front of the customs officer, he makes no pretense at politeness. "State the purpose of your visit," he says curtly, gesturing brusquely for your documents while readying the equipment for the blood test.
>
> "Lucky you, you get to keep your cyberware," he mutters, reading something apparently noted in your VAV. Looks like you've got a friend in high places — or at least a friend with contacts in high places.
**Translated** “Verespit mes ti’cirolle, Se’seterin; perestit se [cyberware].”

*(Arriving at the hotel)*

> By the time you finally leave the airport, the sun is setting. Orange and red light gleams across downtown Portland. Your reservations are at the Rose Branch Inn, smack in the middle of downtown. You're a little uncomfortable at being in the heart of "elf territory," especially as the whole country runs on a weird caste system. Check-in goes quickly, and soon you're all left alone in your rooms. You've got a day and a half before the run; all you have to do is wait for Ms. Johnson's delivery to arrive.

*(When the runners' gear arrives)*

> It's Friday night, the run's tomorrow and your gear still hasn't shown up. You're about ready to go nuts when you hear a knock at the door. Two orks, male and average-looking for their metatype, stroll into the room and throw a suitcase on the bed.
>
> "Merry Christmas," the scruffier one says, with a smirking salute. "Miz Johnson sends her regards, and sez you'll find everything you need in the van, currently charging in Lot C–13." He rattles off the van's doorlock code, then leans forward and whispers, "Off the record, if there's anything else you're looking for while you're visiting our wonderful metropolis, drop me and Zach here a line. We're experienced with Portland's ins and outs, if you know what I mean." He passes you a business card that reads "Eddy and Zach, specialists" with a telecom code. Then he winks and the pair heads for the door.

### Hooks

Flying to Tir Tairngire and wrangling with Customs should have the runners ready to tear their hair out with red tape and bureaucracy. Trying to deviate from it could have severe consequences — at best, a misstep bars the player characters from entering the country; at worst, it could get them killed, then deported. If necessary, subtly remind the players of this through a vivid example: someone in front of them caught trying to give blood that isn't their own, or a commotion when a shaman tries to use magic to slip past unnoticed.

Whether or not the characters venture out much, find ways to emphasize the alien nature of Tir Tairngire society. As representatives of the social hierarchy's lowest strata, the characters are likely to be on the receiving end of xenophobia and class snobbery.

### Behind the Scenes

The planning surrounding this run is solid. Banshee's connections with Telestrian Industries and the Tir government have allowed for considerable string-pulling. Cyberware, magical ability and other oddities that might attract attention will be conveniently overlooked. However, this safety net does not rule out problems created by the characters' own behavior.

If the characters attempt to smuggle anything in, customs people will likely find it. Make a test for weapon or cyberware detection using six dice per the rules on p. 237, SR3. If contraband is discovered, the item will be seized and the character heavily fined. If military grade, the character will be jailed — end of adventure.

The characters have the next five days to prepare for the run. Decker characters may attempt to track down the Universal Omnitech system in Portland; after several days of digging, they'll find that the datastore Banshee mentioned is not connected to the Matrix and is only accessible from inside the Portland facility.

On Tuesday, a courier delivers a package with Visitor's Authorization Visas, plane tickets and hotel reservations.

### The Delivery

Also included is a mapchip and printouts providing a basic layout of the Universal Omnitech facility (give this to the players as a handout — see the Fifth Floor Map on p. 21 of the source book, noting that Banshee's version of the map is dated and several rooms have been switched around).

The gamemaster chooses the deck and programs. A CMT Avatar or Renraku Kraftwerk are good choices (stats on p. 207, SR3). The cyberdeck has seven to ten programs loaded with individual ratings not exceeding the deck's MPCP rating. The deck also comes equipped with a storage memory unit large enough to handle the Universal Omnitech data download.

In the parking lot, the runners find an electric Leyland-Rover Transport van painted green with a "Green Sleeves, Inc." logo.

### The Delivery Boys

Eddy and Zach are inseparable brothers who freelance in the Portland shadows. Banshee hired them through a troll crime boss named Dog; they have no idea who they're working for or what the runners have been hired to do. Eddy does most of the talking; Zach is unnaturally quiet but always first to dive into a task. Both will viciously attack anyone who targets the other.

Eddy is serious when he offers to help the runners. If the characters want extra gear or info, the orks will use their contacts to track it down and act as middlemen, charging an extra twenty percent for profit. They'll also offer their services as tour guides. If the characters play their cards right, Eddy and Zach can lead them toward useful local contacts.

If the runners attempt to hire the orks as backup on the run, Eddy and Zach politely refuse to participate directly but may be willing to take on peripheral tasks (scouting, spotting, creating a diversion).

## SCENARIO 3: FLOWER AND FIRE

### What's Up, Chummer?

This is it — the run. The runners gather their gear and go to Universal Omnitech's subsidiary offices. Posing as the Green Sleeves gardening crew, they pass the gate and enter the building. Because Green Sleeves personnel come every Saturday morning, the security guards do a cursory job of checking the runners out.

Once the runners reach the fifth floor, they can locate a work terminal in a nearby open cubicle for the decker to jack in. Meanwhile, the rest of the team can wreck the Research and Development laboratory. The cakewalk turns nasty, however, when Universal Omnitech security twigs to the runners' presence.

### Tell It to Them Straight

> Saturday mornings used to mean eating a bowl of SweetiPopz in front of the trideo and watching your favorite cartoons. Now you're doing the kinds of things you used to watch. Around you, your team is loading weapons, preparing gear and suiting up as a gardening crew.

*(When the runners arrive at Universal Omnitech)*

> With your Green Sleeves uniforms over your usual togs and carrying bags filled with "gardening tools," you look like one tough gardening crew. You steer the van up to the gatehouse and roll down the window. A mirrored window on the gatehouse slides open. The bored-looking guard manages to pull his eyes away from the replay of last week's hurling match long enough to glance at a clipboard.
>
> "What floor you on today?"
>
> "Fifth," you answer calmly.
>
> "Use Dock Two in back," he says, then waves you on. As he slides his window shut, the gate bar rises. You drive around back and into Dock Two's open parking bay. You unload your gardening tools in the spacious service elevator, mindful of the watching vidcams. You reach the fifth floor less than a minute later. Exiting the elevator, you look to either side. You see a long corridor with one glass wall. On the other side, lush gardens grow on the "roof." The place looks like a giant's terrarium. A quick map check tells you where the lab should be. Now to find a relatively safe terminal to jack into …

*(If the team triggers an alarm)*

> You're almost finished, happily downloading sensitive data and running amok in a corporate R&D lab, when you hear the ding of opening elevator doors. Busted! Looks like it's time to get out of Dodge.

### Hooks

Evoke nervous excitement — butterflies in the stomach, skin all prickly, clammy palms. The ease of penetrating the facility may make the runners cocky, leading them to accidentally trigger an alarm. Play up the tension; sneaking around a deserted office building should be spooky. Feel free to throw a few small surprises at the characters: a noisy screen saver on someone's work terminal, or a random suit working overtime suddenly walking around the corner.

Though they do not realize it, the characters have an inaccurate map, which makes confusion another running theme.

### Behind the Scenes

The Universal Omnitech facility lies just north of downtown Portland in the Faloma district. The facility is one of three stand-alone office buildings surrounded by parking lots. A sculpted hedge surrounds the entire site, concealing a small plascrete barrier to prevent unauthorized vehicle access (Barrier Rating 12). Lampposts spaced every ten meters around the perimeter also sport video cameras linked to the main security office.

The main building is six stories tall and tiered — every two floors, the building gets smaller, like a stepped pyramid. The roofs outside floors Three and Five hold large outdoor gardens. The building has four ground-floor exits and entrances; two elevators, two stairwells and a service elevator allow movement between floors.

The runners should have no problem getting to the site or past the gatehouse guard. The guard will inform the security office of the gardeners' arrival and the floor where they will be working. The security office will open the door to Dock Two so the gardeners can park, and will forget to close the door later.

### Active Security

The security cameras, elevators, outside doors and other security measures are all rigged, but the facility employs an on-call security rigger whose station is currently unmanned. The building system's Security Value is Vigilance Code Green, Technical Rating 7 (see p. 78, *Rigger 2*). The team's rigger can use a dataline tap or remote control deck to access this system from several areas. He or she must then defeat its encryption and emulate its protocols (see p. 80, *Rigger 2*).

A squad of security guards is permanently stationed in the building, usually in the basement-level security office. At least one guard keeps a casual eye on the camera monitors. Saturdays are typically slow, so the guard will be bored and easily distracted; apply a +2 modifier to all Perception Tests to see if he spots anything unusual. Two other guards patrol the building, strolling through the main hallways on each floor.

A single Force 6 bound water elemental also patrols the building, with orders to report any signs of magic use to the security mage and to attack and disable astrally active intruders. The base target number for the elemental to notice an astrally active character is 10 (see astral patrolling rules, p. 80, MitS), plus the modifiers below.

### Fifth Floor Map Key

The players are given the map as a handout from Banshee, but should not receive the full key. Banshee's version of the map is dated and several rooms have been switched around. Give the players a partly incorrect map key, mixing up rooms as you see fit except for the laboratory and elevator locations.

**Area 9:** Assistant lab director's office. Also a Rating 4 maglock. Contains a coffeemaker and a wider variety of knickknacks.

**Area 10:** Emergency stairwells. Can be monitored and controlled via the rigged security system. Rating 5 maglocks, Barrier Rating 8. Security cameras monitor the stairwells, one between each floor.

**Area 11:** Closets holding gardening supplies.

**Area 12:** Cubicles belonging to the floor secretaries. Equipped to handle telecom calls and conferences.

**Area 13:** The research and development lab. Rating 4 maglock (passcard and thumbprint). Any employees present have the passcards necessary to open the door (Barrier Rating 8). **Area 14:** Unlabeled room housing the hardware for the lab's off-Matrix computer host. If the characters get into this room and make a successful Computer or Electronics (6) Test, they will recognize the equipment and identify the module that serves as the datastore — they can unhook and carry away the module, saving the decker time. Rating 5 maglock (passcard and thumbprint required); door can be monitored and controlled by the security rigger (Barrier Rating 8).

### Decking Universal Omnitech

### Behind the Scenes

At the beginning of this scenario, Banshee does not know that her employer has it in for her, and so is not expecting an attack. She is wary of the runners, and intends to brush them off quickly and move on. When the attack begins, she is initially confused but quickly recovers. As soon as she gets a glimpse of the Ghosts, she puts two and two together and realizes she's been sold out herself. Having nothing to lose, she will then join forces with the runners.

Immediately after reading Tell It to Them Straight, have the runners nearest Banshee make a Reaction (4) Test. Let any character who achieves at least 4 successes take a single Simple Action, such as tackling Banshee or pushing her away from the shot. Then move immediately to Initiative and combat. If the runners leave Banshee to take the bullet aimed at her forehead, have the troll bouncer step in front of her as the shot is fired.

Only four Ghosts will be watching Banshee at the Ivanhoe. At least two of the Ghosts (including the team mage) will stay behind cover, providing covering fire, spell defense and ranged magical attacks. The others use their ruthenium-coated armor to stealthily move into melee combat with the runners.

Various bikers and bar patrons will initially dive for cover, then the Ghosts will drop concussion and smoke grenades and flash-paks in the midst of these groups to keep them disoriented. The bikers are a tough and well-armed crew, however, and will attack any Ghosts they see.

### Making Amends

If the runners and Banshee survive the attack, they will have more than a few things to talk about. Banshee will explain to the runners exactly what occurred behind the scenes on this run. She has outlived her usefulness to Telestrian, and is as much a target as the runners are. With this in mind, she has no problem explaining to them exactly what occurred.

Banshee will apologize for framing the runners and make clear her distaste for such actions. She will suggest that she and the runners part ways. If the runners point out that she owes them one for saving her hide, she'll agree to arrange for Dog's people to smuggle the runners out of the country.

### Where to Turn?

The runners may realize that even if they escape the Tir, the repercussions will follow. Their best bet is to resolve the situation while they still have that option. If they get proof that Telestrian was involved, they can hand it over to Saeder-Krupp or Universal Omnitech in exchange for getting out of the country and/or dropping the charges. If the runners captured any of the Ghosts, a thorough mind-probing might provide the necessary evidence. The most valuable proof would be data from inside Telestrian — specifically, Banshee's files regarding this operation, which she kept in off-line storage in her office. The stolen Omnitech data is also in Telestrian's hands.

### Visiting Telestrian

Banshee is initially reluctant to work with the runners, but if pressed, will quickly see the merit in their plan. She will point out that if they act fast, her access codes may still work. If the runners and Banshee make it to the Habitat within an hour or two of the Ghost ambush, Banshee will have no problem smuggling the runners inside.

Getting Banshee's personal files is as easy as walking into her office and grabbing a pocket computer. Getting the data storage unit from the tech lab is more complex — the clerk will discover that Banshee is not authorized to check it out. At this point, the runners must improvise.

## PICKING UP THE PIECES

### It's a Wrap

*Double Take* is open-ended, its resolution depending entirely on what the gamemaster throws at the runners and who they ultimately side with.

**If Saeder-Krupp Gets the Runners:** Scale's primary task is to learn from the runners who is responsible for the frame-up. His secondary job is to gather enough evidence to absolve Saeder-Krupp of any connection to the run and/or place the blame on Telestrian Industries. Scale eventually releases the runners on the condition that they bring him Banshee, the data and proof of Telestrian's plot. This situation opens up several possible scenarios in which the runners are coerced into working on Lofwyr's behalf. Once in the dragon's clutches, the runners should find it difficult to free themselves.

**If the Ghosts Get the Runners:** Under orders from Telestrian Industries, the Ghosts will kill the runners if they get the chance. Alternatively, the Ghosts may secretly have orders from another Tir Prince who is using them to subvert Aithne Oakforest's plans.

**If Universal Omnitech or the Tir Peace Force Gets the Runners:** All Omnitech wants is to get the stolen data back and hold someone responsible for the wrecked lab. The runners mean nothing to the corp, which will hand them over to the Tir authorities at the first opportunity.

**If the Runners Stay Independent:** If the runners get ahold of Banshee's operation files, they can theoretically use them to blackmail Telestrian. Telestrian can retract its media story, clearing the runners as victims of mistaken identity. If the runners retrieve the stolen data, quite a few corps will pay top cred for it.

### Scale and the Saeder-Krupp Agents

Scale is a mysterious entity, able to appear in a wide range of guises. Many believe he is an ally spirit, while others claim he is a lesser dragon. Throughout *Double Take*, Scale appears as a slick-looking, raven-haired elf male, with dark gray eyes and sharply defined features, wearing an exquisitely tailored charcoal-gray suit. If astrally perceived, Scale appears to be a mundane elf; the characters will not be able to pierce his masking.

Scale takes his orders directly from Lofwyr, and should be feared for that reason alone. He presents his demands as obvious and utterly reasonable. The runners should tread carefully when dealing with him, as he excels at manipulation and wordplay. Anyone attempting to trap Scale with words will instead be trapped in turn.

Scale has whatever skills, statistics and abilities the gamemaster wishes to give him. He will avoid a fight, but if forced into one, will display abilities superior to those of a high-grade, initiated adept. Consider him an Ultimate NPC.

Scale's team should include at least one initiated hermetic mage and two top-of-the-line samurai. The gamemaster chooses their statistics based on these guidelines, treating them as Superior to the characters.
