# Blood in the Boardroom — Track 3: Cross Purposes (Ares/CATCo)

## What's Happening and Why

Ares Macrotechnology is the largest corporation in the UCAS and the dominant North American megacorp. For over twenty years, **Damien Knight** has run it with ruthless efficiency, fending off every challenge to his position — including a decades-long war with **Leonard Aurelius**, whose father built Ares and who has never accepted Knight's takeover.

Two things happened to shake Knight's control:

1. Dunkelzahn's death ended Knight's two-year proxy over the Gavilan Ventures Ares stock (held by Nadja Daviar). When the proxy expired in August 2059, Daviar regained control of 12% of Ares. She gave those votes to neither Knight nor Aurelius.

2. Dunkelzahn's will also placed **Arthur Vogel** — eco-activist lawyer and former presidential candidate — on the Ares board with a Dunkelzahn stock bequest. Vogel was one of the last people Knight would choose as a board member.

These shifts were enough to give Aurelius one last shot. He sold his entire Ares stake privately to Vogel, then jumped ship to **Cross Applied Technologies** — a Quebec-based megacorp founded by **Dr. Lucien Cross**, who has hated Knight since 2053 and has been building a shadow war against Ares ever since. Aurelius brought money, insider knowledge, and the motivation of a man who's spent his life in his father's shadow and finally decided to live his own life.

With Aurelius's money and Seraphim-gathered blackmail material, CATCo recently achieved AAA megacorp status and got a seat on the Corporate Court. Now they're a genuine threat to Ares, and Knight is scrambling.

Meanwhile, Vogel holds more Ares stock than Knight but hasn't made a move. Whether he's being cautious or has sold out is the subject of constant speculation.

---

## The Ares-CATCo War For Runners

This is the most active ongoing conflict for shadowrunners because both Ares and Cross Applied Technologies operate in Seattle and have been running shadow operations against each other for years. The corps are roughly matched in operational capability (Ares vastly larger, CATCo's Seraphim vastly more skilled per head). The arrival of Aurelius escalates everything.

Runners can work for either side. Working for both, over time, creates problems — both corps are good at tracking who they've used and comparing notes. The Seraphim in particular have excellent intelligence and will eventually identify runners who've been playing both ends.

## Ares History That Matters

- **Nicholas Aurelius** founded Ares Industries in 2002, turned it into a megacorp
- **Damien Knight** appeared in 2033 via the Nanosecond Buyout — 63 seconds of programmed stock transactions that gave him 22% of Ares; he talked the board into making him CEO
- Knight and **Dunkelzahn** apparently coordinated during the Nanosecond Buyout — Dunkelzahn also acquired a significant stake (via Gavilan Ventures) and used it to help Knight maintain control
- Chicago, 2055: Knight Errant discovered and attacked the largest known insect spirit hive; the raid unleashed the bugs on Chicago's population; the Containment Zone lasted years; in February 2058 Ares used Strain III-Beta (an astral bacteria) to clean it out in under 12 hours
- The Chicago situation created a Ares public image problem that Knight Errant's response to the post-Dunkelzahn assassination riots largely repaired

---

## CATCo History That Matters

**Lucien Cross** was a programming genius working in corporate intelligence who helped Knight plan the Nanosecond Buyout. He didn't want to attach himself to Ares — he wanted startup capital. Knight paid him his commission. Cross kept copies of all the records proving how the Buyout was accomplished and who "David Gavilan" (Knight's cover identity) really was. He's had blackmail material on Knight for twenty-five years and has never used it — it's insurance.

Cross built CATCo through careful acquisitions, Quebec's anti-competition laws (which protect local corps from external takeovers), and his **Seraphim** — an elite intelligence and operations division that includes some of the best operatives in the world. In 2053, Cross grabbed Bioleve (a bioware company) that turned out to be an Ares subsidiary through shells. Knight sent an assassin. Cross survived because a Seraphim agent took the bullet. Dunkelzahn told Knight to back off. The rivalry went underground but never cooled.

Since Dunkelzahn's death removed that protection, Knight has tried three more times to kill Cross. Cross has beefed up security and intensified operations against Ares everywhere he can reach. Aurelius's arrival with blackmail material on Knight has now given Cross a seat on the Corporate Court and the resources for a genuine offensive.

## Key NPCs

### Damien Knight
**Role:** Ares president/CEO, chairman of the board

Possibly the most dangerous man in North America. Played chess with Dunkelzahn and sometimes won. Brilliant, utterly ruthless, no moral floor. Believes in exploiting every advantage and kicking people when they're down. His control of Ares is currently under more pressure than it's ever been, but he's been in worse positions and come out ahead. He will not sit still.

**For runners:** Knight never uses anyone directly. There are layers of intermediaries. Runners who work for "an Ares Johnson" may never know Knight is involved. His Johnsons are professional, well-resourced, and will cut runners loose if they become a liability.

### Leonard Aurelius
**Role:** Formerly Ares chairman, now minority partner at CATCo

Spent his life trying to reclaim Ares for the Aurelius family name, finally realized he was living his dead father's life rather than his own. An argument with his youngest daughter was the catalyst. Selling out of Ares — and selling to Vogel specifically, to ensure someone would make Knight's life difficult — was liberation. He's now taking risks he'd never have taken inside Ares, feels genuinely free for the first time, and still harbors hope of reclaiming Ares someday, but only on his own terms.

### Dr. Lucien Cross
**Role:** CATCo president/CEO

Approaching seventy, shows no signs of slowing. Skilled programmer who still personally oversees Cross Matrix Technologies. Known as "Old Stone-Face" by his employees, who respect him enormously. Polite, drives hard bargains without raising his voice, forgets nothing — favors or grudges. Meets all important contacts face-to-face (possibly because he's a social adept, though this is unconfirmed). Has been preparing for open war with Knight for years and now has the resources to fight it.

**For runners:** Cross's intermediaries are the Seraphim. Getting work from CATCo usually means dealing with a Seraphim Johnson — professional, evaluative, and watching to see how runners perform.

### Arthur Vogel
**Role:** Majority Ares shareholder, Sierra Inc. board member

A dwarf. Two years ago he was an eco-lawyer and second-place presidential candidate. Now he controls more Ares stock than Knight. His supporters say he's being cautious before taking on Knight openly. His detractors say he's sold out. The truth isn't clear yet. He's been examining every major Ares proposal for environmental impact but hasn't taken bold action. His relationship with Knight is outwardly neutral. If Vogel decides to team up with Daviar and her Gavilan Ventures votes, Knight could lose control of Ares entirely.

### Karen King
**Role:** Supervising VP of Ares Seattle

Has run Ares Seattle for ten years. Young-looking fifty, ambitious, looking to impress Knight enough to move up. Knight trusts her and rarely visits Seattle — which means she gets fewer chances to impress him directly. She's been escalating operations against Cross Advanced Electronics (CATCo's Seattle division) to try to get noticed, which risks becoming a problem if the campaign gets too expensive without results. If she wins decisively before Knight notices the cost, she's golden. If she doesn't, she may lose her job.

### Jezebel Surrateau
**Role:** Head of the Seattle Seraphim, de facto head of Cross Advanced Electronics

A physical adept who took a bullet in the spine saving Cross from Knight's assassin in 2053. Paralyzed from the waist down, refused surgery, operates from a high-tech wheelchair. Her tactical brilliance equals her lost physical gifts. She was sent to Seattle to rescue the situation when Bernard Cross ran it into the ground, and she's been quietly running it ever since while Bernard got the credit. She's also recently acquired control of part of Dunkelzahn's former shadow network in Seattle — a web of contacts and fixers that even some of its own members didn't know were working for a dragon. At least a Grade 2 initiate.

**For runners:** Surrateau is the actual person hiring runners for CATCo work in Seattle. She's professional, evaluative, and will be watching performance carefully. She handles Mr. Johnson duties directly for operations that require deniable assets.

### Raymond Briggs (Target)
**Role:** VP of Ares Seattle's Consumer Electronics division

Young, fit — spent years on Ares's competitive tae kwon do team. His father William is about to defect to Cross, and Cross wants Raymond out of Ares before Knight uses him as a hostage. Raymond doesn't know any of this. He's also been married secretly for six months to an ork woman he hasn't told his father about because he knows his father would disapprove.

## Adventure Framework: Double Crossover

**The Job:** Surrateau hires the runners to extract Raymond Briggs from Ares Seattle. He works on the top floor of Ares's Bellevue offices and lives in an Ares-owned executive neighborhood in Beaux Arts, connected by high-speed monorail. Security on both ends is tight. However, Surrateau has back doors into the Matrix systems of the building and monorail control center, provided by William Briggs. Pay is high. Time limit is one week. Raymond must not be injured. One of Surrateau's people, an Amerindian samurai named **Goliath**, accompanies the run to keep Raymond safe.

**Event 1 — The Extraction:**
Back doors help, but Knight Errant security at Ares HQ is the real thing. Surrateau has a simultaneous operation running against another Ares facility to pull security attention. Raymond will fight — he was competitive-level tae kwon do. Non-lethal takedown and exit. Getting him out of the building is the hard part.

**Event 2 — The DocWagon Problem:**
Somewhere during the escape or at the safe house, Raymond quietly breaks his Platinum DocWagon wristband — a band rupture alert summons a High Threat Response team to his location. DocWagon arrives armed for a hostage situation. They'll call Lone Star. Ares will hear from DocWagon and dispatch Knight Errant. The runners need to ditch the tracer bracelet and move fast.

**Event 3 — The Wife:**
Surrateau gets Raymond on comms with his father. Raymond reveals he's been secretly married for six months. His wife is still at their house in the Ares executive neighborhood. His father didn't know about the marriage — his wife is an ork, and Raymond knew his father wouldn't approve. Raymond insists on going back for her and has the passcodes and local knowledge. Goliath accompanies as Raymond's personal bodyguard. When they arrive, Ares security is already moving Mrs. Briggs to a secure area.

**Climax — Novatech Arrives:**
While the runners deal with Ares security at the Briggs residence, a Novatech team shows up. Villiers has been watching CATCo's extraction attempt and recognized an opportunity to poach Raymond for Novatech. The runners must choose: fight the Novatech team (makes an enemy of Villiers, keeps Cross happy), let Raymond go with them (partial payment, makes an enemy of Cross but not necessarily Villiers), or find another angle.

**Aftermath:**
- If runners get Raymond to CATCo: excellent reputation with CATCo, Surrateau will use them again, Raymond becomes head of Cross Advanced Electronics, his father joins CATCo as a special consultant
- If runners lose Raymond to Novatech: CATCo regards them as incompetent, word spreads; Novatech may offer test work
- Bernard Cross gets "promoted" to Montreal either way — he's out of Seattle

## Adventure Framework: Not In My Backyard

**The Setup:** Knight wants CATCo's domestic monopoly in Quebec disrupted. **Quick Trigger Systems** — a Detroit IC-software company secretly controlled by Knight through several shells — has applied to relocate its headquarters to Quebec, where CATCo's anti-competition protections would normally block exactly this kind of move. But the Quebec government has approved it and the Quebec City Council is voting in two weeks. The mayor supports it. The seven aldermen support it. Cross is furious but his Matrix operations against QTS have failed — QTS has sophisticated defenses.

The Seraphim need to make a physical run on QTS's Detroit HQ to get anything useful. Knight anticipates this and wants extra protection — from the shadows, not from Knight Errant (too obvious).

**The Job (For Runners Working For Ares):** A fixer offers the runners a two-week security contract at QTS in Detroit. Tour of the defenses, equipment provided. Night duty, on-call during days. One easy week. Then the Seraphim hit.

**Event 1 — The Seraphim Attack:**
The Seraphim use a multi-team approach:
- Four agents in stolen Knight Errant uniforms commandeer a KE vehicle
- A GMC truck full of Cutters gangers (paid for chaos) rams through the front gate and crashes into the main building
- The Seraphim follow the chaos inside, order security to chase the Cutters, and make for a jackpoint
- A helicopter with a mage on astral overwatch and sniper drones provides fire support overhead
- The truck explodes. Seraphim finish their job and get extracted
- Real KE and DocWagon show up in the aftermath

The Seraphim with the data will escape — they're that good. The runners should manage to capture some Cutters.

**Event 2 — The Trail:**
Knight's Johnson tells runners to track down whoever pulled the job and destroy the data. Interrogating Cutters gives a street name for the Seraphim handler. Several days of legwork leads to a Seraphim agent preparing to leave for Quebec. The Seraphim have noticed the runners and set up an ambush. Surviving runners who take a captive Seraphim find out that Cross has discovered enough about QTS to potentially sway the vote — the Seraphim are taking data to Quebec City's chief of police, **Sebastien Hull**, who influences the seven aldermen. Hull is a Cross supporter. If he knows Knight controls QTS, he can kill the vote.

**Climax — Stopping Hull:**
The vote is tomorrow morning at 10:15am. Stopping the data from reaching Hull is effectively impossible — but if Hull can be prevented from speaking at the council meeting, or delayed until after the vote, Knight wins. The runners are sent to Quebec with the objective: stop Hull from arriving before 11am, by any means necessary. Knight's people handle border crossing and gear.

Hull's convoy: armored Rolls Royce Phaeton limousine with a combat rigger, four personal bodyguards (two trolls, a physical adept, and a mage), four police motorcycle escorts. They don't stop for traffic lights. They have every tool a chief of police commands.

Creative approaches: traffic incidents, infrastructure failures, kidnapping someone Hull cares about, manufacturing a crisis requiring his personal attention, something that doesn't require fighting the convoy head-on.

**Aftermath:**
- If QTS vote passes: CATCo's Quebec monopoly is cracked, more shadow work follows as the two companies battle for the local market
- If QTS vote fails: the runners have failed a very powerful man; Ares will blacklist them in Detroit and other Ares-heavy cities; Knight will not forget
- Killing Hull makes every cop in Quebec a personal enemy

## Adventure Framework: Knight's Gambit

**The Setup:** In Dunkelzahn's will, the dragon left his antique chess set to Knight — with one exception: the black king piece went to Leonard Aurelius. During Aurelius's rapid departure from Ares, his secretary failed to retrieve the piece from a secret wall safe inside his old office in Knight Plaza, Ares corporate headquarters. Aurelius sends a runner team to get it back. Knight finds out and hires the player characters' team to intercept the rival team and get the piece.

**Event 1:**
Through legwork or ritual sorcery (the runners have blood samples from a rival team member who was shot during the Knight Plaza break-in), they track the rival team to Aurelius's large estate, where they're laying low until Aurelius returns from a business trip. The piece was handed to Aurelius's staff and placed in his private study with his chess set. Home occupants: Aurelius's daughter **Evelynn** (known as "Eve Night," guitarist for the Unholy Machine) and her boyfriend **Two-Chord Teddy** (plays for the Detroit Nightmares urban brawl team), taking advantage of Dad's absence.

**Event 2:**
The estate has the rival runner team, Eagle Security contractors, and a home security system. The piece is no longer in the study — Eve stole it to mess with her father. She and Teddy deny knowing anything. She's lying. The piece is in Teddy's coat.

**Event 3:**
Neither team wants to report failure. Aurelius returns, discovers the piece is missing, assumes the player characters took it (they're the obvious suspects since they raided his house), and puts a bounty on them. Knight hears Aurelius is after the runners and assumes they double-crossed him, putting his own bounty on them. Both factions are now hunting the runners and their contacts are avoiding them. The runners need to find where the piece actually went before this gets worse.

**Climax:**
Eve wants to play chess with Damien Knight — specifically to yank her father's chain. She's arranged a date. She doesn't know how to play yet, hence the crash course. She's at a hipster cafe getting lessons from an old squatter. When confronted, she admits she has the piece but won't surrender it — she'll only deal if the runners help her set up the game with Knight.

Knight will agree to a game if asked (he's amused). The runners escort Eve to the meet. The rival runner team, acting on Aurelius's orders, follows them and tries to crash the party.

**Aftermath:**
Eve brings a copy of the piece to the game, not the original — she had a fake made. The fraud would be detectable under close examination, but probably not in a firefight. Whether the real piece goes back to Aurelius or not is her decision. If she and Knight hit it off well enough, this could be an ongoing complication — Damien Knight being briefly entertained by Leonard Aurelius's rebellious daughter is a situation nobody wanted.

## Shorter Adventure Ideas

### Look Before You Leap
Before leaving Ares, Aurelius wants to take a pet research project on crystal-growing and fiber-optic development — something that would make his offer to CATCo more appealing. The project is on Ares's **Daedalus orbital platform**. Aurelius provides runner IDs as repair crew, shuttle tickets up and back (24-hour window), and access codes. Inside the station: no magic (space), no firearms (station policy), rigged security, retinal maglocks, well-armed guards with tasers and stun batons. The shuttle down will be intercepted. Ares prefers not to shoot down its own vessel — instead it'll force it to land and take the passengers.

### Return Policy
A biotech corp (Reactive Meditech, being acquired by Cross) issued emergency bearer bonds during the takeover fight that are about to mature and will destroy the company financially. Their security director has located the bonds — in safe deposit boxes at a well-protected bank. He hires runners to steal them. The bonds are owned by Lucien Cross, who bought them specifically to squeeze RM into submission. The Seraphim will identify the runners after the heist.

### Plague of Angels
An Alamos 20K faction wants samples of Strain III-Beta (the Ares bacteria that killed the Chicago insect spirits). They hire runners under a false flag — presenting as Awakened Liberation Front members who want to "liberate" devil rats from an Ares thaumaturgical lab in Seattle. They're actually going to use the rats to spread Strain III through areas of Seattle populated by ghouls, metahuman mages, and others they consider undesirable. Runners who figure out the real plan have a decision to make. Then Strain III outbreaks start appearing across the city and Ares comes looking for the source.

### Green Piece
Leviathan Technical (Ares subsidiary in Silicon Valley) has been illegally dumping biomedical waste. Terra First! eco-activists have partial evidence but not enough. A Nakatomi-faction Fuchi decker hacked Leviathan's systems and got more evidence — then picked up a data worm that reported back to Ares. Ares is sending a kill team. Vogel hires the runners to reach the decker first. The eco-activists don't know Vogel sent the runners and won't be friendly. If runners get the data, Vogel can pressure Ares from the inside. If Ares destroys it first, Terra First! will attempt sabotage anyway.

### Virtual Funds
Villiers wants to know where Vogel got the money to buy Aurelius's Ares stake. Through intermediaries, he hires runners to trace the money. The trail leads through randomly timed transfers from anonymous accounts, a teleporting SAN, Vogel's private office at Sierra Inc. in Sacramento, and eventually a virtual bank in Santa Fe in the Pueblo Corporate Council. The physical address of the bank is a building gutted by fire. PSF will arrest anyone investigating too closely. All Matrix records have been adjusted. Villiers already knows what he wanted to know before the runners figure it out.

## How This Track Ends

By late 2060:
- **Ares** is largely intact but under real pressure for the first time. Knight still controls the company but Vogel holds more stock and could align with Daviar at any moment. The Ares-CATCo conflict is ongoing and increasingly expensive.
- **CATCo** is a genuine AAA megacorp with a Corporate Court seat. Aurelius's money and Seraphim intelligence have given Cross offensive capability he didn't have before. They're still smaller than Ares but no longer simply playing defense.
- **Vogel** is the wild card. His intentions remain opaque. He's examining environmental practices, not moving against Knight, and leading Sierra in a more assertive direction. If he and Daviar ever align their votes, Knight has a serious problem.
- **Seattle specifically:** Cross Advanced Electronics is now under Raymond Briggs's firm leadership. Surrateau stays in Seattle running the Seraphim. Karen King of Ares Seattle is still trying to crack CAE. The shadow conflict between the two companies in Seattle continues and will intensify.
