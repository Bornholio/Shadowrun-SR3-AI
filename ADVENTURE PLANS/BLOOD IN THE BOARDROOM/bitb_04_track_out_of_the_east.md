# Blood in the Boardroom — Track 4: Out of the East (Yamatetsu/PPG)

## What's Happening and Why

For over fifty years, five Japanese megacorps have dominated the corporate world. Everything in the Pacific flows through them. The Pacific Prosperity Group — an alliance of non-Japanese Pacific Rim corporations led by Wuxing of Hong Kong — has tried repeatedly to break that dominance and failed every time. The math never worked: even combining multiple smaller corps, they couldn't match a single AAA Japanese megacorp.

Then two things changed simultaneously:

1. **Yuri Shibanokuji** — an ork, the secret son of Yamatetsu's chairman — inherited his father's controlling stock in Yamatetsu and decided to actually use it
2. **Buttercup** — a free spirit and significant Yamatetsu shareholder who has been quietly working toward metahuman equality for decades — was ready to make her move

Together, they pulled Yamatetsu out of Japan entirely, relocated to Vladivostok, and joined the Pacific Prosperity Group. The combination of a AAA megacorp's muscle and the PPG's network created something that could actually challenge Japanese economic dominance of the Pacific.

Japan's response — harassment through environmental regulations, stock manipulation, racism — backfired. Yamatetsu left anyway. Japanese metahumans were left worse off than before, used as scapegoats for the departure. The PPG now has two AAA members (Yamatetsu and Wuxing, which achieved AAA status by getting a Corporate Court seat) and is growing rapidly.

---

## The Shibanokuji Story

The emotional center of this track is a father who failed his son and spent decades trying to make it right.

**Tadamako Shibanokuji** was a young division manager when he fell in love with a Russian port official named Tatiana Trigorin in Vladivostok. They married. In 2021, their two-year-old son **Yuri** underwent goblinization — became an ork. Tadamako was fighting for control of the family company's board at the time, and an ork son would give his enemies ammunition. He sent Yuri to Russia with Tatiana. She never spoke to him directly again. He spent the rest of his long career overcompensating — voting against metahuman interests to prove his objectivity — while the guilt accumulated.

In his final years, under Buttercup's influence and his own moral reckoning, he began quietly supporting the pro-metahuman faction on the board. He planned to eventually acknowledge Yuri publicly. He never got to. A stroke in January 2059 left him speechless. He died six weeks later without recovering.

**Yuri Shibanokuji** grew up in Vladivostok knowing his father had chosen his corporation over him. He has reasons to be bitter and largely isn't — he made a life in Vladivostok, built his own career, and when the inheritance dropped in his lap, he came to Kyoto not for revenge but to try to finish what his father had started. He has his father's stubbornness and none of his father's willingness to compromise on metahuman dignity.

Buttercup knew about Yuri for years and spent that time ensuring his influence would work in her favor when the moment came.

## Yamatetsu's Internal Split

Before the move, the board was divided:

**Conservative faction** (led by CEO Saru Iwano and former chairman Hideo Yoshida): Regards metahumans as deformed and sinister. Wants Yamatetsu to conform to Japanese social norms. Controls the company's day-to-day operations and most of the executive ranks.

**Progressive faction** (Buttercup, Newton Chin whose sister is a dwarf, various lesser shareholders): Wants to make Yamatetsu's pro-metahuman reputation a reality rather than marketing copy. Gained momentum under Tadamako's late influence.

**Yuri's arrival** shifted the math. Buttercup had spent weeks before his arrival buying out minor shareholders. Combined with Yuri's inherited stock and Tatiana's shares, plus Buttercup's own holdings, they had over 50% of voting shares when Yuri's motion to relocate was called. Iwano was unable to block it.

Iwano remains as Yamatetsu CEO — Buttercup and Yuri make no move to remove him. He's doing his job and they don't need a power struggle on top of everything else.

## The Pacific Prosperity Group

**What it is:** A corporate coalition, similar to NATO or OPEC. Any Pacific Rim corporation in agreement with the charter can petition for membership. Each member has a representative on the PPG board; the board elects a chairman.

**Current membership includes:** Yamatetsu, Wuxing, Tan Tien, Eastern Tiger Corporation, Federated Boeing, Kwonsham Industries, Monobe International, Malaysian Independent Bank, and a dozen smaller corps.

**What membership gets you:** Special rates with the Malaysian Independent Bank (PPG's alternative to the Japan-aligned Pacific Rim Bank), favored trade status with other PPG corps and countries in their sphere, a united front against Japanese corporate dominance, and access to joint research and production-sharing programs.

**What it's building toward:** The Malaysian Independent Bank is growing toward the size of the Pacific Rim Bank. PPG-favored centers of commerce are developing in Hong Kong, Singapore, Vladivostok, and Vancouver. The Japanese corporations are no longer able to treat the Pacific as their private market.

## Key NPCs

### Yuri Shibanokuji
**Role:** Yamatetsu chairman of the board

An ork in his late thirties, raised in Vladivostok, now running one of the world's largest corporations from a city he called home. He survived at least one assassination attempt from his own board before the relocation vote. He's not especially ideological — he just believes metahumans deserve to be treated like people, which shouldn't be a radical position but apparently is. He leads by example rather than manifesto. He's still learning the megacorporate game and leans heavily on Buttercup's experience.

**For runners:** Yamatetsu under Yuri is more likely to hire runners who are metahuman or who have demonstrated they're not the kind of people who have a problem working with metas. Not a hard rule, but Yuri's people notice.

### Buttercup
**Role:** Free spirit, Yamatetsu board member, largest single shareholder

A free ally spirit summoned centuries before the Awakening who became free sometime before the twentieth century and has been observing humanity ever since. Dunkelzahn bound her in an underprivileged ork's body for a year and a day to teach her about equality — it worked. She's been working toward metahuman equity for decades, investing in corporations and using her influence carefully. She's ancient, vastly knowledgeable, and capable of extraordinary ruthlessness when she decides it's necessary.

In human form: a petite, pretty Japanese girl who looks approximately eighteen, with long black hair and an extremely expressive face. She doesn't walk so much as glide. She uses three-dimensional movement (floating slightly above the floor) without apparent self-consciousness.

**For runners:** Buttercup does not interact with runners directly. She operates through Yamatetsu's legitimate structure and her own network of contacts. If runners encounter her at all, they probably don't know what they're looking at.

### Saru Iwano
**Role:** Yamatetsu president/CEO

The anti-metahuman faction's leader. He lost the boardroom fight and is now executing the relocation he voted against. He's professional enough to do the job, bitter enough about the situation to look for opportunities to undermine the new direction when he can do so without obvious exposure. He and Buttercup have reached an uneasy working relationship — she doesn't remove him, he doesn't openly sabotage the relocation.

### Wu Lung-Wai
**Role:** Wuxing president/CEO, son of the PPG's original architect

Brilliant, charismatic, extraordinarily shrewd. Apparently magically active — some observers think he's a social adept whose talents run to persuasion and influence. Meets all important contacts face-to-face. He has built Wuxing into a megacorp in two years using a 200 million nuyen bequest from Dunkelzahn as the seed. Now the PPG's founding energy comes from him. He is "Hong Kong's Kingmaker" and is becoming one of the most important figures in Pacific commerce.

### Sun Runming
**Role:** Head of Wuxing's Seattle division

Portly, cheerful, apparently hedonistic. Business meetings with him turn into drinking sessions with bawdy jokes and expensive liquor. His "good ol' boy" act conceals an almost supernatural ability to keep his head clear and a reflexive habit of pouncing on any moment of weakness or slip of the tongue. People fall for the act once. His rivals generally don't get a second chance.

**For runners:** Sun Runming is the Wuxing face in Seattle. He hires deniable assets, pays well, and will be watching performance carefully from behind the persona of a man who doesn't seem to be watching anything.

### Newton Chin
**Role:** Yamatetsu senior VP

Pro-metahuman faction leader within the corporation. His younger sister Sophia is a dwarf — personal stake in the question beyond just politics. He was instrumental in building the progressive faction's voting power before Yuri arrived.

### Ramon Dizon
**Role:** Head of Yamatetsu Australasia

One of the few remaining Filipino board members from Yamatetsu's founding companies. The Japanese faction quietly squeezed out most of the Filipino influence over the years; Dizon survived through a combination of competence and political skill.

## The PPG Member Corps (Brief Profiles)

### Eastern Tiger Corporation
Unified Korea's largest corp, based in Pusan. Heavy manufacturing and petrochemicals, expanding into biotech and consumer goods. President Se-jong Lee has a reputation for being quick to seize opportunities and equally quick to cut and run when situations turn sour. His Seattle division head, Yong-jo Moon (a nephew), is dealing with truck attacks by the Red Rovers go-gang and can't get Lee to authorize more security money — Lee thinks it's a diversion for something else. Moon is considering shadow assets but lacks the nerve to reach out yet.

### Federated Boeing
The only significant UCAS corp in the PPG, joining mainly to get back at Mitsuhama for repeated takeover attempts. Has large plants throughout the Pacific but a reputation for ruthless exploitation of local resources and labor that makes their membership contested within the PPG. CEO Jessica Sirianni is a genuinely self-made executive — grew up a squatter in Auburn, put herself through college, worked up from draftswoman to CEO through competence and rule-breaking. Hands-on manager with shady connections predating her corp career.

### Kwonsham Industries
Korean corp formed during the post-reunification buyout period from a coalition of former North Korean companies. One of Unified Korea's largest employers. President Jae-Myung Kim is making multiple visits to Seattle to establish a US transportation division. Credible rumors suggest meetings with the Choson Seoulpa Ring during those visits.

### Monobe International
A "double-A" corp that has been sitting just below AAA for years, held back by a corrupt former president who preferred personal enrichment to expansion. New president Toshio Mitsukuri staged a vicious internal coup in January 2059 and is now aggressively pursuing AAA status by grabbing whatever Yamatetsu, Renraku, and Fuchi have left undefended. The former president's private plane disappeared in flight shortly after Mitsukuri took over. The remaining board walks on eggshells.

### Tan Tien
A small but extremely capable Chinese research corp specializing in protein data storage, cranial cyberdeck design, and nonintrusive neural interfaces. Has maintained independence despite multiple AAA takeover attempts through unknown means (rumors range from otaku protection to the great dragon Lung having a stake). Licensing their research to the highest bidder has kept them solvent. Their newest project, a multi-user cyberdecking interface called Parallel Thought, is rumored to be PPG-exclusive on completion.

### Wuxing
See Wu Lung-Wai above. The PPG's founding energy. AAA status achieved via the Corporate Court seat that came from Yamatetsu nominating Li Feng as a compromise candidate for David Hague's vacant seat. Wuxing has Triad connections that cannot be proved.

## Adventure Framework: The Needle and the Damage Done

*(Note: This framework is referenced in the sourcebook's table of contents but its full text is partially implied through the track structure. The following is GM-ready content based on the track's themes.)*

**Setup:** A Yamatetsu subsidiary in Seattle — operating under its own name, with no obvious connection to Yamatetsu — has been targeted for acquisition by one of the Japanese megacorps (Mitsuhama, Renraku, or Shiawase, depending on which conflict you want to tie in). The acquisition would be straightforward if the PPG weren't involved. Yamatetsu quietly hires runners to complicate the acquisition — disrupt communications, delay legal filings, find evidence of hostile intent that can be brought to the Corporate Court.

The runners will discover the subsidiary is more important than it looks. It's either a research facility for something Yamatetsu needs, or it has data the Japanese megacorp wants to bury. This connects to the PPG's broader efforts to establish independent supply chains that don't run through Japanese-controlled banking.

## Adventure Framework: Mainframed

**The Setup:** A PPG-member cargo vessel has been seized by pirates working for a Japanese megacorp (technically through several shells). The ship and its cargo — sophisticated manufacturing equipment bound for a new PPG facility — are being held in international waters near the Philippines. The PPG can't use official channels without escalating to something that looks like a megacorp naval conflict. Yamatetsu or Wuxing hires runners to handle it quietly.

**The Problem:** The pirates are well-equipped, the ship's own Matrix systems have been compromised by the hijackers' decker, and there's a timer — the ship will be escorted into Japanese-controlled waters if the runners take more than 48 hours.

**The Complications:**
- The shipping crew is still aboard and must not be harmed
- The megacorp's fingerprints need to stay clean — the pirates cannot admit who hired them
- The manufacturing equipment is more valuable than it appears — it's prototypes for technology Yamatetsu wants kept out of Japanese hands, which is why it was targeted

**Event options:** Board the ship (maritime insertion under darkness), deal with a compromised Matrix system to retake ship control, negotiate with or fight the pirates, prevent Japanese corporate security from "rescuing" the ship before the runners can

This framework is adapted for a piracy campaign in the sourcebook but works equally well for a straight runner crew.

## Adventure Framework: What's In A Name?

**The Setup:** Yamatetsu's move to Vladivostok has left dozens of subsidiary companies in legal limbo throughout Japan and the Pacific. Several of these "orphans" don't know who actually owns them anymore — their corporate parent chain is a tangle of shell companies that's been disrupted by the relocation. Some of them are finding out they were secretly owned by Yamatetsu, Fuchi, or other megacorps and reacting very badly to the news.

One such orphan is a medium-sized magical research firm somewhere in the Pacific Rim. They've been doing genuinely innovative research in hermetic theory. Buttercup wants to bring them under Yamatetsu's umbrella openly — but someone else (Mitsuhama or Saeder-Krupp) wants to acquire them before that can happen, and they're not being polite about it.

**The runners' job:** Make contact with the firm's leadership before the rival acquisition team does, assess whether the firm is willing to negotiate with Yamatetsu, and ensure they survive the conversation. The magical nature of the research means physical security will include magical defenses, and the firm's own mages may be hostile to any corporate approach after having been secretly owned without their knowledge.

This framework is specifically noted in the sourcebook as working well for a magical runner team.

## Adventure Framework: Ancient Chinese Secret, Huh?

**The Setup:** Someone in the PPG has a leak. Sensitive negotiating positions for a major trade agreement between PPG corps are showing up in Japanese megacorp strategy sessions before the PPG meetings happen. The source is inside one of the PPG member corps — but which one?

Wuxing hires runners to investigate. The runners will have to work their way through several PPG member companies' operations, which requires navigating different corporate cultures, different languages, different security approaches, and the constant awareness that any of the member companies' employees might be the leaker — or might become suspicious of why strangers are poking around.

The actual source turns out to be multiple levels more complicated than a single mole: the leak is coming through the Malaysian Independent Bank's Matrix systems, which have been compromised by a sophisticated intrusion that's been running for months. The intrusion traces back to the Zurich-Orbital Gemeinschaft Bank — which means either the Corporate Court's bank is compromised, or someone with access to it is running a private operation.

## Shorter Adventure Ideas

### Truck Stop
Eastern Tiger's Seattle operation is being hit by the Red Rovers go-gang on a regular basis. Moon (Seattle division head) finally got authorization to hire outside security after Lee stopped answering his calls on the subject. The runners are hired to protect the next several shipments and find out who's behind the attacks. The Red Rovers are being paid by Mitsuhama through an intermediary. Exposing this would be useful to Lee — but only if the runners can document it clearly enough for use in a corporate complaint.

### Mob Clash
Kwonsham's Jae-Myung Kim has been meeting with the Choson Seoulpa Ring in Seattle to negotiate distribution deals for a new Korean consumer goods division. The Triads (tied to Wuxing, through channels) have decided that a Korean corp backed by a Korean crime syndicate cutting distribution deals in Seattle is not acceptable. Runners are caught in the middle — hired by one side, encountering the other, and gradually realizing that both sides have legitimate grievances and that there isn't a clean answer.

### Tome Raiders
Tan Tien's Parallel Thought interface is further along than anyone outside the company knows. A Japanese megacorp has hired a decker crew to steal the research. Tan Tien hires runners for physical security of the facility while the problem is identified — but the decker crew isn't the only problem. Someone inside Tan Tien is helping them. Finding the mole without tipping off the decker crew or the megacorp backing them requires careful play.

## How This Track Ends

By late 2060:
- **Yamatetsu** is headquartered in Vladivostok, led by Yuri Shibanokuji and Buttercup, aligned with the PPG. Saru Iwano still runs day-to-day operations. The company has left Japan almost entirely — its subsidiaries there have either relocated, been acquired by other Japanese corps, or are being dismantled.
- **Japan** is economically weaker than it's been in decades. Five megacorps still dominate, but Fuchi is gone and Yamatetsu has defected. The metahuman population that Yamatetsu was the largest employer of is now worse off. Racial violence has spiked.
- **The PPG** is a genuine alternative power structure in the Pacific. The Malaysian Independent Bank is growing. PPG-member corporations are increasingly able to operate independently of Japanese-controlled financial systems.
- **Wuxing** has a Corporate Court seat and Li Feng is finding his feet as a justice — every faction thinks they can manipulate him, which is exactly what Wu Lung-Wai expected.
- **Vladivostok** is becoming a significant corporate hub, drawing other companies who want to be near a friendly megacorp HQ.
- **Seattle specifically:** Sun Runming runs Wuxing's Seattle operation. Eastern Tiger's Moon division is still having trouble with the Red Rovers. Kwonsham is establishing a transportation division. The city is increasingly a PPG commercial presence to rival the Japanese megacorps.

The broader implication: Japanese dominance of the Pacific is weakening for the first time in decades. The PPG is the beginning of a new power structure, not the final form of it. For runners, this means new employers, new conflicts, and new factions to navigate — the landscape of corporate power in the Pacific is shifting in ways that won't stabilize for years.
