# Blood in the Boardroom — GM Setup & Hooks

## What This Is

A campaign sourcebook, not a linear adventure. The megacorp wars of 2057–2060 play out in the background of the Sixth World regardless of what your runners do. This sourcebook gives you the machinery behind those events and multiple entry points for runner involvement. The outcome of the corporate conflicts is largely predetermined by canon — your job is to use the chaos as a backdrop for missions, not to simulate the boardroom votes.

There are four major conflict threads (Tracks), each following a different corporate power struggle. You can run one track start to finish, let runners jump between tracks, or use all four simultaneously as background noise that occasionally explodes into a firefight on the runners' doorstep.

---

## The Big Picture

Everything flows from one event: **Dunkelzahn's assassination on 9 August 2057**, the night of his inauguration as UCAS President. His will, read publicly afterward, contained dozens of bequests that destabilised the megacorporate balance of power. The specific bequests that matter:

- **Miles Lanier** (Fuchi head of security) receives Dunkelzahn's Renraku stock and a board seat → triggers Fuchi civil war
- **Arthur Vogel** (eco-lawyer) receives Dunkelzahn's Ares stock and a board seat → threatens Damien Knight's control of Ares
- **Nadja Daviar** (Dunkelzahn's translator, new UCAS VP) inherits Gavilan Ventures, which holds 12% of Ares
- **Wu Lung-Wai / Wuxing** receives 200 million nuyen → enables the Pacific Prosperity Group
- **Inazo Aneki** (Renraku CEO) receives the Seal of the Green Gloves (entry to Tibet — becomes relevant in 2060)

These bequests set four corporate conflicts in motion simultaneously. The runners will feel the effects long before they understand the causes.

## Hooking the Runners

The corp war creates an almost unlimited supply of shadow work. The key is that **runners don't need to understand what's happening to be caught up in it** — they just need to take jobs. Understanding comes later, if they survive.

### The Johnson Approach

The simplest entry point. A corporate Johnson hires the team for what looks like a routine run — extraction, datasteal, sabotage, protection — and it turns out to be a move in the corp war chess game. The runners may not realise this until they're knee-deep in it. Let them accumulate a couple of ordinary-seeming runs before the bigger picture starts to emerge.

- Corps can't trust their own employees right now. Divided loyalties mean internal security may be compromised. Outside deniable assets are more reliable.
- A Johnson working for one faction of Fuchi won't necessarily identify which faction they're working for — or even that Fuchi is involved.
- Payment is usually good because the stakes are high.

### The Contact Web

If any runner has corporate contacts, those contacts are living through the corp war. A fixer who works with Fuchi corps is suddenly having to pick sides. A corporate contact might reach out to the runners — not as a Johnson but as someone who needs help and is out of options.

- A contact may be caught between factions and need extraction from their own employer
- A contact may have information that multiple factions want, and need a safe house and bodyguards
- A contact may have gone missing, and another runner's contact is asking questions

### Making It Personal

The corp war's collateral damage lands on ordinary people. Runners have SINless lives, but they have neighborhoods, they have people they care about, they have fixers and bartenders and street docs who are all affected.

- The Redmond Barrens crash (Flight 1118, July 2059) destroys acres of the Barrens and kills hundreds. If any runner lives in or has ties to Redmond, this hits close to home.
- Corporate security crackdowns spill into the sprawl — Knight Errant patrols in unexpected areas, Renraku Red Samurai operating in the open.
- The Seattle Matrix RTG attack (March 2060) is terrifying and unexplained. Any runner who was jacked in during the ten-minute window has a very bad time.

### Security Work

Corps and their subsidiaries are hiring outside security during the war — they don't know who on their internal team has been turned. This gets runners embedded inside corporate operations, where they'll inevitably witness things they weren't supposed to see.

## Running the Corporate NPCs

The megacorp executives in this book are powerful, paranoid, and operating through layers of deniable assets. Runners will rarely if ever meet them directly. What they'll meet are:

- **Mr. Johnsons** who work for those executives (and may not know their employer's full agenda)
- **Security teams** acting on orders from those executives
- **Other runner teams** hired by rival factions for conflicting objectives

When portraying corp employees, resist the temptation to make everyone a loyal drone or a scheming villain. The war has created genuine confusion and distress at every level:

- Some employees are picking sides based on personal loyalty to a manager
- Some are terrified of losing their jobs and not thinking about the bigger picture
- Some are opportunists looking to land somewhere profitable
- A few are true believers in their corp's mission

This variation makes the corporate world feel real and gives runners room to find unexpected allies — or unexpected enemies — within the same corporation.

## Story Structure Options

### Single Track

Pick the conflict most interesting to your group and run it start to finish. Use events from the other tracks as background noise — things runners hear about on Shadowland but don't get involved with.

### Jumping Between Tracks

Let runners follow the work. A run for Novatech (Track 1) connects to a Renraku datasteal (Track 2) when they discover the same research is at the center of both. This requires more GM prep but creates a richer experience.

### Freeform

Leak information slowly through contacts and Shadowland posts. Let runners decide what they want to pursue. Have all four tracks running simultaneously in the background. This is the most realistic approach and the hardest to run — requires the GM to know all four tracks well enough to improvise.

### Novel Structure

Pick a clear starting point (the Dunkelzahn assassination, or the Flight 1118 crash, or Novatech's announcement) and run events in rough chronological order toward a defined endpoint. Good for groups that want a campaign with a shape.

## Key Seattle Flashpoints

Most of the war's effects can be felt in Seattle regardless of where specific corporate HQs are located. Seattle-specific pressure points:

- **Fuchi's Seattle operations** are a battleground between the Villiers, Nakatomi, and Yamana factions throughout 2058-2059
- **The Redmond Barrens crash** (Flight 1118) creates weeks of chaos, emergency response, and media attention
- **David Hague's body** is found in an abandoned Redmond apartment building a week after the crash
- **The Renraku arcology** closes in December 2059 — runners who try to get in or out will find it impenetrable
- **The Seattle Matrix RTG attack** (March 2060) affects every decker and anyone with headware in the city
- **Samantha Villiers** runs Novatech Northwest from Seattle and is a potential ongoing contact/employer
- **Darren Villiers** (Richard's dwarf brother, physical adept, Grade 3 initiate) operates covertly in Seattle as Novatech's director of special assets — he's the one hiring runners for sensitive Novatech work
