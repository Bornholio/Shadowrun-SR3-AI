# Blood in the Boardroom — Track 1: Civil War (Fuchi)

## What's Happening and Why

Fuchi Industrial Electronics is destroying itself from the inside. For twenty years, three families have run the corporation as an uneasy triumvirate: **Richard Villiers** (Fuchi Americas), **Shikei Nakatomi** (Fuchi Asia), and **Korin Yamana** (Fuchi Pan-Europa). None of them could gain a decisive edge over the others, which kept the company stable if not exactly harmonious.

Dunkelzahn's will broke that equilibrium two ways:
1. Miles Lanier — Fuchi's head of internal security and Villiers' closest ally — received Renraku stock and walked out the door to join Renraku's board
2. Villiers received additional Fuchi stock, solidifying his position as the largest single shareholder

Lanier's departure looked like a disaster for Villiers. In fact, it was the opening move of a long con. Lanier fed Renraku inside information on Fuchi's systems and security — which Nakatomi and Yamana used as a pretext to come after Villiers. What they didn't realise was that Villiers was already building his escape hatch: a separate corporate empire that he'd eventually incorporate as **Novatech**.

By the time Nakatomi and Yamana figured out what Villiers was doing, he'd already stripped Fuchi Americas of its most valuable assets. He then sold off his remaining Fuchi stock at peak price (forcing Nakatomi and Yamana into a bidding war that tripled the stock value), pocketed the profit, and left them holding the ruins.

What's left of Fuchi after Villiers leaves tears itself apart between Nakatomi and Yamana. Yamana eventually wins, marries into the Shiawase family, and sells Fuchi to Shiawase entirely. Nakatomi, furious, throws his lot in with Renraku. On 28 July 2060, Fuchi Industrial Electronics ceases to exist.

---

## The Fuchi Civil War in Play

During 2058-2059, before the endgame plays out, Seattle is a low-intensity warzone between the three Fuchi factions. This is the sweet spot for runner involvement:

- **Datasteal and counter-datasteal** between Fuchi subsidiaries nominally owned by different factions
- **Extraction runs** on employees who've defected or are about to
- **Sabotage** of one faction's facilities disguised to look like the other faction's work
- **Security testing** — factions checking whether their own people are loyal, using runners as cat's-paws
- **Protection work** for executives who don't know which of their own bodyguards can be trusted

The war intensifies after Flight 1118 (July 2059) and Hague's death. By October 2059, when Villiers announces Novatech, the pretense of unity collapses entirely. After that, it's open season.

## Key NPCs

### Richard Villiers
**Role:** Architect of the whole thing. CEO of Fuchi Americas, then founder/president of Novatech.

The most dangerous man in this story, and probably the one runners will work for most often without knowing it. Villiers operates through layers of intermediaries. He is methodical, patient, and ruthless — his ability to manipulate people and events over a multi-year timescale while keeping his true intentions hidden is extraordinary. He may have arranged Kiyoshi Nakatomi's murder forty years ago. He definitely engineered the Lanier/Renraku scheme. He probably had something to do with Flight 1118 and David Hague's death — but nothing can be proved.

**For runners:** Villiers will pay well and his Johnsons will always have plausible cover stories. The catch is that "working for Villiers" and "working for a Fuchi Americas Johnson" may be the same thing for months before runners realise it. Once they're associated with Novatech, Nakatomi and Yamana will treat them as enemies.

### Miles Lanier
**Role:** Former Fuchi security chief, Renraku board member (briefly), then Novatech director of security.

A former UCAS Army sniper who transitioned into corporate security. Brilliant tactician. Arrogant, demanding, difficult to work with. His "defection" to Renraku was a performance — he was always working for Villiers. After the Corporate Court forced him out of Renraku, he went straight to Novatech.

In the Novatech period, Lanier is purging executives at former Fuchi Americas subsidiaries that are being converted to Novatech divisions. He's looking for Nakatomi and Yamana loyalists and removing them. He'll also use runners for this work — it's cleaner than using Novatech's own security people.

### Samantha Villiers
**Role:** Richard's ex-wife, VP of Novatech Northwest (based in Seattle).

Divorced over a decade ago but still a valued partner. She's the one who holds the 2% of post-Villiers Fuchi stock that makes her the tiebreaker between Nakatomi and Yamana — she sells to Yamana in January 2060, triggering the endgame. In Seattle she's a legitimate executive contact, one of the city's most eligible singles, and a potential recurring employer for runners who make a good impression.

### Darren Villiers
**Role:** Richard's younger brother, Novatech Seattle director of special assets.

A dwarf. A physical adept with a knack for stealth and burglary, Grade 3 initiate. He held the title of senior VP of Seattle operations at Fuchi but spent most of his time running covert operations rather than sitting behind a desk. Now does the same for Novatech. He's the one who directly hires runners for sensitive work — sometimes running with them on particularly delicate jobs without revealing who he is or who he works for.

### Shikei Nakatomi
**Role:** Head of Fuchi Asia, later a Renraku board member.

Holds Fuchi together with Yamana after Villiers leaves, then loses the tiebreaker vote when Samantha sells to Yamana. His hatred of Villiers becomes the dominant force in his life. He eventually buys the Lanier Renraku stock block from the Zurich-Orbital bank, joins Renraku's board, and begins using Renraku as a platform for revenge. Runs as "the Business Butcher" — professionally ruthless, now also personally motivated.

### Korin Yamana
**Role:** Head of Fuchi Pan-Europa, eventually head of what remains of Fuchi, then a Shiawase board member.

Ninety years old by the endgame. He achieves the goal he's pursued for forty years — sole control of Fuchi — just in time for it to be worthless. He doesn't have the energy to rebuild from scratch and sees Shiawase as the best available deal. Marries Mitsuko Shiawase in a nationally televised ceremony and sells Fuchi to Shiawase in its entirety. Moves to Osaka. Possibly the saddest figure in this whole story.

## Adventure Framework: Crash Team

**When:** Early July 2059, the night of Flight 1118

**The Job As Presented:** The runners are hired to intercept and detain Corporate Court justice David Hague when he arrives in Seattle on a late flight from Tokyo. Their Johnson wants Hague brought to a safe house in Redmond, where the Johnson will take over. The job is presented as a snatch — keep Hague alive, keep it quiet.

**What Goes Wrong:** Hague is on Flight 1118. It crashes into the Redmond Barrens at 300+ km/h, killing 153 passengers and 51 people on the ground. The runners' plan is immediately obsolete.

**The New Mission:** Johnson calls immediately. Forget the live snatch — now he needs Hague's body (or at minimum Hague's briefcase, which bears the Corporate Court logo). The briefcase is handcuffed to Hague's wrist. Getting to the crash site is the first problem.

**Getting to the Crash Site:**
- Every DocWagon, Lone Star patrol, fire truck, news crew, and rubbernecker in Seattle is heading the same direction
- Go-gangs are running wild while Lone Star is occupied
- Ground travel to the site is effectively impossible
- The Johnson can arrange a Hughes Stallion helicopter pickup from the roof of a nearby parking garage — if the runners can get there

**At the Crash Site:**
The plane hit in multiple pieces across several square miles of the Barrens:
- Tail section in James Lake, mostly submerged
- Nose section against a hillside to the east
- Everything between them is debris, bodies, fire, and chaos

Hague had a first-class ticket — he was probably near the front. When runners reach the hillside where the nose section came down, they find what looks like a DocWagon Crisis Response Team already on scene. Problems:
1. Their ambulance arrived from the east — all other rescue vehicles are coming from the west
2. The "paramedics" are carrying silenced SMGs
3. When a real DocWagon team arrives shortly after, the fake team opens fire on them

The fake team is Renraku operatives. They find Hague's body, cut the briefcase from his wrist, and load both into their ambulance.

**The Chase:** Runners must pursue the Renraku team to an abandoned apartment building in Redmond. Inside, two cyberware techs are waiting to surgically remove Hague's headware memory — they're not worried about damaging the body, but they don't want to damage the gear. One operative is working on the briefcase. Four guards.

**Climax:** Once runners retrieve whatever they can (body, briefcase, or both), the Johnson tells them to return Hague's body to the crash site — it needs to be found there. Getting back through the chaos is as hard as getting out was.

**GM Notes:**
- The Johnson is working for Villiers. Hague was a Yamana supporter — his death (with Villiers' ally Lynn Osborne as the only remaining Fuchi Court rep) is convenient. Whether Villiers arranged the crash is left deliberately ambiguous.
- Renraku wanted Hague's headware memory because he knew about the Lanier scheme. Whatever's in that memory implicates both parties.
- The real DocWagon team, if the runners interact with them, is just doing their job in a nightmare situation. If runners steal their ambulance, DocWagon will want it back.
- Reporters may have caught runners on camera. Depending on their behavior, this could be good or bad for their reputation.

**Follow-Up Hooks:**
- Renraku is spreading rumours that Villiers engineered the crash. Villiers may hire runners to find proof it was someone else (or to suppress proof that it was him).
- Hague's body is eventually found in the apartment building where the Renraku team took it — a week after the crash. The media wants to know how it got there.
- DocWagon's investigation into the fake team at the crash site may circle back to the runners.

## Adventure Framework: Loose Lips Fry Chips

**The Setup:** A freelance decker the runners know — call him HAL — has hit a Fuchi Asia system in San Francisco and run into a piece of experimental black IC code-named "Stoolie." It works like a neurological truth serum: the victim compulsively reveals everything they know, to anyone who will listen, with no ability to stop themselves. HAL is currently in a public shadowrunner bar, loudly describing his illegal runs in detail. To everyone.

**The Problem:** In his current state, HAL will sell out every runner he's ever worked with. He's aware this is happening and can't stop it. He's also carrying information about Fuchi Asia's internal plans that every Fuchi faction desperately wants — and which is a death warrant for him once they have it.

**Event 1:** Fuchi Asia security enters the bar to arrest HAL. If runners don't intervene, HAL gets taken to a secure facility for research and then execution. If they intervene, they now have HAL and Fuchi Asia has them on their list.

**Event 2:** The moment runners hit the street with HAL, two more Fuchi security teams appear — one from Fuchi Americas, one from Pan-Europa. Three-way fight between the factions, all wanting HAL alive, none willing to let the others have him. The runners can use the chaos to slip away.

**Event 3:** Safe house, for now. HAL remembers seeing a psychotropic counter-conditioning program in the Fuchi Asia system during his run. He also now realises he stumbled onto something very big — big enough to sell, but also big enough that Fuchi will kill everyone who's heard it. That includes the runners now.

**Climax:** The counter-program is in the Fuchi Asia San Francisco host, but it's booby-trapped with trace IC and asks for the physical location of whoever downloads it. HAL, in his current state, will answer truthfully. Fuchi strike teams will converge on their location. Multiple Fuchi factions will fight over them. Getting HAL cured and everyone out alive requires outrunning at least two corporate security teams.

**GM Notes:**
- HAL as an NPC is a gift — use his compulsive honesty to reveal old plot threads, embarrassing truths, or seeds for future runs
- Fuchi Americas developed the original "Stoolie" IC. They want to know how it worked in practice.
- The "big information" HAL found can be whatever serves your campaign — upcoming moves against Renraku, Villiers' endgame plan, the identity of a mole, whatever.
- All three Fuchi factions remain on the runners' trail after this run, for different reasons.

## Adventure Framework: Black Operations

**The Setup:** The Yamana faction of Fuchi wants to seize control of a delta clinic hidden inside the Pueblo Corporate Council — a facility that Villiers' people have been running and which is deeply involved in experimental cybermancy research. Yamana can't use Fuchi troops (too obvious) so they're hiring outside.

**The Cover:** A very prestigious fixer offers the runners an exceptional job: penetrate a facility in a Native American nation, pose as a helicopter supply crew, seize the command center, and hold it while a mercenary unit moves in to take control. No unnecessary violence — everything in the facility is expensive. Runners are not told it's a Fuchi facility or a delta clinic.

**Event 1:** Santa Fe. Pick up Fuchi uniforms, IDs, and a rigger (Leonides, if the team lacks one). The helicopter they need to hijack is in a Fuchi hangar across the airport, guarded by armed security and some support staff. Their employers have deckers handling surveillance. The helicopter is loaded with medical supplies and tissue samples.

**Event 2:** The clinic is in the middle of nowhere — helipad, several buildings, serious defenses including rigged security, drones, magical barriers, and background count. The runners' advantage is surprise, detailed floor plans, and access codes. They need to get to the command center. Once they signal all-clear, the mercs move in.

**Event 3:** Dr. Dmitri Zalman, the head researcher and an initiated hermetic mage, is outraged and uncooperative. A Yamana-backed Fuchi executive flies in to take over. The runners are told to guard Zalman in a detention cell while things consolidate. Zalman will attempt mind control spells if given any opening.

**Climax:** While Zalman plays for time, a British vampire named **Mr. Monty Livingston** — a member of the Ordo Maximus initiatory group, which has a deal with Villiers not Yamana — arrives to sabotage the takeover. He's already alerted Villiers' people, who are on their way. The clinic descends into chaos as three-way fighting breaks out between the meres, Livingston's people, and arriving Villiers loyalists. Pueblo Security Forces surround the facility, blocking escape. The runners need to extract anyone they were ordered to extract and get out before it becomes a siege.

**GM Notes:**
- The Ordo Maximus has been teaching Fuchi's mages cybermancy techniques. This is a thread that can lead anywhere you want.
- Zalman has access to tissue samples for ritual sorcery. If runners piss him off and survive, he has options.
- The Yamana faction will reward success. Runners who end up helping Zalman will have a very short remaining lifespan.
- The "experimental tactical computer" subplot from the Adventure Ideas section (Bustin' Out) can be seeded here — maybe Zalman is working on cybermancy applications for tactical headware.

## Shorter Adventure Ideas (Flesh Out As Needed)

### Hostile Takeover
A Fuchi-owned aquasphere near Indonesia has declared independence during the chaos. Fuchi has blockaded it. The workers' council hires runners to smuggle food, supplies, and weapons through the blockade. Getting out is harder than getting in. Internal politics within the council complicate everything — some members want to negotiate with another faction, the hardliners want to stay independent.

### A Gift for the Bride
Yamana wants to impress his future wife Mitsuko Shiawase, who likes large reptiles. He wants a king cobra no less than eight meters long, captured alive in the Philippine jungles. By the time runners close in on a suitable specimen, Mitsuhama's Parashield division has already captured it. The runners need to extract a living eight-meter cobra from a corporate compound full of other Awakened creatures and get it out of the Philippines. Yakuza complications on the way back to Japan.

### Bustin' Out
A group calling themselves Corpbusters wants runners to break their leader out of a Fuchi prison before it changes hands to Novatech or gets dissolved. The catch: their leader has been implanted with an experimental tactical computer, and most of his "comrades" have similar exotic headware implants. None of them are actually Corpbusters — they're Saeder-Krupp acquisition targets. The runners have been hired by Lofwyr's people to extract research subjects under a false flag.

## How This Track Ends

By late 2060:
- **Novatech** is a legitimate AAA megacorp based in Boston, with Richard Villiers at the top and Miles Lanier running security. Samantha Villiers runs the Seattle operation. Darren Villiers runs covert ops. Novatech is a potential long-term employer.
- **Fuchi** no longer exists. Its remnants are split between Shiawase (Yamana's piece) and Renraku (Nakatomi's piece).
- **Nakatomi** is on Renraku's board and pushing for revenge against Villiers. Any runners who worked for Villiers have made a permanent enemy.
- **Yamana** is on Shiawase's board and largely content to retire. The Shiawase acquisition of Fuchi's European assets gives Shiawase a major new foothold.

Runners who worked for the Villiers faction throughout this track are now Novatech assets — potentially valuable, potentially exposed if Nakatomi ever gets his revenge campaign running.
