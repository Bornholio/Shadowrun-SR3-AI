# Blood in the Boardroom — Track 2: Neck and Neck (Renraku)

## What's Happening and Why

Renraku Computer Systems has been quietly keeping a secret that explains its sudden technological leap: they made a deal with **Leonardo**, an elf of extraordinary and apparently unique talent, who was blackmailing every AAA megacorp simultaneously by demonstrating the ability to penetrate their most secure systems at will. Rather than pay the blackmail, Renraku cut a deal — funding for Leonardo's mysterious "Great Work" in exchange for exclusive access to his capabilities.

The results were extraordinary. Leonardo's technology gave Renraku hardware and software a decade ahead of anything the competition could produce. Their IC became nearly impenetrable. Their deckers gained tools no one else had. Renraku surged past Mitsuhama and began closing on Fuchi for second place in the megacorp rankings.

Then it all collapsed. Fuchi brought the Corporate Court down on Renraku over the Lanier espionage affair. Lanier resigned from Renraku's board, sold his stock to the Zurich-Orbital bank, and walked out. Leonardo disappeared — and most of his technology disappeared with it, leaving Renraku's own engineers trying to reverse-engineer products they'd been selling for a year. The arcology's security system went haywire in December 2059. CEO Aneki departed for Tibet in February 2060. A Court rep died in a bombing in New Delhi. The RTG attack hit Seattle in March. By the time Nakatomi bought the Lanier stock block and joined Renraku's board in April 2060, Renraku was battered but still standing — and now had a partner who shared their hatred of Richard Villiers.

---

## Renraku's Position for Runners

Renraku was already generating substantial shadow work before the crisis (their surge required constant acquisition of competitor research). During the crisis, they generate even more — patching holes in their Matrix defenses, trying to find out what's missing from their databases, investigating the arcology shutdown, hunting for Leonardo. After Nakatomi's arrival, they'll start building toward something against Villiers and Novatech.

Renraku's shadow operations have a different flavor than most corps — they're disciplined, organized, and professional, but they're also more paranoid than usual right now. Runners who work for Renraku will be vetted carefully and may find themselves subject to loyalty tests they don't know they're taking.

---

## Renraku Corporate History (For Context)

The core of Renraku was originally **Keruba International**, a Slovenian military manufacturer that diversified into telecom and computer hardware. Its CEO died in the Crash of '29 and **Inazo Aneki** — then a young corporate raider — swooped in, bought the wreckage, and transformed it into Renraku. Aneki spent years managing the company poorly (corruption ran rampant throughout the 2030s) before a near-coup scared him into becoming the leader he should have been. By 2045, Renraku was a stable, if predictable, megacorp. Then Leonardo showed up and made it something more.

## Key NPCs

### Inazo Aneki
**Role:** Renraku CEO, absent from February 2060

Seventy years old, patient, intelligent, genuinely devoted to his company and its people. Never married — Renraku is his family. He pulled the company back from the brink twice (once from corruption in the 2030s, once from the Fuchi/Lanier crisis). He's taking his leave of absence to use the Seal of the Green Gloves Dunkelzahn left him — an entry token for Tibet. Whatever he's going to do there, he thinks Renraku can survive without him for a while. He's probably wrong about that.

**For runners:** Aneki is too remote and too careful to interact with directly. He's a background figure whose absence creates power vacuums.

### Yukiako Watanabe
**Role:** Renraku chairman of the board

One of the most powerful women in Japanese megacorporate history, which says everything about how capable she is. Runs the fractious Renraku board with an iron hand. Generally agrees with Aneki on major decisions — their alignment is what kept Renraku from the same kind of internal warfare that destroyed Fuchi. With Aneki gone, she's increasingly the real power.

**For runners:** Watanabe is suspicious of everyone and operates through layers of intermediaries. She's the architect of the "Prove It To Me" scenario, using runners to test Lanier's loyalty without exposing herself.

### Haruhiko Nakada
**Role:** Renraku COO, acting CEO during Aneki's absence

"Harry" to everyone, even in Chiba. Cheerful, outgoing, and fundamentally ruthless underneath. He wants the CEO job permanently and knows this leave of absence is partly a test. He won't do anything reckless to grab power, but he's paying close attention to every decision and positioning himself carefully.

### Miles Lanier
See Track 1 for full profile. During his time at Renraku, he's a board member feeding inside information back to Villiers while appearing to help Renraku. After he leaves, he goes straight to Novatech.

### Sherman Huang
**Role:** Head of Renraku America, executive director of the Seattle arcology

An eccentric who gets underestimated constantly. He was deeply personally invested in the arcology project and is genuinely puzzled by the shutdown — he believes he should be able to fix it and keeps returning to Seattle to test theories. He's a close friend of Aneki's, politically aware despite appearances, and extremely perceptive. He's currently running Renraku America from New York but keeps one eye on Seattle.

### Hiroshi Ushida
**Role:** Arcology director (presumably still inside)

He was sent to rescue the arcology project from dysfunctional management in 2055 and succeeded. He was expecting to move on to another project; instead he got stuck as the facility's permanent director. He was inside the arcology when the systems went haywire in December 2059. He hasn't been heard from since. Huang is counting on him.

### Shikei Nakatomi
**Role:** Former head of Fuchi Asia, Renraku board member from April 2060

Arrives at Renraku as a major investor with a personal grudge against Villiers that makes corporate rivalry look mild. Known as the "Business Butcher." His arrival gives Renraku an infusion of cash and a new direction — anti-Villiers, anti-Novatech, aggressive. Any runner associated with Novatech is now on his list.

### Transys Neuronet (Supporting Corp)
A UK-based AA corp backed by the Hildebrandt-Kleinfort-Bernal financial empire and a Welsh dragon named Celedyr. They were briefly close to Renraku's tech level before Leonardo's contributions blew them out of the water. They've pivoted to researching Matrix connections for paranormal creatures (including dragons). They're opportunistic during the crisis — sending agents to grab whatever tech they can while Renraku is distracted.

## The Arcology Situation

The Renraku arcology in Seattle closes in December 2059 after its security systems go haywire. The official explanation is "computer problems." The reality is much worse and not detailed in this sourcebook — Renraku has lost control of its own arcology. No runner team, no megacorp intelligence operation, no one has been able to successfully penetrate the physical defenses to find out what's happening inside.

**For runners:** The arcology is a source of mounting dread throughout this track. Rumors are getting darker. If runners have contacts who work in the arcology, those contacts have gone silent. The March 2060 Matrix attack on Seattle's RTG may be connected. Huang keeps coming back to test theories. Ushida is presumably still in there. Whatever is happening inside that building is not good.

Use the arcology as atmosphere and pressure. Don't answer questions about it — that's what the Renraku Arcology: Shutdown sourcebook is for.

## Adventure Framework: This Is Your Brain On Otaku

**Background:** Renraku has been running an undercover operation to learn about the otaku — deckers who connect to the Matrix without hardware. One of their agents, **Diana Peng** (age 20, presented as a data entry clerk, actually a trained security operative), infiltrated an otaku tribe in Seattle called the Beamwalkers. She went through the Deep Resonance as her initiation. Something went wrong. Renraku found her on a street corner, physically unharmed, but severely brain-damaged. She's now in a small private clinic in Tacoma, checked in under the false name "Olivia Tang," her expenses paid by a fake "Roger Tang."

**The Job As Presented:** A middle-aged man named "Stuart Peng" contacts the runners, claiming Diana is his daughter and that a cult called the Beamwalkers is paying for her care. He can't legally remove her because he can't prove his relationship to "Olivia Tang." He wants the runners to extract her illegally and get evidence he can use against the cult.

**Who "Stuart Peng" Actually Is:** A corporate expediter for the Nakatomi faction of Fuchi. The Nakatomis think Renraku's otaku investigation was connected to Villiers' operations and want Diana to find out more. The real Stuart Peng is a mid-level Renraku executive who believes his daughter is on an assignment with the Red Samurai.

**What Diana Is:** A Renraku security agent with a datajack and a high-quality retractable spur she doesn't seem to know she has. Her brain damage impairs her ability to relate to reality — she communicates mostly in confused fragments, and she becomes frantic near cyberdecks, screaming "Back! Back! Back!" Renraku has a tracking signal implanted with her headware memory and will know where she is.

**Event 1 — The Clinic:**
The Stasky Institute is a neurological clinic in Tacoma. Security is oriented toward keeping patients in, not intruders out. Diana is on the second floor in a room with a single narrow window. A Beamwalker otaku is watching the clinic's Matrix systems for any sign of who's looking for Diana — and will trip the alarm as the runners leave to make them easier to track.

**Event 2 — The Transfer:**
"Stuart Peng" wants to meet at the Villa Plaza mall. During the ride, Diana is dissociated and quiet. If she sees a cyberdeck, she goes wild trying to reach it. When runners contact the fake Johnson, Renraku's surveillance drones are already following them and ground teams are moving into position. A few miles from the drop point, two Chrysler-Nissan Patrol-1s with three Renraku company men each plus a rigger block the route and demand Diana back. They'll fight for her but use reduced-lethality tactics to avoid damaging her.

**Climax — The Mall:**
The Beamwalker otaku have also been following Diana's signal (they detected the tracker during the Deep Resonance). At the handoff, the mall's security systems are taken over by Beamwalker otaku. The tribe surrounds the group and demands Diana be released. Diana, distracted, runs toward a Hardware Etcetera window display of cyberdecks. The mall entrance opens for her — the otaku have taken over the building's systems. People inside are waiting to bring her to safety.

The runners are now stuck between:
- The Beamwalker tribe controlling the building's systems
- Fuchi guards loyal to the Nakatomi faction
- Surviving Renraku pursuers
- Mall security

Whatever side the runners choose, or if they try to take Diana somewhere else entirely, the Beamwalkers have the home field advantage.

**GM Notes:**
- Diana's condition makes her impossible to interrogate meaningfully, which is frustrating for every faction. Her value is in what's in her headware memory, not in what she can say.
- The real Stuart Peng will eventually find out his daughter has been moved and will spend serious money looking for her.
- If Diana ends up with the Beamwalkers, runners who treated them decently may find Matrix problems mysteriously disappear. Runners who fought the tribe will find Matrix problems mysteriously appear.
- Several weeks later, regardless of the run's outcome, a runner may spot Diana entering the Renraku arcology's mall — and then she seems to simply disappear inside.

## Adventure Framework: Prove It To Me

**Background:** Yukiako Watanabe doesn't trust Miles Lanier. He's been on Renraku's board for months, his inside information on Fuchi has been valuable, but she despises his personality and suspects his motives. Before he gets too embedded in Renraku's operations, she wants his loyalty tested — through a series of covert operations designed to catch him acting against Renraku's interests.

**The Job:** Runners are hired through the Matrix by a faceless Mr. Johnson offering good nuyen for "diverse activities" in Boston. The Johnson has heavyweight decker support. He says they're "testing new security measures." He's not lying exactly — just omitting that the security measure being tested is a person.

**Event 1 — Surveillance:**
The runners set up in Boston and begin watching Lanier. His residence, office, LTG numbers, schedule — everything. The Johnson wants everything tapped and documented. Lanier's life is not immediately exciting (he's being careful), but he talks to interesting people. He's also a former security professional who knows how surveillance works, so this is not easy work. Whatever the runners find, the Johnson (Watanabe's agent) is never satisfied — she's right that Lanier is being subtle.

**Event 2 — The Fake Hit:**
Watanabe decides to act boldly. She passes the runners information about a marine biology forum and gala at the New England Aquarium that Lanier will attend. Their orders: make an assassination attempt on Lanier — emphasis on attempt, not success. The Johnson says it's a security test and that the attempt is not meant to succeed.

What Watanabe has also done: anonymously told Fuchi (Villiers' people) about the planned hit, and placed Renraku agents throughout the area to watch for Fuchi's response. Villiers does send people to watch out for Lanier — but they spot the Renraku surveillance and smell a trap. They don't interfere with the assassination attempt. They try to track the runners afterward for questioning.

**Event 3 — The Frame:**
Watanabe tries to frame Lanier for espionage. The runners' decker poses as a disenchanted Renraku employee selling classified data — specifically partial codework for a new piece of Renraku Tar Pit IC. The bait gets posted to Matrix shadowboards and sent to a suspected Lanier contact.

A decker named **Phaze** responds and arranges a Matrix meeting at Rox Rootz, a Boston decker bar. The runners' job is to sell the data, then trace Phaze to his physical location for capture and use as evidence against Lanier. Watanabe provides one-shot Rating 10 scanner and tracking programs. She'll assign two Renraku combat deckers as backup if requested.

Phaze takes the data, notices it's incomplete, and will fight or run if threatened. If traced, he's decking from an illegal hookup in Boston's Rox area. When runners arrive at his location, heavily armed corporate types extract him — Lanier's people, who were watching.

**Climax — The Mole:**
Watanabe's final gambit: a captured Fuchi mole from inside Renraku. She has the mole transported to Boston and uses runners as babysitters, planning to leak news of the mole's capture to Lanier's network and watch for an extraction attempt. Lanier's security team has been tracking the runners by now. While some runners go to pick up the prisoner, Lanier's people capture the others using non-lethal weaponry.

Now the group is split: some with the Fuchi mole, some captured. Lanier will arrange a trade. Red Samurai arrive to complicate the exchange. Throughout all of this, Lanier never personally appears — everything is handled through people who will admit working for Lanier only under extreme duress, and who know nothing of his actual loyalties.

**Resolution:** No evidence of Lanier's continuing Fuchi connection is obtainable from this adventure. Everything is inconclusive. Watanabe grudgingly gives him more access. The runners have been used, and their Mr. Johnson's faceless UMS icon isn't enough to trace back to anyone. If they manage to track the Johnson and try anything, Red Samurai show up.

## Adventure Framework: What Does A Ten-Thousand-Year-Old Dragon Get?

**Background:** Leonardo has disappeared, and the great dragon **Hestaby** wants to know why. She recently formed an alliance with him and is concerned about certain plans he was pursuing for his Great Work. She's investigated his destroyed sanctuary in Africa and found nothing — now she's hiring runners to pick up whatever threads Renraku still has.

**The Job:** Runners are approached by a California shaman named Breeze — neo-hippie presentation, serious resources. He wants them to investigate Leonardo's disappearance, starting at a location in Iran. Suborbital tickets to Baghdad, helicopter from there to the town of Ahvaz. Excellent forged IDs provided.

**Event 1 — Ahvaz:**
The observatory Leonardo used is now occupied by Abu Khalid and his Blacksand Revolutionaries — a force that recently drove off a Renraku military unit from the area. The locals recognize pictures of Leonardo and say he lived in the observatory until "the sirrurg came" (by which they mean a dragon) and destroyed it. Runners who piece this together realize a dragon demolished Leonardo's sanctuary.

**Event 2 — Inside the Observatory:**
Abu Khalid has set up a command post in the burned-out tower. Almost everything inside has been destroyed. However: there are German-speaking "military advisers" present who are actually Saeder-Krupp officers. After the observatory was demolished, Lofwyr hired Abu Khalid's group to secure the site and destroy anything remaining. The SK officers are here to make sure that's been done — and to discourage investigation through torture. Some Renraku computer equipment has been salvaged, but runners won't find useful data about Leonardo. The only clues are a pointer to a Renraku matrix host, a specific filename ("MU Zeta Five"), and an optical chip with unbreakable encryption.

**Event 3 — The Renraku Host:**
Accessible from anywhere with satellite uplink (hundreds of miles from Ahvaz). The host benchmarks around Red-5. No references to Leonardo exist — they've been systematically erased. A successful Dump Log or Edit File operation reveals a sophisticated virus did it, on the same day Leonardo disappeared. Searching for "MU Zeta Five" returns a "Slave Syntax Error" and a pointer to a partially deleted personnel file: **Kura Kineda**, personal aide to COO Haruhiko Nakada. A Locate Slave operation finds an encoder system for someone with data lock headware — and the current transponder frequency for Kineda's headware implant. Whatever Leonardo told Renraku is now in Kineda's head.

**Climax — San Francisco:**
Kineda is on a suborbital landing in San Francisco shortly. Runners have just enough time to get there. They arrive to sirens, running guards, and distant autofire — someone else just grabbed Kineda and departed in a stealth helicopter. Pursuing it (steal a helicopter, hire something faster) leads to a chase to the border of Tir Tairngire near Shasta Mountain. The other team: **Tir Ghosts** sent by Ehran the Scribe, with at least one physical adept and one initiated mage. They're racing for the Tir border and calling for backup.

If Breeze has been kept informed, Hestaby's Shamans of Shasta may arrive to provide just enough force to turn the Tir team back — framing it as preventing a Tir incursion rather than revealing Hestaby's hand.

**Resolution:** Breeze takes custody of Kineda. He also has what's in Kineda's head — but he still needs codes to access the data-locked information. Renraku will try to activate Kineda's cortex bomb to eliminate the security threat. Ehran and the Tir are interested in the data and may pursue the runners. The encrypted optical chip remains a mystery.

**GM Notes:**
- Lofwyr's involvement in destroying Leonardo's sanctuary is a major thread. What does Saeder-Krupp know about the Great Work? Why did Lofwyr want it destroyed?
- Hestaby and Ehran being on opposite sides here is a dragon politics hook that can go anywhere.
- Leonardo himself is not found. He may or may not be dead.

## Adventure Framework: This Hurts Me More Than It Hurts You

**The Setup:** Jacob Kitt owns HyperSense, a small simsense production company that Renraku bailed out two years ago in exchange for a 51% controlling interest and use as a testbed for Leonardo-derived tech. The research has secretly paid off — HyperSense has made genuine breakthroughs it hasn't reported to Renraku. Kitt wants to regain control of his company by faking a corporate hit, convincing Renraku to divest from a company that looks like a liability.

He's borrowed money from local yakuza to fund a secret new production facility, where he's producing BTL chips — he owes them a favor. Now he wants runners to burn down the original HyperSense building so Renraku will walk away from it.

**The Job:** A twitchy man presents himself as a Mr. Johnson and offers good nuyen to hit a low-security facility — go in, burn the main building, minimal violence, specific target: HyperSense. He tells runners how to bypass the security. He doesn't want to negotiate much. Something is clearly off.

**Legwork:** Almost everything Kitt told them checks out. Buried in the data is Kitt's name — he owns HyperSense. Twenty-two of Fuchi Americas' recently-lost subsidiaries were taken by Cambridge Holdings — connected to Villiers. A large cash transfer to Cambridge Holdings from Silveri Investments (Villiers). Cambridge Holdings bought Fuchi Americas subsidiaries. Villiers bought Cambridge. His own companies, now his personally. Digging further: a new simsense facility in another part of town, protected by yakuza, clearly just operational. Connecting HyperSense's breakthroughs to a new BTL strain called "Flashers" on the street — distributed by the yaks.

**Yakuza Thread:** Any investigation into HyperSense attracts yakuza attention fast. First a polite warning. Then one runner gets hurt badly as an example. Then escalating violence. The oyabun doesn't know Renraku has a controlling interest — when he finds out, he'll be furious at Kitt for hiding it, because now he's got a megacorp as a competitor for his chip factory.

**Renraku Thread:** Renraku eventually notices interest in HyperSense and starts investigating Kitt's financial irregularities. They beef up security at the facility dramatically (right before the runners try the job). When they find the secret facility, they'll move to seize it — triggering a battle with the yakuza that may destroy everything.

**Climax:** Multiple parties converging: the runners trying to do their job, yakuza protecting their investment, Renraku moving to secure theirs, possibly Triads or Mafia investigating the Flashers BTL supply chain. Kitt is trying to avoid everyone. The secret facility is likely to be destroyed in the crossfire.

## Shorter Adventure Ideas

### Unwilling Transfer
Nakatomi wants to extract Filipino marine biologists from a Fuchi Asia facility in the Philippines and bring them to Renraku's Underwater Living Project. The runners are hired for the extraction. The biologist doesn't want to go — his company was only bought by Fuchi months ago and he's heard bad things about the Renraku ULP director. The moment he gets any slack, he runs to his contacts in the rebel-pirate Huk Network. Renraku will blame the runners if he escapes.

### The Squeeze
Using Lanier's inside information, Renraku is buying out WasserKraft — a Germany-based corp researching water purification through magical and technological means, currently held by the Yamana faction. They need the last major shareholder, an elf named Deiter Arkona, to sell. He's told everyone to frag off. Runners are hired to apply pressure however they see fit. Arkona has connections to the rulers of the elven Duchy of Pomorya and a policlub called Klabauterbund with ties to GreenWar and North Sea pirates. If cornered, he'll sell to Fuchi just to spite Renraku.

### Public Secrets
Renraku databases throughout the Seattle RTG are spontaneously revealing internal classified files — secret holdings, financial records, illegal practices, personnel files. A frightened Renraku sysop contacts runners for outside help. When they investigate, they find an icon that appears to be the source — a semi-autonomous knowbot inside a partially-crashed Fuchi host system. A mystery decker has been using the abandoned system and found the dormant Fuchi corp-war weapon, activating it with different objectives. The decker is an elite Aztechnology operative trying to rewrite the SK's mission parameters.

### Transys Neuromess
Transys Neuronet hires a runner team to steal from both Fuchi and Renraku while both are distracted. They provide forged credentials tagging runners as top Fuchi Asia scientists. The runners loot freely — until a Fuchi Pan-Europa military force descends to claim the facility for Yamana. Chaos. A Renraku strike team then extracts the "valuable Fuchi scientists" and takes them to a Renraku facility. The runners are now behind Renraku security with cover identities that are already eroding.

## How This Track Ends

By late 2060:
- **Renraku** is wounded but surviving. The arcology remains closed. Aneki is in Tibet. Nakada is running the company. Nakatomi has arrived with money and anti-Villiers energy.
- **Leonardo** is still missing. His Great Work remains unknown. His technology is mostly gone — Renraku's own engineers are reverse-engineering products they don't fully understand.
- **Fuchi's remnants** (Nakatomi's share) are integrating into Renraku. This gives Renraku a significant boost in Asian operations but also means Nakatomi's personal vendetta is now a Renraku corporate priority.
- **Transys Neuronet** is still out there, opportunistically grabbing research, backed by a Welsh dragon.
- **Hestaby** knows something about Leonardo. Ehran the Scribe also wants that information. Neither is done with Seattle.
