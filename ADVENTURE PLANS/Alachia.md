# Alachia (Earthdawn) — compressed historical notes

## Identity and role
Alachia is the Queen of the Elven Court of **Wyrm Wood / Blood Wood**, one of the central elven rulers in Earthdawn history. In the setting’s broad historical arc, she is the key political figure behind the decisions that transformed Wyrm Wood into Blood Wood and reshaped the legitimacy of the Elven Court among other elven peoples.

## Core historical arc

### 1) Ruler of Wyrm Wood before and during the Scourge
Before the transformation of the forest, Alachia ruled the Elven Court in **Wyrm Wood**, which was a center of elven power and culture. Later summaries consistently identify her as the queen whose decisions defined the Wood’s fate.

### 2) Rejection of Theran aid and the Separation
As the threat of the Scourge intensified, the elven peoples were divided over whether to accept **Theran protection / aid**. Later FASA summaries state that **Alachia rejected Theran assistance** and that the court’s policy of separation and pressure on elves who accepted Theran help widened the split among the elven nations.

**Historical significance:** this is one of the clearest direct actions attributed to Alachia in canon. It is a political and ideological choice, not just a passive background fact.

### 3) The crisis of Wyrm Wood’s defense
When the magical protection of the Wood proved insufficient against the Horrors, Alachia’s court faced a survival crisis. Earthdawn reference material ties the next decisive step to **the Queen’s Warders** (later Blood Warders), acting in response to this emergency.

### 4) Acceptance of the Ritual of Thorns
The most famous action associated with Alachia is her acceptance of the **Ritual of Thorns**. Summaries of the ritual say that it was devised by the Queen’s Warders to protect the elves of Wyrm Wood; Alachia is described as initially resistant, but ultimately accepting it when no safer solution remained.

**Historical significance:** this is the defining act of her reign.
- It preserved the Court and many of its people.
- It transformed **Wyrm Wood into Blood Wood**.
- It permanently altered the land and the elves bound to the ritual.
- It became the foundation of later challenges to her legitimacy.

### 5) Consequences of the Ritual
Earthdawn summaries emphasize that the consequences of Alachia’s decision were immense:
- Wyrm Wood became **Blood Wood**.
- The Wood and its pattern were damaged or corrupted by the continuing ritual.
- Elves outside Blood Wood increasingly questioned or rejected the Elven Court’s authority.
- The Blood Elves’ survival came at a moral, spiritual, and political price.

### 6) Post-Scourge rule
Later Earthdawn material does **not** treat Alachia as merely a dead historical queen. It presents her as still ruling from the **Rose Throne** in Blood Wood and still attempting to uphold the primacy of the Elven Court over the broader elven world.

### 7) Ongoing political posture
In later-era Earthdawn material, Alachia is still portrayed as proud, controlling, and unwilling to publicly concede that the cost of Blood Wood’s survival may have been too high. FASA’s later overview text also presents her as expecting other elven nations to recognize Blood Wood’s example and leadership, while rivals and reformers challenge that position.

## What is actually clear in Earthdawn canon
There **is** clear information about Alachia, but most of it is clearest at the level of:
- major political decisions,
- court policy,
- the Ritual of Thorns,
- Blood Wood’s legitimacy,
- and her continued rule.

There is generally **less** openly available scene-by-scene material about her private thoughts than there is about the historical consequences of her acts.

## Practical one-paragraph summary
Alachia is the Queen of the Elven Court who ruled Wyrm Wood during the approach of the Scourge. She rejected Theran aid and upheld the court’s separationist position, then ultimately accepted the Ritual of Thorns when Wyrm Wood’s survival was at stake. That decision preserved her people but transformed Wyrm Wood into Blood Wood, corrupted the land, and permanently damaged the authority of the Elven Court in the eyes of many other elves. In later Earthdawn material she still rules Blood Wood from the Rose Throne and continues to defend the court’s primacy despite ongoing political and spiritual challenges.


Alachia is vain to a fault, but her vanity is not a weakness. She is no fool, and centuries of intrigue have taught her discretion. She recognizes flattery for the empty thing it is, and though she expects a level of deference from her subjects that blurs the line between flattery and manners, she never allows her vanity to overcome her common sense. Anyone who attempts to manipulate her through flowery compliments risks an explosion of royal temper. Alachia never lets a sycophantic remark affect any significant decision; as she sees it, her subjects should flatter her without thought of anything in return. She sometimes flies into rages when addressed with empty praises; those who have seen such outbursts say that “only the Horrors are more terrifying.” Those who hope to use her furies against her are likewise doomed to disappointment. Alachia has held power for too long to allow temper to lead her astray.

Only a handful of Name-givers dare to claim they truly know Alachia, and of those none would be wholly correct. The queen rarely confides in others lest she risk betrayal. She has pledged her heart to none who have courted her over the centuries, though she has kept several not-so-secret lovers. None of them lasted more than a few decades; those who endured longest had no political ambitions and just enough spirit to be interesting.

Her one real friend is Ethenia Carithasca, a companion of many centuries’ standing since their earliest days in the Western Kingdoms. Ethenia has given Alachia her wholehearted support and personal affection throughout the queen’s political career—but Alachia values even this friendship only so far as it helps her maintain her position. Alachia trusts Ethenia above all others because she knows Ethenia prefers to remain a power behind the throne, rather than to sit on it herself.
