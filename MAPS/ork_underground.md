## Overview — Pre-2058 State

The Seattle Underground is a network of underground passageways and excavated tunnels beneath Downtown Seattle, origin dating to the city's 19th-century street elevation. After the Night of Rage (2039) it became a refuge for orks, trolls, and dwarfs. Dwarfs departed following internal disputes in the early 2040s. By 2058 it is ork-controlled, ork-policed, and effectively a city within the city.

**Not officially recognized as a district** — that happens in 2074. In 2058 it exists in legal ambiguity. Lone Star does not patrol it. The Underground has its own city hall and private police force.

**Matrix reception:** Poor throughout. Assume significant signal degradation — no reliable wireless, hardline access only at established nodes. Singer's deck and BT network will be degraded without physical hardline tap.

**Security posture for outsiders:** The Underground is not safe for non-orks who display bigotry or whose purpose is unclear. Night of Rage memory is fresh and real. Those with legitimate business, a guide, or an ork escort are tolerated. Those without are not.

## Entrances — Known Public (22 total)

The only reliably safe entries for non-orks:

### Big Rhino Restaurant — PRIMARY
**Location:** Basement level · Seneca Street & 1st Avenue · Downtown
**Surface:** Big Rhino is a well-known ork restaurant, street-visible, operates openly
**Access:** Through the restaurant into the basement — known to anyone with Underground business
**Security rating approach:** Downtown B corridor on Seneca; the entrance itself is ork-policed below
**Notes:** Most-used entry for outsiders with legitimate purpose. Ork staff knows who goes through. Expect to be evaluated on arrival. The Big Rhino itself functions as a loose checkpoint — regulars know you or they don't.

### Lordstrung's Department Store — SECONDARY
**Location:** Basement level · 5th Avenue & Pine Street · Downtown
**Surface:** Major department store, SIN-optional, public-facing
**Access:** Through store basement
**Notes:** Busier surface traffic, more anonymous approach possible. Less ork-community atmosphere than Big Rhino — more transactional.

### Other Entrances
22 known public entrances total. The remainder range from ork-community-only to active hazards for outsiders. Additionally: ventilation shafts, private entrances, power conduits, sewer and storm drain portals — number unknown, probably not fully mapped by anyone.

**Underground geography:** Main body lies under Downtown. Extensions reach toward Tacoma, Everett, and Puyallup Barrens. Some border enclaves may not be connected to the main body.

## Underground Factions — 2058

**Ork city government:** Functional, informal by surface standards. City hall exists. Decisions made by community consensus filtered through established figures.

**Underground police (pre-Skraacha formalization):** Ork community enforcement. No uniforms. Known faces. Authority respected within the community. Will not assist surface law enforcement.

**HMHVV infected:** Present in numbers. Ghouls occasionally tolerated if they don't prey on residents. Vampires and worse exist in deeper sections. Surface runners should not go deep without purpose and escort.

**Deep level:** Feral. Free spirits, vampires, feral ghouls. Not relevant to Bronson cache location — cache is near-surface tier.

## Operational Notes for 3PR

- **Night One appearance:** Seven-foot albino elves are conspicuous anywhere. Underground is no exception. Good Reputation 2 does not apply here — they are unknown to this community. Approach via Big Rhino should be quiet, purposeful, no weapons visible.
- **Flurry:** Dual-natured. The Underground's astral texture is dense — spirits, HMHVV auras, old emotional residue from the Night of Rage. Flurry will be alert. May draw attention from anything astrally aware.
- **BT network:** Degraded. Singer's radio budget shrinks significantly underground. Assume BT subscriber list functions on short-range encrypted burst only — no reliable extended range.
- **Matrix:** Dead zones throughout. Singer decking underground requires a hardline at an established node. Cold Assist at best without one.
- **Scent:** Carpenter has Reyes's scent locked. The Underground will introduce new scent profiles fast — GT presence here would stand out to Carpenter if it exists.
