*Malice Family Campaign — SR3*
*File location: MAPS/transit_south_corridor.md*

## Overview

South rail corridor runs King Street Station (Downtown) → Lakewood (south Tacoma).
Mixed-use: commuter, freight, irregular passenger. Not a polished metro — functional with visible Rainier damage from Auburn south.
Service degrades south of Auburn. South of Puyallup City: freight-only, no passenger infrastructure.

## Station Notes

`King Street` — Primary south terminus. SIN gates functional and staffed. Off-peak scrutiny higher than peak. International District outside is A-rated; easy street blend.

`Tukwila` — C-rated. Roving patrols, no checkpoint. Good transfer point for airport corridor bus routes. Sea-Tac (AAA) is nearby but separate access entirely.

`Kent` — Security boundary zone: A (Downtown) / B (Tacoma). Station applies A in practice. Boeing worker traffic keeps it maintained. First visible ash fringe on older platform concrete.

`Auburn` — Campaign home base hub. AA, fully staffed, SIN gates active. Best-maintained station south of King Street. White River lahar bridge (post-2017 new construction) visible from east platform. Bus connections east to Covington/Maple Valley B-belt.

`Sumner` — McHugh's vicinity. Sits at the confluence of White River and Puyallup River lahar paths — most geologically exposed functional station on the line. B–C transitional, scheduled patrols only. Partially repaired roof, one platform edge never fully restored. Off-hours arrivals unremarked.

`Puyallup City` — Last station with any passenger infrastructure. Token LS presence — holds city center, nothing else. Station rebuilt from partial collapse; irregular lighting on south platform; train schedules unreliable. Beyond here: freight-only, E and Z zones. Tarislar (effective A via Knight Errant) reachable by surface from here.

`Tacoma Dome` — Tacoma transit hub, rail and bus convergence. C neighborhood but Lone Star contract is better-funded than fringe zones. Busy enough during shift changes to get lost in. I-5 south repaired post-2017 but reduced spec.

`South Tacoma / Lakewood` — Southern terminus. Fort Lewis immediately adjacent — Metroplex Guard and UCAS MP overlap with LS jurisdiction here. Unpredictable authority layering. Lakewood Center is A-rated; South Tacoma is C.

## Lahar Zone Crossings

| Crossing | Location | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| White River Bridge | Kent–Auburn / Auburn–Sumner segment | Rebuilt post-2017 | Visible new construction; speed restriction |
| Puyallup River Bypass | Sumner–Puyallup segment | Bypass — original alignment abandoned | Lower spec; freight weight limit; adds ~800m to route |
| Carbon River corridor | Puyallup fringe → Orting direction | Not rebuilt | Orting (Z) unreachable by maintained transit |
| Secondary drainage crossings | Auburn–Sumner scattered | Repaired or bypassed | Multiple small structures; some improvised |

`Sumner confluence note` — Sumner sits where White River and Puyallup River lahar paths converge. Both valleys were inundated in 2017. Most geologically exposed functional station on the line.

`Carbanado note` — SR3 district name for the Carbon River valley community (Carbonado in real geography). Sits at the upper end of the Carbon River lahar path. E-rated (Puyallup district). No transit access.

`Chokepoint implication` — In a security crackdown, the White River and Puyallup River crossings are the natural containment points for the Auburn–Sumner corridor. Few bridges, controlled approaches.
