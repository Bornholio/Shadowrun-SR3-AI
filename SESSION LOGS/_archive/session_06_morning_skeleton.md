## Setup — The Go Gang Diversion

**~0700:** A running battle between two go gangs — the Halloweeners fringe crew and a Disassembler splinter pack — erupts on 1st Ave South, spilling toward the waterfront stairs. The runner from the 0318 log (the teenager who knew the stairs) is involved — he's a Disassembler prospect who used the stairs as an escape route he'd already mapped.

This is loud, fast, and messy. Bikes, fireworks, one Halloweener with an actual flamethrower attachment on his bike. Minor property damage. A parked delivery truck gets clipped.

**LS response:** All car units divert. Both foot units pulled to cordon. Dispatch calls in Unit 5 and Unit 6 from the Belltown sector to assist. **Belltown goes thin — one car unit left on rotation, response times 20–30 minutes minimum for non-priority calls.**

This window lasts approximately **2.5 to 3 hours** before LS restores normal coverage (~0930–1000).

Singer's TC has the patrol pattern mapped. The gap is visible in real time if he's watching.

## The Three Street People

### 1. MARTA — The Woman on the Bench

**Who she is:** The woman logged at 0104 sitting on Bell Street "waiting for someone." She was — but the ride that picked her up wasn't hers. She got into the wrong vehicle. She's back now, on foot, shaken, missing her bag.

**What happened:** The vehicle that collected her was running a low-level rolling roll — two humans in a clean car, targeting SINless or near-SINless people waiting alone at odd hours. They took her bag (credstick, burner comm, personal documents) and dropped her six blocks away when she fought back. She has bruising on her left arm and a split lip.

**Where she is now:** Sitting in the alley behind the restaurant block on 1st — the same alley Unit 1 logged as a SINless camp at 0347. The other two individuals from that camp (logged as "moved on" by 0537) were her people. They scattered when they heard the go gang noise starting up. She can't reach them and doesn't know where they went.

**What she needs:** Her bag recovered, or at minimum the credstick. The burner comm has a contact she can't reconstruct from memory — someone helping her stay off LS radar. Without it she's exposed.

**The roll crew:** Two humans, male, late 20s. Clean Americar, gray. No gang affiliation — independent opportunists working the late-night gap. They've done this before. They park near a location where they can watch for isolated individuals, run the pick-up as a "ride share confusion," and strip the bag before the target understands what happened. Their current staging area is the parking structure on 1st — level three, the same level Unit 6 found a blanket and a food wrapper at 2344. The offline camera on that level wasn't a coincidence.

**Connection to log:** 0104 (woman on bench), 2344 (parking structure level three, offline camera), 0347 (SINless camp, 1st Ave alley), 0537 (camp cleared before social services).

**Complication:** The credstick has 340¥ on it. The roll crew already knows it's low value — they're deciding whether to dump the bag in the sound or sell it to a SIN broker who might find the documents useful. Window is maybe 2 hours before that decision gets made.

### 2. DARO — The Stim Crash

**Who he is:** The SINless male logged at 2211, passed out on Lenora. Independent DocWagon treated him and left. He's ambulatory now but badly off — the stim crash was on top of existing damage. He's been awake since ~0500, sitting in the alley cluster behind the Noodle House (the one Unit 7 cleared at 2231 — he came back after they left).

**What happened:** Daro is a low-level BTL chiphead running errands for a mid-tier street dealer called **Patch** — an ork male, independent, operating out of a ground-floor commercial space two blocks from Sandy Systems. Daro was supposed to deliver a package last night. He didn't make it. The stim crash took him down before he got there.

The package is still on him — taped inside his jacket lining. He doesn't know what's in it. He does know Patch is going to send someone to find him when the morning delivery window closes (~0900).

**What he needs:** Help getting clear before Patch's collector arrives. The package off his hands without him taking the blame. Or just someone to stand between him and whatever's coming.

**Patch's collector:** A human woman, **Lace** — not Singer's Fixer contact Lace, different person, same street name because it's a common one. She's not violent by preference but she carries a light pistol and has Patch's authority behind her. She'll arrive on foot around 0845–0900 if Daro hasn't checked in.

**The package:** Small, hard, wrapped in static shielding. About 200 grams. Could be a chip stash, a data module, or a piece of restricted hardware. GM discretion — the contents can be a hook or a dead end depending on session needs. If it's data: it's a personnel file from a DocWagon mid-tier administrator. If it's hardware: it's a signal booster component, illegal grade, the kind used to punch through LS jamming.

**Connection to log:** 2211 (stim crash on Lenora), 2219 (independent DocWagon unit, not DW proper — notable), 2231 (alley behind Noodle House cleared, Daro came back).

**Complication:** The independent DocWagon unit that treated him logged his biometrics. That data went somewhere. It didn't go to Lone Star — but it went to whoever runs that independent unit, and that operator sells data on the side. Daro's biometrics are now in a broker's queue. If anyone's looking for him by body profile, they'll find a hit by ~1100.

### 3. OSWIN — The Nurse Who Wasn't

**Who he is:** Not actually the nurse logged at 0308. The nurse was clean — work SIN, late shift, checked out fine. Oswin is the person who watched that stop from across the street and clocked exactly how Unit 7 ran the check: SIN scan, work tag, released in four minutes.

Oswin is SINless, mid-50s, human male, former DocWagon paramedic — lost his SIN in a creditor action three years ago after a medical negligence claim he says was fabricated. He has all the skills, none of the paperwork. He lives in the parking structure (different level than the roll crew — he's on level two, in a corner with a tarp and a camp stove).

**What happened:** This morning the go gang fight pushed through his level. He wasn't hurt but his camp was destroyed — stove knocked over, his kit scattered, his one valuable item (a well-maintained trauma kit, DocWagon-grade, worth ~800¥ on the open market) is now in the hands of one of the Disassembler prospects who grabbed it thinking it was a weapon bag before dropping it when he saw what it was.

The kit is somewhere in the waterfront stair area, where the fight moved through. The prospect dropped it on the stairs — it may still be there, may have been kicked into the water, may have been picked up by the fishermen Unit 4 logged at 0251.

**What he needs:** The trauma kit recovered. And, if anyone's paying attention, a reason to talk — because Oswin has been watching this sector for three years and he knows things. He knows the roll crew (he's seen them twice before). He knows Patch's operation and can describe Lace. He knows which DocWagon independent unit services this area (it's not standard DW — it's a gray-market operation using stolen DW livery, operated out of a garage on 3rd). He won't volunteer any of this. He'll trade it.

**Connection to log:** 0308 (nurse stop — Oswin watched it), 0347 (SINless camp, 1st alley — he knows who those people are), 2344 (parking structure — his home territory), 0251 (fishermen with pier access — one of them may have the kit).

**Complication:** Oswin's trauma kit has his old DocWagon employee serial number etched into the case. That number was flagged in a civil action three years ago. If anyone scans it, it comes back as evidence in a pending DocWagon internal investigation. Oswin doesn't know this — he thought the case was closed.

## Opponents

### The Roll Crew — Kemp and Sutter
*Independent opportunists. Not connected to any of the three street people directly — connected to all three contextually.*

**Kemp** (driver): Human male, 28. Clean SIN, corp-adjacent background, no priors. Drives clean. Makes this look legitimate enough that a fast scan won't flag him. Carries a hold-out pistol. Not a fighter.

**Sutter** (operator): Human male, 31. SIN is fake — Rating 2, good enough for a street scan. Former security contractor, let go after a weapons discharge incident that wasn't prosecuted. He's the one who scopes targets and runs the door. Carries a light pistol and has a collapsible baton. Will fight if cornered but prefers to run.

**Their vehicle:** Gray Americar, late model. Currently in the parking structure level 3 (they haven't left yet — Marta's bag is still in the back seat). One license plate, fake. The chassis will come back clean on a fast scan — it's a cloned registration from a legitimate vehicle in Renton. A deeper scan or physical inspection will show the VIN doesn't match.

**Stats (SR3 ballpark):**
- Kemp: B3 Q4 S3 C3 I4 W3 | Pistols 3 | Etiquette (Street) 3
- Sutter: B4 Q5 S4 C3 I4 W4 | Pistols 4 | Clubs 4 | Stealth 3 | Ex-security instincts — will assess threat level before engaging

**LS connection:** The offline camera on level 3 wasn't them — they benefited from it. It's been out for a week because the building super is lazy. If LS ever runs the chassis properly, Kemp and Sutter have a 48-hour window before the Renton registration owner notices the clone and files a complaint.

### Patch's Collector — "Lace"
*Not connected to the roll crew or Oswin. Connected to Daro only.*

Human female, 34. Independent contractor for Patch. Professional, not sadistic. Carries a light pistol (Ares Predator, loaded, concealed under a jacket). Has Patch's authority to collect the package or collect Daro — she's authorized to use judgment on which.

If she finds Daro in bad shape and clearly not running, she'll try to recover the package without incident. If she thinks he burned it or sold it, she escalates. If someone is visibly standing between her and Daro and that someone looks like they know what they're doing, she'll call Patch before doing anything else.

**Stats:**
- B4 Q5 S3 C4 I5 W4 | Pistols 5 | Etiquette (Street) 4 | Negotiation 4
- Combat Pool 7 | No armor — dressed to move, not fight

---

### The Disassembler Prospect — Rat
*Connected to Oswin's kit only. Peripheral.*

Human male, 17. The runner from the 0318 log — he did know the waterfront stairs, he is a Disassembler prospect, and he dropped Oswin's trauma kit on the stairs in the chaos. He's currently hiding under the pier nursing a burn on his left forearm from a Halloweener's flamethrower proximity blast.

Not dangerous to the 3PR. Scared, hurt, doesn't know who has his bike (it was still running when he bailed down the stairs). The trauma kit is about eight meters from where he's sitting, wedged against a piling. He hasn't looked at it closely.

If approached without threat: cooperative. Has information about the go gang fight and where it went. Can confirm the fishermen picked up the kit and then put it back down when they saw the DocWagon serial.

**Stats:** B3 Q5 S3 C2 I3 W3 | Brawling 2 | Stealth 3 | Streetwise 4 | Burn wound: −1 die all physical tests until treated

## Structure — Loose Scene Order

**~0700** — Go gang fight erupts. LS sector thins. Singer's TC registers the gap.

**~0715** — Marta is in the alley. She's visible from the street if anyone is moving. Her bruising is obvious. She won't approach the family — she'll watch them to figure out if they're safe.

**~0730** — Daro is in the Noodle House alley. If the go gang noise pushed anyone out of Sandy Systems for any reason, he's within two blocks. Otherwise he's findable by Search (Hearth Spirit), street awareness, or following up on last night's log.

**~0800** — Oswin emerges from the parking structure after the gang noise settles. He's methodical — he'll retrace where the fight came through before going to the waterfront stairs. He won't be visible from Sandy Systems but he's in the patrol zone.

**~0845** — Lace arrives in sector looking for Daro.

**~0930** — LS starts filtering back into Belltown. Window closes.

**~1100** — Daro's biometric hit comes back in the broker's queue. Someone gets a ping. Who bought that data and why is GM-controlled.

## GM Notes

**The DocWagon independent unit** (gray market, stolen livery, garage on 3rd) is a free-floating hook. It connects to Banshee's Corp Secretary contact at the edges — the secretary sees transport logs and crew assignments; a gray-market unit using DW livery would not appear in her logs, which means if Banshee asks her about the unit that treated Daro, the secretary will have no record of it. That absence is itself information.

**The package contents** — leave undecided until the scene develops. The DocWagon personnel file version connects to Kluger's contact Chart (Auburn DocWagon) if the name on the file is someone Chart knows. The signal booster version connects to Singer's hardware interests and is immediately useful or immediately dangerous depending on who commissioned it.

**Oswin as a contact** — he has Rating 1 potential for any character who treats him with basic dignity and recovers his kit. Medical background, three years of sector observation, gray-market DocWagon knowledge. He won't ask for a formal arrangement. He'll just start being useful and see if anyone notices.

**The roll crew** — Kemp and Sutter are small. They are not a combat encounter unless cornered. The interesting play is recovering Marta's bag without tipping them that the family exists. If the family is seen, Sutter will clock them immediately as something other than street people and will want to leave the sector entirely.

**Rat** — if treated, he owes a small debt. Disassembler prospect with local knowledge. Not a contact worth cultivating but useful once. Knows the waterfront stairs better than any LS unit.
