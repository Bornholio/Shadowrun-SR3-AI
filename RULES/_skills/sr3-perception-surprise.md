## PERCEPTION

### Procedure

Stat: **Intelligence**
Base TN: **4** — modified by table below.
One success = something is there. See Success Table for further detail.

Group Perception Test: average INT + 1 die per team member. **Do not use group tests when surprise is possible** — use individual tests only.

Complementary skill: **Stealth (Alertness)** may add dice to Perception Tests when applicable — spotting a tail, detecting camouflage, recognizing a disguised person, noticing someone is being followed.

### Perception Success Table

| Successes | Result |
|---|---|
| 1 | Something is there |
| 2 | Something is definitely there — perceiver suspects general type |
| 3 | Perceiver knows type and suspects exact nature |
| 4+ | Perceiver knows what it is — no specifics without further examination |

## VISION SYSTEM RULES

### Low-Light Vision
Amplifies available light — requires at least some light to function. Useless in true zero-light environments. Natural low-light is always operating. Characters can carry a light source to generate enough ambient light for low-light to function — even a penlight provides sufficient illumination.

### Thermographic Vision
Reads heat (infrared) emitted by targets. Natural thermographic is always operating, layered over normal vision. Cybernetic thermographic superimposes over a basic amplified low-light display.

**Ambient temperature matters:** Thermographic is most useful when target temperature differs significantly from environment. Hot engine in cold air = clearly visible. Person in tropical rainforest = nearly invisible. GM adjudicates applicable modifiers.

### Multiple Vision Systems
Characters with more than one active vision system use the **lowest applicable modifier** from all active systems. Systems may be used simultaneously. Cybernetic vision systems can be switched off individually if needed — but flash/glare effects already suffered persist even after switching systems.

## STEALTH

### Sneaking / Hiding

Stat: **Quickness** (Sneaking) or as appropriate to specialization
Test type: **Open Test**
Result: **High die rolled = TN for Perception Tests** by anyone who might notice the character.

Specializations and their applications:
- **Sneaking** — moving quietly, tailing without being spotted
- **Hiding** — physically hiding, camouflage, blending into crowds, disguise
- **Alertness** — noticing others using Stealth (Complementary Skill for Perception)
- **Theft** — sleight of hand, pickpocketing (Opposed Test vs target INT)

### Stealth as Opposed Test (Theft / Pickpocket)
Pits Stealth vs target's Intelligence. Success = item acquired undetected.

### Alertness as Complementary Skill
Adds dice to Perception Tests only when stealth-relevant: spotting a tail, detecting camouflage, recognizing disguise, noticing surveillance.

## SURPRISE

### When Surprise Applies
Any situation where characters appear unexpectedly — planned ambush or accidental encounter. Resolved per-opponent, per-character. A character may be surprised by one opponent but not another in the same encounter.

Astral barriers, foci, programs, and IC **cannot be surprised.**

### Procedure

1. All participants roll **Reaction** vs **TN 4**
2. Ambushers with delayed actions lying in wait: **TN 2** instead of TN 4
3. GM may apply additional modifiers for terrain, camouflage, circumstances
4. Compare each character's successes **individually** against each opposing character's successes
5. Resolve before any actions in the Combat Phase

### Outcome — Per Opponent

| Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| More successes than opponent | Can act against that opponent normally |
| Same or fewer successes than opponent | Cannot take any actions that directly affect, impede, or counteract that opponent. Cannot use Combat Pool to defend against that opponent's attacks. |

**Actions still permitted when surprised by a specific opponent:** Actions not directed at that opponent — dropping prone, readying a weapon (not firing it).

### Complete Surprise

If **all** opponents rolled more successes than a character: character is completely surprised. Cannot take **any** actions including Free Actions.

### TC Interaction

TC surprise pool benefit applies to the Reaction Test itself — at Rating 3, 75% of Combat Pool may be added to the Surprise Reaction Test. At Rating 4, 100%. This is the primary TC surprise benefit, not a separate roll.

### SUT Ambush Bonus

When SUT is used in preparation for an ambush:
- Adds 1 die per 2 SUT successes to Reaction/Surprise Tests for prepared side
- Applies to ambushers or ambushees if used defensively
