# SR3 Nanotech
*Source: Man & Machine. SR3 canonical.*

---

## CATEGORIES

| Category | Detection | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nano-implants | Cyberscanner | Cyberware interacting with nanite systems |
| Nanoware | Nanoscanner | Transient or free-floating nanite systems |
| Nanogear | — | Miscellaneous nanite gear |

`Nanoware does not interfere with magic.`

---

## NANOWARE TYPES

`Free-floating` :: Carried in blood/extracellular fluids. Max 2 simultaneously; third fails and existing suffer −25% per excess system.

`Transient` :: Short-term; cleared by filtration/immune systems. No tailoring required.

`Fixed` :: Segregated in capsule until triggered; becomes transient/free-floating after release.

Nanites at flesh/cyber interface: part of cyberware implant, not nanoware.

---

## ACQUIRING NANOWARE

Beta- or delta-level clinics only. Clinic finding rules: SR3 p. 140. Not recommended for starting characters. Mostly legal with permit; self-replicating nanotech heavily restricted. Unlicensed proprietary nanotech = serious corporate offense.

---

## INSTALLATION

`Fixed` :: Surgery required (cyberware implantation rules M&M p. 146).

`Free-floating` :: Injection + 1 day intensive care + 2 days regular care. Regular care reducible to 1 day with Body (6). Skip hospitalization: Body (12) or S Stun.

`Transient` :: Injection only.

`Facilitator required` :: Free-floating systems need Nanite Facilitator or suffer time-based degradation. All free-floating systems immune-tailored to host; mismatches caught during hospitalization.

---

## NANITE LOSS

| Source | Loss |
|---|---|
| Per box of Physical damage | −5% |
| Time (transient) | −10%/day |
| Time (free-floating, no facilitator) | −3%/day |
| Time (free-floating, with facilitator) | none |

| % Remaining | Effect |
|---|---|
| 100–50% | Normal |
| 49–25% | Reduced (see system) |
| 24–0% | Non-functional |

Healing removes damage but does not restore nanites. Replacement nanites may be injected without waiting to heal.

`Excessive Nanoware` :: >2 free-floating systems simultaneously: each system beyond 2 fails; each existing system suffers −25% per excess system.

---

## THERMOGRAPHIC DETECTION

Active nanites (all transient, free-floating, and activated fixed systems): −1 TN for thermographic detection or Sensor Tests against host.

---

## NANOWARE DETECTION

Requires nanoscanner. Nanoscanner catches blood-borne nanites and their containers. Does not catch nano-implant cyberware (caught by cyberscanner).

| Type | TN |
|---|---|
| Fixed (inactive) | 10 |
| Transient | 8 |
| Free-floating | 6 |
| Below 50% effectiveness | +1 |
| Below 25% effectiveness | +3 |

## NANO-IMPLANTS
*Cyberware. Standard Essence cost rules. Detected by cyberscanner.*

### Nanite Facilitator
Liver/kidney/spleen modifications. Prevents filtration of free-floating nanites. Each level of blood filtration cyberware (SR3 p. 300): −0.05 Essence cost.

### Nanite Hive
Replicates specific nanite species; maintains normal levels. Restores 5%/day to supported free-floating system. Replenishment ~every 6 months (10% of nanite price). Cyberlimb: 0.1 Essence, 1 ECU, −1 Concealability. Each hive individually numbered; generates significant heat (insulated).

### Nano-Biomonitor Systems
Advanced biomonitor: analyzes body chemistry/hormones. Includes biomonitor (free-floating component) + diagnosis processor. Subdermal display purchased separately. Both models include nanite hive.

**CrashCart Med-Alert:** Functions as Rating 6 medkit. Cyberlimb: 0.2 Essence, 2 ECU.

**Cross Biotech Guardian Angel (military):** Rating 6 medkit; −2 TN Biotech (First Aid). If unconscious: attempts revival. If Deadly wound: attempts stabilization; on failure, doubles overflow time (1 box per Body × 2 CT). Not compatible with metabolic arrester bioware. Cyberlimb: 0.3 Essence, 2 ECU.

## NANOWARE

### Bioware Regenerator
Fixed transient. Trigger: bioware implant at Moderate Stress or higher. Effect: reduces implant Stress by 1D6 ÷ 2/day. Cannot heal permanent Stress or repair failed bioware. Replenishment requires implant surgery (M&M p. 147). Reduced: 1 Stress/day.

### Carcerand-Plus
Upgraded carcerands. Programmed to trigger on specific stimulus (molecule presence or electrical charge); releases one dose into bloodstream. Trigger specified at installation. Available free-floating or transient.

### Cutters
Assassination tool. Indiscriminately attack blood cells, vessels, and organs. Damage: 9M Physical/CT. Duration: 1D6+3 consecutive CTs. Staging: per Additional Dosage rules M&M p. 107.

### Cyberware Repair Unit
Fixed transient. Trigger: cyberware implant at Moderate Stress or higher. Effect: reduces implant Stress by 1D6 ÷ 2/day. Cannot heal permanent Stress or repair failed cyberware. Replenishment requires implant surgery (M&M p. 147). Reduced: 1 Stress/day.

### Fingerprint Mappers
Rating 1–10. Rebuilds skin to match scanned fingerprint/palm print (one hand). Re-mapping time: Rating hours; +2 TN tactile Perception during mapping. Duration: 30 days. Opposed Test vs print scanner (SR3 p. 235). Reduced: rating halved (round down) if still mapping.

### Gemini
Mitsuhama. Nanites stimulate preprogrammed emotional response on trigger (hormonal, chemical, or ultrasound). Causes addiction via emotional manipulation. Override: Complex Action + Charisma (8); if successful, choose own emotion for 1 CT then Gemini returns. Willpower +1 while active. Reduced: Charisma (6) to override.

### Gremlins
Seek and destroy cyberware. Each full day: roll 1D6 = number of cyberware wound effects (M&M p. 126). Reduced: 1D6 ÷ 2 effects/day.

### Nanite Hunters
Programmed to destroy a specified nanoware system (specified at injection). Effect: −1D6% to target system/hour. Reduced: −1D6 ÷ 2%/hour.

### Nanosymbiotes
Free-floating. Aid regeneration; remove/repair damaged cells, stimulate growth, fight infection. No increased food intake. Effect: reduce base healing time by one Damage Level (e.g., Moderate uses Light base time). Stun healing: 30 minutes/box. Nanite loss: −5% per Physical damage box. Incompatible with symbiotes bioware. Reduced: revised base time × 1.5. Detection: nanoscanner TN 6; −1 TN thermographic vs host; −1 Signature while healing.

### Nanotattoos
Programmable tattoos that change shape, size, color, texture over time. Price varies. Reduced: quality and rate of change diminished.

### Nantidotes
Carry antidote for one specific toxin; activate and release at site of poisoning on detection. Available transient or free-floating. If present before or injected before Speed expires: toxin has no effect. If injected after Speed period: prevents further damage, −2 TN healing toxin damage. Reduced: if before Speed expires — Power halved, Damage Level −1; if after — same further damage reduction, −1 healing modifier.

### Oxy-Rush
Diamondoid oxygen carriers. Allow breath-holding for hours. +2 dice Athletics Tests. Pain Resistance 1 equivalent (SR3 p. 170). Incompatible with oxygenated fluorocarbons; use strongest effect only. Reduced: still extends breath; only +1 die Athletics.

### Retinal Tailors
Rating 1–10. Reworks retinal pattern to match preprogrammed print. Re-mapping time: Rating hours; eyes itch during. Duration: permanent until reapplied. Opposed Test vs print scanner (SR3 p. 235). Incompatible with cybereyes and retinal duplication. Reduced: rating halved (round down) if not complete.

### Taggants

**Etchers (transient):** Inlays magnetic metallic pattern onto bone (SIN, employee ID, etc.). Detectable by MAD and cyberware scanners; TN 3. Removal requires specially programmed etcher nanites or surgery.

**Markers (free-floating):** Blood-borne information capsules (5 Mp). Readable by nanoscanner. Used for discreet ID tagging or message delivery.

## NANOGEAR

### Monowire
Nanotech buckytube monofilament. Physical security rules: SR3 p. 234. Concealability 8 (extended/taut).

### Nanoscanner
Rating 1–10. Tests blood, saliva, or tissue for blood-borne nanites. Detected nanites can be analyzed for data, construction, and purpose. Does not detect cyberware (Nanite Hive, Facilitator, Repair Unit).

### Savior Advanced Medkit
Shiawase Biotech. Backpack/satchel. Rating 6 medkit; −1 TN Biotech (First Aid) and stabilization Body Tests. Nanite supply limited: roll 1D6 after each use — exhausted on 1–2.

### Smart Corrosives
Nanites in chemical solution; each carries corrosive programmed for one specific target substance. Unaffected materials unharmed. Application via squirtgun, spray canister, splash grenade, or Chemtech Gear (M&M p. 115). Cost does not include corrosive carried.

## MEDICAL GEAR (SR3 Core — Cross-Reference)

### Biomonitor (External)
Worn over wrist/arm/heart. Measures vitals; built-in transmitter Rating 2. Distinct from implanted biomonitor and nano-biomonitor systems.

### Medkit
Rating determines expert system (Biotech 3 base). Diagnoses, advises, can concoct antidote (Biotech vs toxin rating). Supplies exhausted on 1D6 roll of 1 after treatment.

### Stabilization Unit
Enclosed capsule (standard DocWagon). Automatically stabilizes occupant. −2 TN all healing recovery tests. Stacks with Guardian Angel (GA affects First Aid/stabilization; unit affects healing recovery).

### Slap Patches
Adhesive drug-dispensers. Dermal armor reduces effectiveness (reduce Patch Rating by Impact Armor). Blood filtration cyberware (kidney replacement, oral filters) blocks all but trauma patch. Bioware filters do not block by RAW.

**Antidote Patch:** Before Damage Resistance Test: −patch rating Power. Deadly damage + patch: +rating dice stabilization tests. Multiple patches: no additional effect.

**Stimulant Patch:** Reduces Stun Condition Monitor by patch rating boxes. Magicians: Magic Test vs TN equal to rating or Essence loss (as Deadly wound). Effects wear off 2D6 minutes; reduced damage +1 box restored.

**Tranq Patch:** Opposed: patient Body vs patch rating. Each net patch success: +1 Stun box.

**Trauma Patch:** Place over heart. Second chance against death. SR3 p. 125.

*House rule: attack-vector patches (tranq) receive Nanosymbiote and Toxin Extractor Power reduction bonuses against 3PR characters.*
