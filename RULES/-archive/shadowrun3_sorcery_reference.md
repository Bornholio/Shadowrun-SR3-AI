# Shadowrun 3rd Edition Sorcery Reference

## Sorcery Overview

**Sorcery** is the magical skill of shaping mana to create specific effects, known as spells.

It can be used for:

- **Spellcasting**
- **Spell Defense**
- **Dispelling**

Mages control mana through specific formulas and force of will. Shamans cast more intuitively, improvising from the moment and their understanding of it. In rules terms, both traditions use the same spellcasting rules.

A magician does not need gestures, chanting, dancing, or similar theatrics to cast a spell. Those are only window dressing.

---

# Spells

Spells differ by:

- **Type**
- **Category**
- **Duration**
- **Range**
- **Force**
- **Drain Level**
- **Effect**

Spells intended to inflict damage also have **Damage Codes**, like weapons.

---

## Type

### Physical

Physical spells affect the physical properties of a target.

- Only physical spells affect **non-living objects**

### Mana

Mana spells affect mental, spiritual, or magical things, such as:

- spirits
- emotions
- thoughts
- life force
- other magical phenomena

- Only mana spells affect **astral forms**

---

## Category

A spell’s category determines what kind of effect it is intended to produce.

There are five spell categories:

- **Combat**
- **Detection**
- **Health**
- **Illusion**
- **Manipulation**

### Combat Spells

Combat spells are fast, direct, and violent.

- They channel magical energy directly into the target
- They damage from within
- They ignore armor and other non-magical external protection

### Detection Spells

Detection spells enhance perception.

They can:

- extend sight or hearing
- grant extra sensory perception
- detect beings
- detect magic
- detect life
- detect enemies

### Health Spells

Health spells affect the condition and performance of a living body.

They can:

- heal injuries
- treat damage
- purge poisons or toxins
- raise or lower Attributes

### Illusion Spells

Illusion spells fool the senses.

They can:

- deceive observers
- make things invisible
- confuse senses
- create simple or complex entertainments

### Manipulation Spells

Manipulation spells transform, control, animate, create, or channel matter and energy.

They can:

- control emotions or actions
- move objects
- shape matter
- create or direct light, fire, and similar energies
- alter a target’s form or appearance

These are often among the most powerful and exhausting spells to cast.

---

## Range

Most spells are **Line of Sight (LOS)**.

- If the caster can see the target, regardless of distance, the target can be affected

Some spells, especially many **Health** spells, require **touch**.

### Touch Spells

To affect an unwilling target with a touch spell:

- the caster makes a normal **Unarmed Combat Test** as part of the same **Complex Action**
- the target number for this unarmed attack gets a **–1 modifier**
- the caster only needs **1 net success** to touch the target

---

## Duration

Spells have one of three durations:

- **Instant**
- **Sustained**
- **Permanent**

### Instant

Instant spells take effect and vanish in the same action.

Their effects may still last afterward.

Examples usually include:

- combat spells
- some manipulation spells

### Sustained

Sustained spells remain active as long as the caster keeps concentrating.

- sustaining a spell requires no action
- if concentration is lost, the spell ends
- an unconscious or sleeping caster cannot sustain spells
- the caster may voluntarily drop a sustained spell as a **Free Action**

If a caster takes damage while sustaining a spell, they must make a **Sorcery Test** against:

- the spell’s **Force**
- plus any **injury modifiers**

If the test fails, the caster stops sustaining the spell.

#### Sustaining Penalties

Each sustained spell gives:

- **+2 target modifier to all tests**
- including **Drain Resistance Tests**
- but **not** normal Damage Resistance Tests

A character may sustain a number of spells equal to their **Sorcery rating**.

To perform an **Exclusive Action**, the caster must drop all sustained spells.

### Permanent

Permanent spells must be sustained for a certain amount of time before becoming permanent.

If the caster stops sustaining the spell before that time expires:

- the spell ends
- its effects disappear, just like a sustained spell

The base time required depends on the spell’s **Drain Level**.

#### Reducing Permanent Spell Time

The caster may allocate successes from the Sorcery Test to reduce the time needed.

- Divide the base time by the number of successes allocated
- Those allocated successes do **not** count toward the spell’s effect

---

## Permanent Spell Base Time

| Drain Level | Time Required |
|---|---|
| Light | 5 Turns |
| Moderate | 10 Turns |
| Serious | 15 Turns |
| Deadly | 20 Turns |

---

## Force

A spell’s **Force** represents how much magical energy the caster puts into it.

Force determines:

- the spell’s power
- its potential effectiveness
- how difficult it is to resist
- how hard the Drain is to resist

### Learning and Casting Force

A spell is learned at a specific Force.

- it may be cast at a **lower** Force
- it may **not** be cast at a higher Force than learned
- minimum Force is **1**

To use a spell at a higher Force, the caster must **learn it again** at that higher Force.

Higher Force improves the spell’s possible effect, but also makes Drain more dangerous.

---

## Sorcery Drain

A spell’s **Drain Level** reflects how exhausting it is to cast.

Drain Levels use the normal damage scale:

- **L**
- **M**
- **S**
- **D**

Drain is treated like an attack on the caster from the mana being channeled.

### Stun vs Physical Drain

Drain is usually **stun**, except in these cases:

- if the spell’s **Force exceeds the caster’s Magic Attribute**, Drain becomes **physical**
- if the spell is cast while **astrally projecting**, Drain is always **physical**

Physical Drain represents actual bodily injury, such as:

- muscle strain
- burst blood vessels
- burns
- similar backlash

### Resisting Drain

Drain is resisted using **Willpower** against a Drain Power based on the spell’s Force.

Each spell being sustained at that moment adds:

- **+2 to the Power of the Drain**

Successes reduce the Damage Level of the Drain.

---

# Spell Pool

Spellcasters have a special dice pool called the **Spell Pool**.

## Spell Pool Calculation

Add together:

- **Intelligence**
- **Willpower**
- **Magic**

Then:

- divide by **3**
- round **down**

## Uses of Spell Pool

Spell Pool dice may be added to:

- **Sorcery Tests for spellcasting**
- **Spell Defense**
- **Dispelling**
- **Drain Resistance Tests** using Willpower

### Limit on Spell Pool Use

No more Spell Pool dice may be used than the number of **Sorcery dice allocated to the test**.

Spell Pool refreshes like other dice pools.

---

# Learning Spells

Before a spell can be cast, it must be learned.

A spell can be learned from:

- a **teacher**
- a **spell formula**

A magician may only learn from a teacher or formula of the **same magical tradition**.

---

## Learning from a Teacher

A teacher already knows the spell and teaches it directly.

### Cost

A typical teaching price is:

**1,000 nuyen × desired Force**

plus living expenses.

A teacher may teach:

- any spell they know
- at any Force up to the maximum Force they know

If the teacher quits before learning is complete:

- all time spent is wasted
- the student must start over

---

## Learning from a Formula

A spell formula is the published pattern of a spell.

Formulas are often available from:

- talismongers
- sometimes the Matrix

### Formula Forms by Tradition

**Shamanic formulas** are often artistic or crafted items, such as:

- paintings
- carvings
- similar symbolic works

**Hermetic formulas** are usually:

- written diagrams
- complex symbolic notes
- arcane formulae

Formula cost depends on the spell and is determined separately.

For spells with variable Drain Code, use the **highest applicable Drain** to determine formula cost.

---

## Required Study Environment

### Shaman

A shaman learns a spell through:

- chanting
- dancing
- trance work
- ritual study

They must study in a **shamanic lodge** with a rating at least equal to the spell’s desired Force.

### Mage

A mage learns a spell through:

- quiet meditation
- formal study
- ritual practice

They need a **Sorcery library** with a rating at least equal to the spell’s desired Force.

---

## Learning Test

Learning a spell requires a **Sorcery Test**.

- **Magic Background** may be used as a **Complementary Skill**
- shamans receive **totem modifiers**
- mages may receive extra dice from an appropriate **elemental**
- injury modifiers and distractions still apply
- sustaining spell penalties also apply

### Learning Target Number

The target number is:

**desired Force × 2**

### Instruction Skill

If the teacher has **Instruction**, they may make an **Instruction Test** against **Target Number 4**.

- every **2 successes** adds **1 die** to the student’s Sorcery Test to learn the spell

Virtual instructors cannot be used for learning spells.

---

## Learning Time and Karma Cost

Learning a spell requires:

- a number of **days equal to desired Force**
- divided by the **number of successes**
- minimum learning time is **1 day**

Learning also costs **Good Karma equal to the desired Force**.

### Failure

If the Sorcery Test gets **no successes**:

- the learning attempt fails
- the time spent is wasted equal to the desired Force in days
- **no Karma** is lost
- the teacher still expects payment

---

# Limited Spells

A magician may learn a spell with built-in restrictions to make it easier to cast or easier to learn.

These are called **limited spells**.

A limit may reduce either:

- the spell’s effective **Force for Drain purposes**
- or the **Karma cost and learning difficulty**

The player chooses which benefit applies when the spell is learned.

A limited spell may include:

- **Fetish**
- **Exclusive**

A spell may have **both**, but each limit must serve a separate purpose.

If a caster later wants the unrestricted version, that version must be learned separately.

---

## Fetish-Limited Spells

**Modifier: –1**

Casting a fetish-limited spell requires a specific enchanted reusable object called a **fetish**.

### Fetish Rules

- fetishes are tied to a specific spell category
- a fetish for one category cannot be used for another
- when the spell is learned, it is attuned to that specific fetish
- the fetish must be **touching the magician’s body** to cast the spell

If the fetish is lost:

- a replacement must be found
- it must be attuned to the magician and spell again
- this takes a number of **hours in meditation equal to the Force of the spell**

---

## Exclusive-Limited Spells

**Modifier: –2**

An exclusive-limited spell requires so much concentration that:

- casting it is an **Exclusive Action**
- sustaining it is also treated as an **Exclusive Action**

# Quick GM Notes

## Sorcery Covers

- spellcasting
- spell defense
- dispelling

## Spell Categories

- Combat
- Detection
- Health
- Illusion
- Manipulation

## Spell Durations

- Instant
- Sustained
- Permanent

## Important Sustaining Rules

- +2 target modifier per sustained spell
- applies to almost all tests, including Drain Resistance
- does not apply to normal Damage Resistance
- sustaining limit = Sorcery rating
- exclusive actions require dropping sustained spells

## Force Reminders

- spells are learned at a specific Force
- may cast lower, never higher
- minimum Force 1
- must relearn to increase Force

## Drain Reminders

- Force > Magic = physical Drain
- astral projection spellcasting = physical Drain
- sustained spells add +2 to Drain Power

## Spell Pool Formula

**(Intelligence + Willpower + Magic) ÷ 3, round down**

## Learning Spell Basics

- target number = Force × 2
- learning time = Force days ÷ successes
- minimum 1 day
- Karma cost = Force
- no successes = failed attempt, time wasted, no Karma lost

## Limited Spell Modifiers

- Fetish: –1
- Exclusive: –2
