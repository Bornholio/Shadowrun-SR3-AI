# SR3 CONJURING
*Source :: SR3 core rulebook*

---

## OVERVIEW

`Conjuring` is the magical skill for dealing with spirits. It covers three operations: `Summoning` (call and command spirits), `Controlling` (command uncontrolled spirits), and `Banishing` (destroy spirits by disbanding their energies).

Only a shaman can summon or control a `nature spirit`. Only a mage can summon or control an `elemental`. Either tradition can banish either type.

`Force cap` :: a character cannot summon a spirit with a `Force` greater than twice his `Magic` Attribute.

---

## NATURE SPIRITS

Nature spirits personify forces of the natural world: Land, Sea, Sky, and Man. They exist and act only within their `domain`.

### Domains

A shaman can summon a nature spirit only within the spirit's domain. A spirit cannot leave its domain or extend its powers beyond it. If a shaman leaves the domain after summoning, any unstarted services are canceled; any service already in progress continues until complete or until the next sunrise/sunset, whichever comes first.

Overlapping domains are possible (e.g., a forested mountain slope under open sky could be forest, mountain, or sky domain). A shaman can only be in one domain at a time and must choose which. A shaman can only summon one nature spirit per domain.

### Summoning Nature Spirits

`Action` :: Exclusive Complex Action

The shaman selects a `Force` for the spirit. That `Force` is the target number for a `Conjuring Test`. `Totem` modifiers and `spirit foci` dice apply. Conjuring dice may be held in reserve for the `Drain Resistance Test`.

Each success = one service owed by the spirit. Zero successes = no spirit appears.

Whether the spirit appears or not, the shaman must make a `Drain Resistance Test` (see Conjuring Drain). If Drain kills or knocks out the shaman, the spirit departs.

Nature spirits vanish at sunrise and sunset regardless of visibility. All remaining unspecified services are lost at that time.

### Nature Spirit Services

When summoned, a nature spirit appears on the astral plane and manifests as a ghostly image in the physical world. It can remain in `astral form` or be sent to stand by.

Calling a nature spirit on standby takes a Simple Action and is not Exclusive.

A nature spirit in `astral form` can only use mana powers affecting astral forms or its summoner through their magical link. To affect physical targets, it must assume `physical form`.

Continual use of a single power counts as one service. Changing the parameters of a service (e.g., extending `Concealment` to more targets) costs another service. Using combative powers against any number of foes counts as one service.

## ELEMENTALS

Elementals arise from the four hermetic elements: Earth, Air, Fire, Water. Mages only.

### Preparation

Before summoning, the mage requires:

| Requirement | Minimum Rating |
|---|---|
| `Conjuring library` | = elemental's Force |
| `Hermetic circle` (correct type) | = elemental's Force |
| Ritual materials (from talismonger) | 1,000¥ × Force |

Each element also requires a physical source:

| Element | Required Source |
|---|---|
| Fire | Bonfire, fireplace, or large brazier |
| Water | Large pool or tub; satisfied by nearby body of water |
| Air | Large quantities of burning incense |
| Earth | Man-sized heap of earth, clay, or rock; satisfied by open ground |

### Summoning Elementals

`Action` :: Exclusive Action; takes a number of hours equal to the elemental's `Force`

At the end of the ritual, make a `Conjuring Test` against a target number equal to `Force`. `Spirit foci` dice apply. Dice may be reserved for Drain.

If the ritual is interrupted, it automatically fails and the summoner still resists Drain.

On success, ritual materials are consumed and the elemental appears outside the hermetic circle. Each success = one service. Zero successes = no elemental, but all materials are consumed regardless.

The summoner must then make a `Drain Resistance Test` using `Charisma` dice against a TN equal to the desired `Force`. `Spirit foci` or allocated Conjuring dice may add to this test.

If Drain kills or knocks out the summoner, the elemental becomes uncontrolled. Make a `Force (6) Test` for the elemental: one or more successes = it flees immediately from physical and astral planes; zero successes = it attacks its former master, or rampages against the nearest living beings if the master is already dead. It does not stop until killed, banished, or controlled.

### Binding Elementals

A mage can bind a number of elementals equal to his `Charisma` simultaneously. An elemental owing services is bound and treats the mage as its master. If at the limit, one bound elemental must be released before another can be summoned.

A bound elemental departs after summoning and vanishes from physical and astral planes until called.

To call a bound elemental: Exclusive Complex Action. The elemental appears in `astral form`. Multiple elementals of the same element may be called with one Complex Action. Commanding a called elemental is a Simple Action.

A called elemental must remain within a radius equal to (summoner's `Willpower` + `Charisma` + `Magic`) × 10 meters. If forced beyond that radius, remaining services are canceled, but it still fulfills its last command.

If the elemental is present when its master is killed or knocked unconscious, it becomes uncontrolled (see above).

If twenty-four hours pass while an elemental is present in astral or physical form—even while performing a service—an additional service is consumed. This does not apply while the spirit is on standby awaiting its master's call.

The summoner may order an elemental to obey another character as master, costing one service. Mundanes cannot use elementals to cast spells but may otherwise receive the same services.

### Elemental Services

Elementals can only perform one service at a time. Each service costs one of the elemental's owed services to initiate.

**Aid Sorcery**

The elemental provides extra dice for `Sorcery Tests` in one spell category, functioning like an auxiliary `Spell Pool`. Each die provided reduces the elemental's `Force` by 1. Dice must be allocated the same way as regular Sorcery dice. Only one elemental may aid a character at a time.

| Element | Spell Category |
|---|---|
| Fire | Combat spells |
| Air | Detection spells |
| Water | Illusion spells |
| Earth | Manipulation spells |
| (none) | Health spells |

When `Force` reaches 0, the elemental vanishes. It can be re-called with an Exclusive Complex Action and returns at full `Force`.

**Aid Study**

The elemental adds its `Force` in dice to help a magician learn a new spell (see Learning Spells). One service; one spirit per spell; must match the appropriate spell category.

**Physical Service**

The elemental materializes and uses its powers to a directed end (combat, movement, breaking through barriers, etc.). See Spirits and Dragons for elemental powers and Spirit Combat below.

**Spell Sustaining**

The elemental sustains a spell in the appropriate category for one `Combat Turn` per point of `Force`. When its `Force` reaches 0, it vanishes. The mage may take over sustaining before the spirit disappears.

A mage can also permanently bind an elemental to a spell. This irrevocably depletes the elemental: it sustains the spell for a number of days equal to its `Force`, losing 1 point of `Force` per day (or part thereof). At `Force` 0, the spirit is consumed and gone. The mage may end early by releasing the elemental, but the spirit is still freed—it cannot be recalled.

If the spirit is killed or banished while sustaining, the spell ends.

**Remote Service**

The mage sends the elemental beyond its normal radius to perform a single task. `Remote Service` forfeits all other remaining services. The elemental pursues its orders single-mindedly and cannot be halted—even by its summoner—once dispatched. When the service is complete, the elemental is freed and vanishes. Remote Service may be performed in astral or physical form, switching between as needed.

## CONJURING DRAIN

After summoning, the summoner makes a `Drain Resistance Test` using `Charisma` dice (adjusted by `totem` modifiers and `spirit foci`) against a TN equal to the spirit's `Force`. Conjuring dice may be allocated from the Conjuring Test to assist.

Drain Level is determined by comparing spirit `Force` to summoner `Charisma`:

| Spirit's Force | Drain Level |
|---|---|
| ½ summoner's Charisma or less | L Stun |
| Summoner's Charisma or less | M Stun |
| Greater than summoner's Charisma | S Stun |
| Greater than 1.5 × summoner's Charisma | D Stun |

If spirit `Force` exceeds the summoner's `Magic` Attribute, Drain causes Physical damage instead of Stun.

## ASTRAL CONJURING

All uses of `Conjuring` are Exclusive and cannot be performed while astrally projecting. A magician may summon a spirit, then astrally project and command it from the astral plane.

---

## SPIRIT FORMS

Spirits have two forms. Changing between them is a Simple Action for the spirit.

**Astral Form**

The spirit exists entirely on the astral plane. All astral Attributes equal `Force`. Can communicate with its summoner and be assensed. Cannot be harmed by physical weapons. `Weapon foci` can affect it, but the wielder must be in astral space. Only `mana spells` affect astral form spirits.

Spirits in astral form can manifest in the same manner as projecting magicians.

**Physical Form**

Spirits use the `Materialization` power to assume physical form. They function as dual beings, interacting with physical and astral planes simultaneously.

Physical spirits have `Immunity to Normal Weapons`, granting Armor equal to twice their `Force` against all attacks.

A character in melee with a physical spirit may engage it in a contest of wills instead of normal combat. The character rolls `Willpower` as a melee attack (no `Combat Pool` dice). A weapon may be used; mundane weapons do not affect base damage but the weapon's `Reach` bonus applies. On success, the attack deals `(Charisma)M Stun`. The spirit resists using only its `Force`; no `Combat Pool` and armor does not apply.

## SPIRIT COMBAT

Spirits use standard combat rules. Individual power descriptions determine dice used, target numbers, and whether `Damage Resistance` or `Spell Resistance Tests` apply.

**Initiative**

| Form | Base Reaction | Initiative Dice | Modifier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Astral | Force | 1D6 | +20 |
| Physical | Per spirit type | 1D6 | +10 |

Spirits switching from astral to physical form mid-Combat Turn are ineligible for another action for two Initiative Passes. Switching from physical to astral grants no modifier unless in that form at the start of the next Combat Turn.

Spirits can only receive new commands on their summoner's or master's actions. They Delay Actions to wait for commands if they have none.

**Elemental Vulnerabilities**

Elementals are Vulnerable to attacks from their opposing element, including in astral combat. Opposing attacks increase Damage Level by one. Opposed pairs: Fire/Water, Earth/Air.

**Nature Spirit Conflicts**

When two nature spirits oppose each other within a domain, the spirit with greater `Force` wins. The winning power takes effect at a `Force` equal to the difference. Ties = no effect. Against damage-causing powers or weapons, standard combat rules apply.

**Spirit Karma Pool**

A spirit's `Karma Pool` equals the number of successes from its Conjuring Test plus one.

If a spirit is killed or disrupted, its summoner knows immediately.

## CONTROLLING

A magician may attempt to wrest control of a spirit from another magician. Shamans control nature spirits; mages control elementals.

`Action` :: Exclusive Complex Action (same as summoning)

Both magicians make a `Conjuring Test` against a TN equal to the spirit's `Force`. The spirit's current controller adds `Charisma` dice. `Totem` modifiers and `spirit foci` apply for both.

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Controller wins (more successes) | Nothing changes; challenger resists Drain as if summoning |
| Challenger wins (more successes) | Challenger gains control (1 service per net success); both resist Drain |
| No successes on either side | Both resist Drain; elemental becomes uncontrolled / nature spirit vanishes |

**Uncontrolled Elementals**

A mage encountering an uncontrolled elemental may attempt to control it. Make a test as if banishing (see below). On success, the elemental is bound and owes one service. For additional services, make a `Conjuring Test` against TN equal to `Force`; every two successes = one additional service. `Spirit foci` dice apply.

---

## BANISHING

Banishing destroys a spirit, removing it from physical and astral planes.

`Action` :: Exclusive Complex Action

The banisher rolls a `Conjuring Test` against TN equal to the spirit's `Force`. If the banisher is also the spirit's summoner, add `Charisma` dice. `Spirit foci` and `totem` modifiers apply.

The spirit rolls a `Force Test` against TN equal to the banisher's `Magic` Attribute.

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Spirit wins (more successes) | Banisher's `Magic` temporarily reduced by 1 per extra success |
| Banisher wins (more successes) | Spirit's `Force` reduced by 1 per net success |
| Tie | No change; contest continues |

The winner decides whether to continue for another round. If continuing, neither side may act until the winner's next Combat Phase.

The contest repeats until one side reaches 0, or the round's winner breaks off. Spirit reduced to `Force` 0 = destroyed. Banisher reduced to `Magic` 0 = Deadly Stun damage, unconscious; spirit goes free; magician must immediately check for permanent `Magic Loss`.

When a spirit breaks off, it typically flees.

`Magic` or `Force` reduced by banishing recovers at 1 point per hour.
