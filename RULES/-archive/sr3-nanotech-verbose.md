# SR3 Nanotech — Verbose Reference
*Source: Man & Machine (M&M). All rules SR3 canonical.*
*This file is a verbose rules reference. For 3PR character inventory, see `shared_3pr_augmentations.md`.*
*Surgery and stress rules: separate module. Drug/chemistry rules: external.*

---

## CATEGORIES

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Nano-implants | Cyberware that interacts with nanites or nanoware systems — detected by cyberscanner |
| Nanoware | Transient and free-floating nanite systems implanted in a metahuman body — detected by nanoscanner |
| Nanogear | Miscellaneous tools and gear involving nanites |

**Nanoware does not interfere with magic or the use of magic.**

---

## NANOWARE TYPES

**Free-floating nanites:** Carried in blood and extracellular fluids. Ideal for general monitoring and rapid response. Maximum two free-floating systems at one time. Third system fails to function and existing systems suffer diminished effectiveness (see Excessive Nanoware).

**Transient nanites:** Short-term presence; cleaned from blood by filtration and immune systems. Used for cyberware implantation, bladeless surgery, cosmetic modification, antidote treatments, and similar procedures.

**Fixed systems:** Segregated in protected capsule or sac until triggered by specific event or localized to one region. Once released: considered transient or free-floating until function complete.

Nanites monitoring the flesh/cyber interface are part of the cyberware implant — do not count as nanoware.

---

## ACQUIRING NANOWARE

Most street docs cannot acquire nanoware. Legal installation more involved than cyberware or bioware. Usually patented.

Finding a nanoware clinic: same rules as finding a clinic, SR3 p. 140. Only beta- or delta-level clinics can provide nanoware treatments. Not recommended for starting characters.

**Legality:** Most personal-augmentation nanoware legal with appropriate permit. Ranges from fully legal to Class B Controlled Substances (Legality Rating Y) depending on local government and system capabilities. Authorities particularly restrict self-replicating nanotech. Unlicensed possession of proprietary nanotech is a serious corporate offense.

---

## INSTALLATION

**Fixed systems:** Require surgery (cyberware implantation rules M&M p. 146 or transimplantation).

**Free-floating systems:** No surgery required. Injection + hospitalization — 1 day intensive care, then 2 days regular care (SR3 p. 240). Regular care reducible to 1 day with Body (6) Test.

Characters who skip hospitalization: Body (12) Test. Success: nanites and body fine. Failure: S Stun as body rejects nanites.

**Transient systems:** Simple liquid suspension; injection only. No surgery required.

**Modified Filtration System:** Free-floating systems require nanite facilitators (see Nano-Implants) to prevent filtration as bodily waste. Without facilitators: long-term effectiveness limited.

**Immune System Tailoring:** All free-floating nanoware customized to match host's immune markers. Mismatches detected during post-injection hospitalization. Transient systems do not require tailoring.

## NANITE LOSS

Nanite effectiveness measured as percentage. Loss cumulative. Systems function even with some loss.

| Nanite Loss | Transient | Free-floating without Facilitator | Free-floating with Facilitator |
|---|---|---|---|
| Per box of Physical damage | −5% | −5% | −5% |
| Time | −10% per day | −3% per day | — |

| Percent Remaining | Effect |
|---|---|
| 100%–50% | System works properly |
| 49%–25% | Effectiveness noticeably reduced (see specific system) |
| 24%–0% | System no longer works |

Healing removes physical damage boxes but does not restore lost nanites.

If character has free-floating system supported by nanite facilitator: no percentage loss over time.

**Excessive Nanoware:** More than two free-floating systems simultaneously → every system beyond second fails. Each existing system suffers 25% loss for each additional system introduced beyond limit of two.

**Maintenance:** Maintain nanite levels through periodic replenishment or nanite hive. Character does not have to wait to heal before injecting replacement nanites.

---

## THERMOGRAPHIC DETECTION

Active nanites produce energy output detectable as mild fever symptoms. Apply −1 to TN for any thermographic detection or Sensor Tests against character with active nanites. Applies to all transient and free-floating nanoware, and fixed nanites when activated.

*Note: This is separate from the −1 Signature effect of Nanosymbiotes specifically noted in the 3PR inventory.*

---

## NANOWARE DETECTION

Requires nanoscanner. Standard equipment in hospitals and clinics. Separate test per nanite system.

**Nanoscanner scope:** Detects blood-borne nanites and their containers — free-floating, transient, and fixed capsules in tissue. Does not detect: nano-implant cyberware (Nanite Hive, Nanite Facilitator, Cyberware Repair Unit) — these are caught by cyberscanner.

| Type of Nanoware | Target Number |
|---|---|
| Fixed (inactive) | 10 |
| Free-floating | 6 |
| Transient | 8 |
| System working below 50% | +1 |
| System not working (below 25%) | +3 |

## NANO-IMPLANTS
*All nano-implants are cyberware — follow standard cyberware rules including Essence cost. Detected by cyberscanner, not nanoscanner.*

### Nanite Facilitator
Comprises minor modifications to liver, kidneys, and spleen. Prevents nanite filtration as bodily waste.

Allows a character to possess free-floating nanoware without suffering time-based nanite degradation.

Blood filtration synergy: each level of blood filtration system cyberware (SR3 p. 300) reduces Essence cost of nanite facilitator by 0.05.

### Nanite Hive
Implanted hardware and software replicating a specific nanite species. Produces enough new nanites to maintain normal levels — replacing those excreted and replenishing those lost to injury. Incorporates monitoring and raw material gel reservoirs. Sealed unit hardwired to produce only what it was programmed for at creation.

Nanite restoration: 5% function restored per day for a supported free-floating nanoware system.
Replenishment: approximately every 6 months; costs 10% of price of the nanite breed produced.
Cyberlimb installation: 0.1 Essence, 1 ECU, −1 Concealability.

Each hive is individually numbered (often with tracer device). Generates significant heat; includes insulation to prevent immune response.

### Nano-Biomonitor Systems
Advanced versions of basic biomonitor cyberware. Analyze body chemistry and hormone levels at finer scale. Programmed with normal value ranges for user. Triggers warning display and can send emergency calls if values exceed tolerances. Both models combine biomonitor (free-floating nanoware component) and diagnosis processor. Subdermal display must be purchased separately. Both include a nanite hive to sustain the nanoware component.

**CrashCart Med-Alert** (Shiawase origin; licensed to Yamatetsu/CrashCart):
Functions as Rating 6 medkit.
Cyberlimb: 0.2 Essence, 2 ECU, no Concealability effect.

**Cross Biotech Guardian Angel** (military-grade):
Functions as Rating 6 medkit. −2 TN modifier to Biotech (First Aid) Tests. If character falls unconscious: attempts to revive. If character receives Deadly wound: attempts to stabilize. If stabilization fails: doubles time in which Overflow damage is incurred (1 box every Body × 2 Combat Turns). Not compatible with metabolic arrester bioware.
Cyberlimb: 0.3 Essence, 2 ECU, no Concealability effect.

## NANOWARE

### Bioware Regenerator
Fixed transient system. Cluster of capsules around a bioware implant. Nanites analyze implant status; if problems detected, expelled to repair tissue, stabilize chemical levels, clean dead tissue, stabilize implant environment.

Trigger: bioware implant accumulates Moderate Stress or higher.
Effect: automatically reduces implant Stress by 1D6 ÷ 2 points per day.
Cannot: heal permanent bioware Stress or repair failed bioware.
Replenishment: requires implant surgery (repair cyberware procedure, M&M p. 147).
Reduced Effect: removes only 1 Stress per day.

### Carcerand-Plus
Upgraded carcerands. Passive normal carcerands break down within narrow time period; this version can be programmed to respond to external stimuli (presence of specific molecule or electrical charge). When triggered: releases chemical into bloodstream.

Payload: equivalent of one dose of a compound.
Trigger: must be specified before installation.
Availability: free-floating or transient.
Duration: circulates until trigger condition met or purged.

### Cutters
Known as "juicers." Assassination tool. Once injected: distribute throughout body and enter supercharged power mode. Indiscriminately cut through blood cells, blood vessels, and organ tissues. Active on internal energy reserves for at most 30 seconds.

Damage: 9M Physical per Combat Turn.
Duration: 1D6 + 3 consecutive Combat Turns.
Staging: per Additional Dosage rules M&M p. 107.

### Cyberware Repair Unit
Fixed transient system. Cluster of capsules connected to a cyberware implant's fault-checking circuitry. If major malfunction or damage detected: nanites expelled to rejoin neural connections, seal fractures, repair circuitry, fix minor damage. Cannot discriminate between individual wires and neurons. Cannot restore function after severe trauma (e.g., lost limbs).

Trigger: cyberware implant accumulates Moderate Stress or higher.
Effect: automatically reduces implant Stress by 1D6 ÷ 2 points per day.
Cannot: heal permanent Stress or repair failed cyberware.
Replenishment: requires implant surgery (repair cyberware procedure, M&M p. 147).
Reduced Effect: reduces only 1 Stress per day.

### Fingerprint Mappers
Programs nanites with scanned fingerprint or palm print; rebuilds skin layers to match. Affects one hand only.

Rating: 1–10; used in Opposed Test with print scanner (SR3 p. 235).
Re-mapping time: Rating hours; hands tingle and itch during procedure.
During re-mapping: +2 TN modifier all tactile-based Perception Tests.
Duration: 30 days; natural prints grow back after that.
Reduced Effect: if re-mapping still in progress, rating reduced by half (round down).

### Gemini
Nanotech-based drug (Mitsuhama). Nanites traverse body until receiving preprogrammed trigger signal (hormonal levels, chemical presence, or ultrasound signal), then stimulate neural responses to produce preprogrammed emotional response. Causes addiction through emotional manipulation.

Trigger: preprogrammed; must be specified.
Effect: character must play out programmed emotion for duration of nanites' existence.
Overriding emotion: Complex Action + Charisma (8) Test; if successful, can display desired emotion for one Combat Turn, then Gemini-controlled state returns.
Willpower bonus: +1 while under influence.
Reduced Effect: Charisma (6) Test to display another emotion.

### Gremlins
Nanites programmed to seek out and sabotage cyberware within the host. Target neural connections, fiber optics, superconductive pathways, microelectronics, and implant casings.

Effect: each full day active, roll 1D6 — result = number of cyberware wound effects suffered (M&M p. 126).
Reduced Effect: 1D6 ÷ 2 wound effects per day.

### Nanite Hunters
Nanites programmed to hunt and destroy a specified nanoware system.

Targeting: must be programmed for specific nanoware system before injection.
Effect: reduce targeted nanite system by 1D6% per hour.
Reduced Effect: 1D6 ÷ 2% per hour.

### Nanosymbiotes
Free-floating nanites aiding body's regenerative capabilities. Remove and repair damaged cells, stimulate cell growth, fight infection. Do not require increased food intake. Susceptible to normal nanite degradation through blood loss.

Effect: reduce base healing time by one Damage Level (e.g., Moderate Physical uses Light base time of 24 hours instead of 10 days).
Optional healing rules: calculate base time as if wound modifier were one level lower.
Stun healing: reduces base time for Stun damage boxes to 30 minutes each.
Nanite loss: −5% per box of physical damage, as normal.
Incompatible with symbiotes bioware.
Reduced Effect: calculate revised base healing time as above, then increase by ×1.5.

*Detection: free-floating system — nanoscanner TN 6. Active nanites: −1 TN thermographic detection against host. −1 Signature while healing.*

### Nanotattoos
Nanites producing tattoos that change shape, size, color, and texture over time per programming. Price varies by artist, design uniqueness, size, and other factors.

Reduced Effect: tattoo quality and rate of change diminished.

### Nantidotes
Nanites carrying antidotes tailored to combat one specific toxin. Circulate dormant; activate and release antidote at site of poisoning when toxin detected.

Availability: transient or free-floating.
Specificity: one toxin per system; closely related compounds may also be affected at GM discretion.

If present when toxin introduced, or injected before toxin's Speed duration expires: toxin has no effect.
If injected after Speed period: prevents further damage; reduces TN for healing toxin damage by 2.

Reduced Effect: if present before Speed expires: toxin Power halved (round down), Damage Level reduced by one. If applied after damage: further damage reduced as stated; −1 modifier to healing tests.

### Oxy-Rush
Diamondoid oxygen-carrying nanites. Provide enough oxygen and remove enough CO2 for host to exist without breathing for hours. Also counter effects of low blood pressure from damage and shock.

Breath-holding: character can hold breath for hours until nanites wear out.
Athletics bonus: +2 dice to Athletics Tests.
Pain Resistance: equivalent of 1 level (SR3 p. 170).
Incompatible with oxygenated fluorocarbons (M&M p. 113); if both applied, use only strongest effect.
Reduced Effect: character can still hold breath; only +1 die Athletics Tests.

### Retinal Tailors
Nanites that rework retinal pattern to match preprogrammed retinal print.

Rating: 1–10; used in Opposed Test with print scanner (SR3 p. 235).
Re-mapping time: Rating hours; eyes itch during procedure.
Duration: permanent; must apply retinal tailors again to change.
Incompatible with cybereyes and retinal duplication cyberware.
Reduced Effect: if re-mapping not complete, reduce print's rating by half (round down).

### Taggants
Nanite systems designed to mark host for security purposes.

**Etchers (transient):** Inlays magnetic metallic pattern onto bone. Marks convicts or employees permanently. Detectable by MAD systems and cyberware scanners. Scanners can read imprint (SIN, employee ID, etc.). Removal requires specially programmed etcher nanites or surgery.
Detection TN: 3 for MAD system or cyberware scanner.
No reduced effect — operate as normal until gone.

**Markers (free-floating):** Blood-borne information capsules encoded with identifying information. Readable by nanoscanner. Used as discreet message delivery or to tag runners for later identification.
Data capacity: 5 Mp.
No reduced effect — operate as normal until gone.

## NANOGEAR

### Monowire
Superfine monofilament wire constructed via nanotech buckytubes. Nearly invisible. Used for physical security or as lethal garrote (coiled in wrist-worn container with pull tab).

Physical security rules: SR3 p. 234.
Concealability: 8 (when extended and taut).

### Nanoscanner
Hand-held device. Tests blood, saliva, tissue, or other materials for nanoware — especially taggant nanites. Detected nanites can be analyzed for carried data and general construction and purpose.

Rating: 1–10; determines dice rolled to detect nanites in a sample.
Detection rules: Nanoware Detection Table above.

**Scope clarification:** Detects blood-borne nanites and their containers. Does not detect: cyberware supporting nanoware (Nanite Hive, Facilitator, Repair Unit — caught by cyberscanner). Intent is blood-borne detection only.

### Savior Advanced Medkit
Designed by Shiawase Biotech. Backpack or satchel unit. Injects diagnostic and repair nanites along with stimulants, coagulants, and painkillers. Nanites rejoin damaged tissue, stop blood loss, minimize shock.

Rating: 6 medkit; follows all standard medkit rules.
Bonus: −1 TN modifier to Biotech (First Aid) and Body Tests to stabilize.
Nanite supply: limited; roll 1D6 after each treatment — exhausted on 1–2.

### Smart Corrosives
Swarm of nanites in clear chemical solution. Each nanite carries corrosive payload; programmed to seek specific target substance, bind to it, apply corrosive only to that substance. Everything around target unaffected.

Application methods: squirtguns, spray canisters, splash grenades, and other Chemtech Application Gear (M&M p. 115).
Specificity: each solution programmed for one specific substance (skin, metal, plasteel, etc.).
Cost: does not include cost of corrosive carried.

## GEAR — MEDICAL (SR3 Core)

*External gear relevant to nano/bio/cyberware interactions. Rules noted for cross-reference.*

### Biomonitor (External)
Small compact device worn over wrist, upper arm, or heart. Measures heart rate, blood pressure, temperature, etc. Analyzes blood, sweat, and skin samples. Built-in transmitter; transmitting model Rating 2. Secondary model can hook up to vidlink or other transmitter.

*Distinct from: implanted Biomonitor System (M&M bodyware) and Nano-Biomonitor systems (nanotech).*

### Medkit
Rating determines expert system capability (Biotech 3 base). Diagnoses problems from user-supplied information; requests more if diagnosis unclear; advises on techniques. May concoct specific antidote (Biotech Skill Test vs toxin rating) to cancel toxin effects. Supplies exhausted on 1D6 roll of 1 following treatment.

### Stabilization Unit
Enclosed capsule; standard DocWagon vehicle equipment. Automatically stabilizes any person placed inside. −2 TN modifier for all healing tests made to recover from wounds.

*Stack with Guardian Angel: GA modifies First Aid/stabilization tests; stabilization unit modifies healing recovery tests. These are distinct — they stack.*

### Slap Patches
Adhesive drug-dispensers releasing measured doses. Applied directly against skin. Dermal armor reduces effectiveness (reduce Patch Rating by Impact Armor Rating). Blood filtration cyberware implants (kidney replacement, oral filters) make all but trauma patch ineffective — bioware equivalents do not block by RAW.

**Antidote Patch:** If applied before Damage Resistance Test: reduces toxin Power by patch rating. If exposure continues: continues to modify Power. Deadly physical damage + patch: add patch rating dice to stabilization tests. Multiple patches: no additional effect.

**Stimulant Patch:** Non-addictive stimulant. Reduce Stun Damage on Condition Monitor by patch rating boxes. Magicians: make Magic Test vs TN equal to patch rating; failure = possible Essence loss procedure as if Deadly wound taken. Effects wear off in 2D6 minutes; removed damage +1 box is restored.

**Tranq Patch:** Opposed Test: patient's Body vs patch rating. Each net success by patch: fills one box on Stun damage track.

**Trauma Patch:** Must be placed over victim's heart. Allows second chance against death. See Damage and Healing p. 125.

*House rule: attack-vector patches (tranq) receive Nanosymbiote and Toxin Extractor Power reduction bonuses when used as attacks against 3PR characters.*
