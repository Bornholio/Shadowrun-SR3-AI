# Shadowrun 3rd Edition Spellcasting Rules

## Spellcasting Overview

The **Spellcasting** specialization of **Sorcery** focuses on direct, immediate effects that can be cast in a single **Complex Action**. All spellcasting resolution occurs within **Step 3B (Resolve Actions)** of the Combat Turn Sequence.

Spellcasting is resolved in this order:

1. Preparation
2. Spell Targeting
3. Sorcery Test
4. Spell Resistance Test
5. Spell Effect
6. Drain Resistance Test

---

## 1. Preparation

Choose:

- the spell or spells to be cast
- the **Force** of each spell
- how many **Spell Pool** dice will be used
- whether the character is sustaining or dropping any sustained spells
- the number of **Sorcery** dice allocated to spellcasting
- the radius of effect for area spells

Casting a spell requires a **Complex Action**.

### Casting Multiple Spells

A spellcaster may split Sorcery and Spell Pool dice among multiple spells and cast them simultaneously with one Complex Action.

- The caster receives a **+2 target number modifier to the Drain Resistance Test for each extra spell**, applied to **all** of the spells cast.
- Each spell gets its own separate Drain Resistance Test.
- Multiple spells are resolved in whatever order the caster chooses.
- A caster may cast multiple copies of the same spell at different targets.
- The maximum number of spells that can be cast in one Complex Action is equal to the caster’s **Sorcery Skill Rating**.
- At least **one Sorcery die** must be allocated to each spell cast.

### Area Spell Radius

The base radius for all area spells is the caster’s **Magic Attribute in meters**.

Area spells affect all valid targets within the radius, including friend, foe, and the caster.

#### Reducing Radius

The caster can reduce the base radius by **1 meter for every 2 dice withheld** from the Sorcery Test.

- Withheld Sorcery dice cannot be used for any other Sorcery Tests.

#### Increasing Radius

The caster can increase the radius by **1 meter for every 1 die withheld** from the Sorcery Test.

- This is more difficult to control than simply letting the energy spread.

---

## 2. Spell Targeting

To cast a spell, the caster must:

- be able to **see the target**
- be present on the **same plane** as the target (**physical** or **astral**)

For most spells, there is no visible physical effect during casting. On the astral plane, the caster’s aura shimmers with spell energy.

### Line of Sight

A physical spellcaster can cast at any physical target they can see **unaided by imaging technology**.

Allowed for line of sight:

- optical lenses
- mirrors
- fiber optics
- cybernetic vision enhancements
- natural metahuman vision abilities

Not allowed:

- digital imaging equipment
- simsense
- spells or effects that alter vision, such as **Clairvoyance**

Anything that modifies the original image before it reaches the caster prevents spell targeting.

### Cover and Visibility

- Concealed targets gain **cover modifiers**, increasing spellcasting difficulty.
- The gamemaster may require a **Perception Test** if line of sight is uncertain.
- Transparent obstructions such as glass do **not** affect most spells.
- Opaque barriers block line of sight.
- Semi-transparent obstructions apply visual modifiers.

### Area Spells

An area spell affects all **valid targets** within its radius.

- Roll the Sorcery Test once.
- Compare the results against the target number of each valid target in the area.
- Count successes separately for each target.
- Resolve a separate Resistance Test for each target.

Targets that are not valid targets are unaffected.

Example: someone completely concealed behind a wall within the radius of a **Powerball** would not be affected if the caster cannot see them, even if the wall is destroyed.

### Elemental Manipulation Spells

Elemental manipulation spells work differently.

They create a damaging physical medium such as:

- fire
- acid
- lightning

These spells are treated as ranged attacks.

Because they create actual matter or energy:

- they are impeded by physical obstructions like glass or barriers
- they interact with barriers using the normal rules for firing through barriers
- they can still be blocked by effects that defend against spells, such as **Spell Defense** and **astral barriers**

#### Area Elemental Spells

Targets with complete visual cover can still be affected by area elemental spells.

Example: a target behind a wall within the radius of a **Fireball** can still be hit, even if the caster cannot see them.

### Astral Spellcasting

#### Astrally Projecting Casters

An astrally projecting character can cast a spell at any astral form they can see, including:

- other astral projectors
- astrally perceiving characters
- spirits
- dual beings
- active foci

Astral targets, including dual beings, can **only** be affected by **mana spells**.

Drain from spells cast while astrally projecting always causes **physical damage**, regardless of Force.

#### Astrally Perceiving Casters

An astrally perceiving spellcaster can cast at:

- anything physical or astral they can see with normal vision
- anything physical or astral they can see with astral perception

This gives them broad targeting options, but leaves them vulnerable from both planes.

### The Physical/Astral Barrier

The barrier between the physical and astral planes is like an unbreakable pane of one-way glass.

- A caster on the physical side cannot see or affect the astral side.
- A caster on the astral side can see physical things, but can only affect valid targets according to normal astral spellcasting rules.
- A dual being exists on both sides simultaneously and can attack or be attacked from either side.

## 3. Sorcery Test

To cast a spell, roll:

- allocated **Sorcery** dice
- plus **Spell Pool** dice, if desired

Spell Pool dice added to the Sorcery Test cannot exceed the number of Sorcery dice allocated.

### Failure

- If there are **no successes**, the spell fails and has no effect.
- If the roll is **all ones** (Rule of One), the spell fails and the target number for the Drain Resistance Test increases by **+2**.

Record the number of successes from the Sorcery Test.

### Detection Spells

For detection spells, the **gamemaster** rolls the Sorcery Test in secret.

- On failure, the GM may say the caster learns nothing or receives unclear impressions.
- On all ones, the GM gives false or misleading information.

### Target Numbers

Base target number depends on the spell and target.

#### Living Targets

- **Mana spells** usually use the target’s **Willpower**
- **Physical spells** usually use the target’s **Body**

#### Inanimate Targets

The target number depends on the material’s **Object Resistance**.

The more advanced or processed an object is, the harder it is to affect with magic.

The spell’s **Force** must be at least equal to **half the Object Resistance**, rounded down, in order to affect the object.

For vehicles:

- add **Body**
- add **half armor**
- then use that adjusted value before applying the Force requirement

Other spells may have specific target numbers in their own descriptions.

### Modifiers to Sorcery Test Target Number

The target number increases for:

- cover
- visibility penalties
- injury modifiers
- sustaining other spells
- astral barriers crossed by the spell

#### Sustaining Modifier

If the caster is sustaining spells, add **+2 target number per sustained spell**.

#### Astral Barriers

An astral barrier adds its **Force** to the target number of spells cast across its boundary, unless the caster created the barrier.

### Touch Range Spells

Touch-range spells are not affected by cover or visibility modifiers because the caster must touch the target.

However, the **Unarmed Combat Test** to touch the target uses normal melee modifiers.

### Elemental Manipulation Sorcery Test

Elemental manipulation spells are treated as ranged attacks using **Sorcery** as the Ranged Combat Skill.

- Base target number is **4**
- Range does not modify this target number, as long as the caster can see the target
- Cover, visibility, injury, and sustaining modifiers still apply
- These spells can be **dodged**
- Spell Pool may be used normally

---

## 4. Spell Resistance Test

Living targets may always make a Spell Resistance Test unless they are willing targets.

The target rolls the relevant attribute, usually:

- **Body**
- **Intelligence**
- **Willpower**

The target number is the spell’s **Force**.

No target modifiers apply unless specifically noted.

Non-living, non-magical targets do not get a Spell Resistance Test.

### Elemental Manipulation Exception

For elemental spells, the resistance roll is a **Damage Resistance Test** under ranged combat rules.

- **Combat Pool** may be used

---

## 5. Spell Effect

### Against Non-Resisting Targets

If the target makes no Spell Resistance Test, all Sorcery Test successes are applied according to the spell description.

For non-resisting targets, **one success always ensures some degree of effect**.

### Against Resisting Targets

If there is a Spell Resistance Test:

- compare the caster’s successes to the target’s successes
- if the target gets equal or more successes, the spell has no effect
- if the caster gets more successes, the spell affects the target

The spell’s effect is based on the **difference in net successes**.

For damaging spells:

- stage up the Damage Level by **one level for every 2 net successes**

### Elemental Manipulation Effect

Unless completely dodged, elemental spells strike the target.

- Damage Level is staged up by **one level for every 2 successes** on the Ranged Combat Test
- the target stages damage down with a Damage Resistance Test
- even if damage is staged down to nothing, the spell’s secondary effect may still cause harm

---

## 6. Drain Resistance Test

Immediately after casting the spell, during the same Complex Action, the caster resists Drain.

Roll:

- **Willpower**
- plus any **Spell Pool** dice allocated to Drain Resistance

### Drain Target Number

Drain target number is:

**Force ÷ 2 (rounded down) + Drain Modifier**

No target modifiers apply unless specifically stated.

### Reducing Drain

- Every **2 successes** reduces the Drain Level by **one level**
- If Drain is reduced below **Light**, the caster takes no Drain damage

### Stun vs Physical Drain

- If the spell’s Force is greater than the caster’s **Magic Attribute**, Drain is **physical**
- All spells cast while **astrally projecting** cause **physical Drain**
- Otherwise, Drain is **stun**

# Spell Defense

Sorcery can be used to defend against incoming spells.

To use Spell Defense, allocate:

- **Sorcery** dice
- plus any desired **Spell Pool** dice

### Limits

Protected subjects must:

- be on the **same plane** as the defending magician
- be within **Magic × 100 meters**

The magician can protect a maximum number of subjects equal to their **Sorcery Skill Rating**.

Most magicians include themselves among protected subjects.

### Using Spell Defense

When a protected subject is targeted by a spell:

- the defending magician automatically senses it
- the magician may roll allocated Spell Defense dice against a target number equal to the spell’s **Force**
- each success subtracts directly from the caster’s Sorcery Test successes
- if the caster’s successes are reduced to **0 or less**, the spell fails

The defending player may choose whether or not to use Spell Defense against an incoming spell.

### Action and Refresh

- Allocating Spell Defense dice is a **Free Action**
- Once used, Spell Defense dice are gone until the magician’s **next Combat Turn**
- Spell Pool dice used for Spell Defense also do not refresh until the next Combat Turn

# Dispelling

A magician may use Sorcery to cancel an existing sustained spell.

Dispelling requires a **Complex Action**.

The magician must:

- be able to **see the target of the spell**
- be on the **same plane**

### Dispelling Test

Roll:

- **Sorcery**
- plus any allocated **Spell Pool**

Target number is the spell’s **Force**.

Each success permanently reduces the sustained spell’s original casting successes by **1**.

- When the spell’s successes reach **0**, the spell ends
- If the spell has a **Threshold**, reducing successes below that Threshold also ends the spell

### Drain from Dispelling

After each Dispelling Test, the magician must make a **Drain Resistance Test** as if they had cast the spell being dispelled.

- If the spell’s Force exceeds the dispeller’s Magic Attribute, Drain is **physical**
- Otherwise, Drain is **stun**

Because of this risk, it is wise to assense a spell and determine its Force before attempting to dispel it.

### Cooperative Dispelling

Multiple magicians may cooperate to dispel a single spell.

- each magician’s successes reduce the spell’s successes
- each magician resists Drain separately

# Quick GM Notes

## Spellcasting Sequence

1. Choose spell, Force, dice, sustaining choices, and radius
2. Confirm valid targeting and line of sight
3. Roll Sorcery Test
4. Roll Spell Resistance Test if applicable
5. Apply spell effect
6. Roll Drain Resistance Test

## Core Reminders

- Spell Pool added to Sorcery Test cannot exceed Sorcery dice allocated
- Sustaining spells gives **+2 TN per sustained spell**
- Casting multiple spells adds **+2 Drain TN per extra spell** to all those spells
- Area spell base radius is **Magic in meters**
- Mana vs living target usually uses **Willpower**
- Physical vs living target usually uses **Body**
- Drain is physical if **Force > Magic**
- Astral projection spellcasting always causes **physical Drain**
- Spell Defense subtracts successes from the incoming spell before resistance
