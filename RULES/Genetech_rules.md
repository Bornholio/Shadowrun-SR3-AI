# Genetech Rules

## General Rules

- Genetech modifications are considered **natural**, not augmented.
- A character may undergo **more than one genetic procedure at a time**. Cost and Bio Index costs are cumulative, but total treatment time is only the **longest single treatment duration** involved.
- Many genetech applications have a **Bio Index** cost.

## Genetic Modification Before Birth

- Genetic modifications performed **in utero** have:
  - **Half normal duration**
  - **75% of normal cost**
- This reduction **does not apply** to genengineered substances.
- At the gamemaster's discretion, starting characters may receive this discount if their background includes genetic manipulation before birth.

## Ritual Sample Effects

- Genetic modification changes a character's DNA, aura, and magical signature.
- Previously taken ritual samples become less effective.
- **Linking Test penalty:** **+1 target number per 0.2 Bio Index** gained through genetic engineering, to a **maximum of +4**.

## Genetech and Cyberware

- If genetech conflicts with cyberware or bioware, **genetech takes precedence**.
- Additional specific incompatibilities are listed in the compatibility table below.

## Gene Therapy

### Core Rules

- Whole-body gene therapy generally requires at least:
  - **2 months** and **50,000¥** for major whole-body changes
  - **1 month** and **30,000¥** for whole-body therapy that only restores the body's existing genetic pattern
- Bio Index impact depends on the scale of the change.

### Augmented Healing

- Heals **all remaining damage boxes**, even if magical healing was already applied.
- **Healing time is reduced by half**.
- Can restore **damaged organs** and **regrow severed limbs**.
- May be used on characters with cyberware/bioware via tailored nanites:
  - **Cost increases by 25%**
  - **Treatment time unchanged**
  - Cannot repair damaged cyberware or bioware

### Cellular Repair

- Repairs otherwise irreparable **somatic damage**.
- Can heal:
  - Permanent neurotoxin damage
  - Physical Flaws
  - Attribute loss due to disease or severe physical trauma
- Can also **reverse the effects of boosted reflexes cyberware**.

### Age Rejuvenation

- Requires:
  - **1 major therapy session**
  - **Maintenance sessions every 6 months for 3 years**
- Restores patient to a physical age of about **21**.
- Base treatment time is **1 month** for patients age **40 or below**.
- For every decade above 40, **add 2 weeks** to treatment time.
- Can only be performed a limited number of times before bodily systems fail.

### Phenotypic Alteration

- Can make changes from superficial cosmetic alterations to major physical changes.
- Any alteration not natural to the patient's metatype counts as a **major change** for treatment time and cost.
- Can increase the character's **Racial Modified Limit** for **Body, Strength, and/or Quickness by 1**.
- Rules for attribute-limit increases:
  - Each attribute requires a **separate treatment**
  - Each attribute can only be altered **once**
  - These always count as **major changes**
  - This also changes that attribute's **Attribute Maximum** to **Racial Modified Limit × 1.5 (round up)**
  - It does **not** increase the current attribute score, only the limit/maximum

## Environmental Microadaptation

### Cold Adaptation

- Gain **+2 dice** for resisting **cold-related damage**.
- Gain **–2 modifier** for **cold-environment Survival Tests**.

### Heat Adaptation

- Gain **+2 dice** for resisting **heat damage** — applies to direct fire-based attacks (flamethrowers, fire spells, contact heat sources such as Flame Aura) and ambient heat sources (burning buildings, hot environments).
- Does **not** apply to **ongoing burning damage** — the secondary escalating damage state when a character or their clothing/gear catches fire (6M base, +2 Power per Combat Turn). Burning is a secondary effect, not a direct heat attack.
- Gain **–2 modifier** for **hot-environment Survival Tests**.

### Low-Oxygen Adaptation

- When using holding-breath rules, each success on the Athletics Test increases duration by **10 Combat Turns** instead of **3**.
- At normal oxygen levels, the subject suffers **hyperoxia**; treat as **oxygen toxicity**.

### Pollution Tolerance Adaptation

- Gain **+3 Body dice** to resist chemical attacks from **pollutants, heavy metals, and related toxins**.

## Direct Protein Synthesis

### Core Rules

- Minimal-treatment versions take only long enough to rewrite DNA.
- Enhancement reaches full effectiveness about **1 month after treatment is complete**.
- Enhancements with significant physical effects may require longer treatment to express those changes.

### Angiotensin

- After a **Deadly physical wound**, additional damage from blood loss and shock accrues at **1 box every (Body × 2) Combat Turns**.

### Calcitonin

- Gain **+1 Body** for **Damage Resistance Tests**.
- Bones do not break under normal circumstances.
- Bones have **Barrier Rating 6**.
- **[HR]** Unarmed melee damage increases to **(STR+2)M** — house rule synchronized with plastic bone lacing cyberware effect, applied without the associated unnatural weight penalty of physical lacing.

### Erythropoietin

- Gain **+2 dice** for **all Athletics Tests**.

### Glucagon

- **Double all listed times** when dealing with the effects of **fatigue**.

## DNA Masking

- A DNA scanner cannot automatically produce a positive identification.
- Resolve with an **Opposed Test** between:
  - **DNA masking rating**
  - **DNA scanner rating**
- If the masking and scanner achieve **equal successes**, the scanner reports that further identification by other means is required.

## DNA Scanners

- DNA scan results are enough to **hold a suspect for further investigation**, but are **not legally admissible** as forensic evidence.
- Scan time against a character with DNA masking is **a number of minutes equal to the scanner's rating**.
- If the scanner wins the Opposed Test, scan time may be **reduced by excess successes**.
- If the target has **no unregistered DNA alterations**, results take **1 minute**.

## Immunization

- Treatment is immediate, but full immunity develops after **1 month**.
- Grants **complete resistance to normal doses** of the specific compound.
- Against excessive or abnormal exposure:
  - immunity may fail,
  - but the **Power** of the disease, toxin, or pathogen is **halved (round down)**.
- At gamemaster discretion, similar compounds may also be affected, possibly at half Power.
- A patient may only be immunized against a number of compounds equal to **Body** (excluding race, cyberware, or bioware modifiers).
- Each immunization is **permanent**.
- Genetic immunization shots do **not** work against:
  - **neurotoxins**
  - **fast-acting compounds** with **Speed: Immediate**
- Those require **cellular repair genetic therapy** instead.

## Genetech Compatibility

| Genetech | Cyberware and Bioware Conflicts |
|---|---|
| Augmented healing | Increased cost **(+25%)** if character has any cyberware or bioware |
| Cellular repair | Incompatible with **boosted reflexes** |
| Age rejuvenation | Increased cost **(+25%)** if character has cyberware or bioware |
| Phenotypic alteration | Specific changes may be incompatible with organ replacements such as **cyberlimbs, cybereyes, orthoskin, etc.** |
| Cold microadaptation | Incompatible with **boosted reflexes, metabolic arrestor, heat microadaptation** |
| Heat microadaptation | Incompatible with **boosted reflexes, dermal sheathing or plating, orthoskin, suprathyroid gland, cold microadaptation** |
| Angiotensin synthesis | Incompatible with **muscle replacement, metabolic arrestor**; effects negated if character has **more than two cyberlimbs** |
| Calcitonin synthesis | Incompatible with **muscle replacement, flex hands, platelet factory**; effects negated if character has **more than two cyberlimbs** |
| Erythropoietin synthesis | Incompatible with **muscle replacement** |
| Glucagon synthesis | Incompatible with **suprathyroid gland** |
