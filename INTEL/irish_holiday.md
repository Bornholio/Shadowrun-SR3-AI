# Irish Holiday Calendar

| Date | Name | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 February | Imbolc | Beginning of spring; associated with lambing, milk, purification, and the return of light. |
| 1 May | Bealtaine | Beginning of summer; protective fires, cattle rites, fertility, and boundary customs. |
| 24 June | Midsummer / Saint John’s Eve | Bonfires and seasonal fire customs; important in folk practice, though later tied to a saint’s feast. |
| 1 August | Lughnasa | Beginning of harvest; assemblies, hill gatherings, games, matchmaking, and first-fruits customs. |
| 29 September | Michaelmas | Important quarter day in pre-modern rural life; hiring, rents, geese, and harvest completion. |
| 31 October / 1 November | Samhain | Beginning of winter; feasting, divination, protection rites, and the boundary between living and dead. |
| 11 November | Martinmas | Seasonal slaughtering, geese, and household provisioning in some regions. |
