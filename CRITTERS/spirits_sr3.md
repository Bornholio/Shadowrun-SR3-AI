## Spirit Rules

### Forms

Spirits exist in two forms. Changing between them is a **Simple Action**.

**Astral Form**
- Exists entirely on the astral plane
- All astral Attributes = Force
- Can only perform services affecting the astral plane or directly affecting their summoner through the magical link
- Can communicate with summoner; visible to astral space occupants
- Cannot be harmed by physical weapons; only mana spells affect them
- Weapon foci can harm astral spirits but wielder must be present on the astral plane
- Can manifest in the same manner as projecting magicians (SR3 core p. 173)

**Physical Form**
- Assumed via Materialization power; required to use powers on non-astral targets
- Functions as a dual-natured being — interacts with both planes simultaneously
- Spirits dislike physical form; it makes them vulnerable to physical attack
- Physical Attributes determined by spirit type (see individual entries below)
- Has **Immunity to Normal Weapons** in physical form: Armor = twice Force against all attacks
- **Melee contest of wills:** Any character in melee with a physical spirit may use Willpower instead of Combat (Combat Pool dice may not be used). A weapon may be used as a symbol; mundane weapons add no damage but Reach bonus applies. Success = (Charisma)M Stun to the spirit. Spirit resists with Force only; no Combat Pool, no armor protection against this attack type.

### Initiative

| Form | Base Reaction | Initiative bonus | Init dice |
|---|---|---|---|
| Astral | Force | +20 | 1D6 |
| Physical | As listed per type | +10 (materialized) | 1D6 |

If a spirit in astral form changes to physical form during a Combat Turn, it is ineligible for another action for two Initiative Passes. Spirits switching astral→physical receive no Initiative modifier unless in that form at the start of the next Combat Turn.

Spirits can only receive new commands on their summoner's or master's actions. They will Delay to wait for commands if they have none.

### Elemental Opposition

Elementals are Vulnerable to attacks from opposing elementals, including in astral combat — increases Damage Level by one.

| Opposed Pair |
|---|
| Fire ↔ Water |
| Earth ↔ Air |

Nature spirits may oppose each other within a domain; conflicts of opposed powers are resolved using standard opposed test rules.

### Domains

Nature spirits can only exist in their home domain. They cannot be summoned elsewhere and will not leave it. A sea spirit cannot move onto land; a prairie spirit will not enter a forest.

### Stat Format

All spirit stats are expressed as functions of **F** (Force Rating, set at time of summoning):

`B Q S C I W E R`

- **E** always = `(F)A` — primarily astral; dual natured when manifested via Materialization
- **Initiative:** two values given — Astral (F + bonus + 1D6) and Physical (F + bonus + 1D6)
- Materialized spirits receive +10 Initiative; astral spirits receive +20 Initiative

## Spirit Dice Pools

**Combat Pool:** (Q + I + W) ÷ 2, rounded down. All values at Force = F:
- Spirits of Man / Fire Elemental / Sky Spirits: Q=(F+2), I=F, W=F → Pool = (F+2+F+F)÷2 = **(3F+2)÷2**
- Water Elementals / Water Spirits: Q=F, I=F, W=F → Pool = **3F÷2**
- Earth Elementals / Land Spirits: Q=(F−2), I=F, W=F → Pool = **(3F−2)÷2**
- Air Elementals / Sky Spirits: Q=(F+3), I=F, W=F → Pool = **(3F+3)÷2**

**Astral Combat Pool (projecting):** Force × 1.5, rounded down.

**No other pools apply to use of powers** (SR3 core, p. 262).

## MitS Additional Powers

*Source: Magic in the Shadows (MitS). Supplement to SR3 core power list in critters_powers_sr3.md.*

---

### DIVINATION
*Type: M · Action: Exclusive Complex · Range: Self · Duration: Instant*

Spirit makes a limited guess at the next actions of entities within its domain, similar to the Divining metamagic (MitS p. 69). Uses Force for the Divination Test. Successes determine how much information the spirit can reveal. Spirit may answer in up to twice as many words as its Force. GM makes the answer as specific or vague as the story requires.

---

### POSSESSION
*Type: M · Action: Complex · Range: LOS · Duration: Sustained*

Allows the spirit to possess a living being, similar to the Possessing metamagic (MitS p. 69), with these differences:

- Spirit can also inhabit a **willing host**, but the host must be astrally active (exception: loa summoners and serviteurs, who may be possessed through the magical link they have established with the loa)
- The host invites the spirit to enter; spirit decides when to leave unless exorcised
- Spirit has access to its own knowledge/skills and the host's knowledge/skills
- All host **physical** Attributes increased by spirit's Force
- All host **mental** Attributes replaced by spirit's
- Unwilling host: +2 to all spirit's target numbers while possessing
- Host killed or unconscious: spirit driven back to astral form, unharmed
- Banishing the possessing spirit disrupts it rather than killing it
- Mana spells and critter powers affect the spirit; physical spells and critter powers affect the host body only

**Cannot use while possessing:** Corrosive Secretions, Engulf, Materialization. All other powers available.

---

### STORM
*Type: P · Action: Exclusive · Range: LOS · Duration: Sustained*

Spirit causes a storm anywhere in its domain. Maximum area radius = Force × 100 meters. Storms may feature howling winds, lightning, rain, twisters, or domain-appropriate phenomena (tidal waves, earthquakes, avalanches, flash floods). Storm spirits can create a storm regardless of current domain conditions.

**Storm Strike:** Summoner may call on a bound spirit to make a storm strike — a devastating attack using raw storm fury (lightning bolts, hail rocks, falling trees, smashing waves, twisters, tumbling boulders, etc.). Everyone in the storm area is subject to storm strike damage unless protected by Guard of a spirit of equal or greater Force (including the striking spirit). A domain spirit can also guard targets in its domain.

- Damage: **(Force)S** — armor offers no protection; Combat Pool dice may be used to resist
- Against vehicles: **(½ Force)M** only
- Storm strike: **Complex Action**, once per Combat Turn
- Each storm strike = separate service
- Summoner resists **Drain** as though conjuring the spirit's great form each time they call a storm strike (no second Drain Test for the great form itself)
- Uncontrolled spirit using Storm: resists **(Force)D Stun** per storm strike thrown

---

### SEARCH (Expanded Version)
*Type: P · Action: Exclusive Complex · Range: LOS · Duration: Special · Source: MitS*

Optional expanded resolution for the basic Search power (SR3 core p. 265). GM chooses basic or expanded per story needs.

**Summoner requirements:** Must clearly envision the subject. Spirit reads the image from the summoner's mind through the astral link. Commands must be specific enough to give the spirit a generic image (a uniform, a building type, a face). Nature spirits have difficulty with electronic data and high technology.

**Search area:** Force × 10,000 square meters (base area). Each doubling of the search area: +2 TN.

**Test:** Spirit rolls dice = twice its Force.
- Living being: base TN 4
- Non-living object: Object Resistance Table (SR3 core p. 182)
- Target concealed or in barrier: add concealer's Essence or barrier's Force to TN
- Target not in spirit's domain: successful search reveals that fact; unsuccessful = cannot determine whether target is present

**Time:** 20 minutes × number of base areas searched. Great form spirits: 10 minutes × base areas. Spirit spends full base time even if search comes up empty.

## Watchers
*Source: MitS*

Watchers exist solely on the astral plane. They can never leave the astral plane — cannot materialize in the physical world or ascend to a metaplane.

**Perception:** Can see and hear the physical plane; can manifest (become visible and audible to living beings) per SR3 core p. 173. Like apparitions, they cannot touch material things or affect the physical plane directly.

**Attributes:** All Attribute Ratings = Force.

**Intelligence:** Single-minded and clever at assigned tasks; everything else tends to go over their heads. Comparable to a well-trained, loyal dog. Takes commands extremely literally (never tell a watcher to search *every* corner of a building).

**Limitations:**
- Rarely manage to overcome unforeseen difficulties (astral barrier in path = remain there until time runs out and dissolve)
- Bound tightly to summoner; another magician cannot take control
- Can be banished normally; can be engaged in astral combat

**Astral combat:** Inflict **(Force)L Stun** damage. Cannot inflict Physical damage — cannot affect astral forms only affected by Physical damage (barriers, foci).

## MitS Spirits of the Elements

*Source: MitS. Shamanic tradition equivalents to hermetic elementals. Distinct from SR3 core elementals.*

**Domain rule:** Cannot leave their domain except as great form spirits. Domain boundary: radius of Force × 10 meters from summoning point.

**Domain requirement:** Must be natural in origin. Manmade sources of raw power do not qualify. Example: salamander domain = bonfire, volcano, hot roadway; NOT a nuclear reactor or smelting facility.

**Metaplanes:** Gnomes, Salamanders, Sylphs, and Undines have metaplanes similar to their elemental counterparts but subtly different. Manitous have the Metaplane of Wood — no elemental parallel; a place of living forests where each plant is conscious.

**Conflict with toxic spirits:** Spirits of the elements hate toxic spirits and will attack them on sight regardless of summoner's commands.

---

*Source: MitS. Conjured via the Invoking metamagical technique (MitS p. 75).*

### Physical Bonus

When a great form spirit is conjured, the summoner may increase the spirit's materialized physical form. The summoner receives a number of bonus points equal to their initiate grade ÷ 2 (rounded down) to distribute using the table below.

| Bonus | Cost (points) |
|---|---|
| Armor | 1 per point |
| Body | 1 per point |
| Reach +1 | 2 |

*Example: Grade 8 initiate conjures a Force 6 great form fire elemental. Gets 4 points (8÷2). Takes +1 Reach (2pts) and +2 Body (2pts).*

---

### Great Elementals
*Source: MitS*

Great elementals appear as larger versions of their normal form (SR3 core p. 266). Capabilities beyond normal elementals:

- May use powers on a number of targets **simultaneously equal to Force**
- **Area engulf:** affects all targets within radius = Force in meters; may choose not to attack friendly targets within the area
- **Elemental special effects** (physical form only, within LOS):

| Type | Special Effect |
|---|---|
| Fire | Ignite flammable materials |
| Water | Burst water mains and pipes, cause plumbing to flood, direct masses of water like a fire hose |
| Air | Generate fierce winds — makes affected area Difficult Ground (SR3 core p. 112) |
| Earth | Set up minor earth tremors (Richter 4–5) — damage to fragile structures/items, create Difficult Ground conditions |

---

### Great Nature Spirits
*Source: MitS*

Unlike ordinary nature spirits, great nature spirits can **cross domain lines** (SR3 core p. 184).

Additional capabilities:
- Great nature spirits of the **land, sea, and sky** gain the **Storm** power (MitS p. 99)
- Great nature spirits of **man** gain the **Divination** power (MitS p. 99) for questions related to their domain
- May use powers simultaneously on a number of targets **equal to Force**

**Binding rules:** Great form nature spirits do not count toward the shaman's limit of one spirit per domain. Because they can cross domain lines, shamans may only bind at one time a number of spirits less than or equal to their Charisma.

---

### Great Spirits of the Elements
*Source: MitS*

Great form spirits of the elements can cross domain lines in the same manner as great nature spirits.

Additional capabilities:
- Count as **one spirit** toward the magician's limit of bound spirits equal to Charisma
- Gain the **Storm** power (MitS p. 99)
- Gain the **Cleansing** power — treat as the Cleansing metamagical technique (MitS p. 74), using the spirit's **Essence in place of Sorcery**

---

### Great Loa
*Source: MitS. See Loa Spirits section (pending) for base loa rules.*

On the astral, great loas appear as brighter, more potent versions of their normal forms.

Additional capabilities beyond normal loa:
- Can **possess other astrally active characters and beings**, in addition to houngans and serviteurs
- When possessing, the host takes on the physical visage of the loa — similar to the shamanic mask (SR3 core p. 163) but more pronounced, covering the host's entire body
- Great loa can possess a host for **as long as they want** (normal loa are more limited)
- Gain **Immunity to Normal Weapons** while possessing a body
- Each great loa also gains **one additional power** depending on its type — see Loa Spirit Powers (MitS p. 102); *to be added when Loa Spirits section is complete*

---

### Great Blood Spirits
*Source: MitS. See Blood Spirits (MitS p. 134) — to be added.*

Great form blood spirits appear as larger, more potent (and more disgusting) versions of their normal form. Not limited to staying within range of sentient beings, though they usually do. Can use powers simultaneously on a number of targets equal to Force. Gain the **Engulf** power, allowing the spirit to drown victims in blood — treat as a water engulf (SR3 core p. 263).
